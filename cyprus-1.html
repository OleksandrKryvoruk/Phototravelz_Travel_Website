Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Cyprus.

EUROPE > South Europe
1.Nicosia
Nicosia, or Lefkosia - the capital of the Republic of Cyprus, in the central part of the island of Cyprus. In general, Nicosia - the last divided capital in the world. The northern part of the city is still under Turkish occupation. Museum: History and Archives; Cyprus Museum; Folk Art Museum; Lapidarium. Church: Gothic cathedral of St. Sophia (13-14 cc.); Dame Cathedral and the Augustinian (both around 1330), the Cathedral of St. John (rebuilt in 1655), a cycle of paintings - about 1730. Important monuments: the Archaeological Museum, the Folk Art Museum of Cyprus, Venetian Walls, Gardens at the monastery of St. Procopius.

2.Ayia Napa
Ayia Napa is a resort town, famous for its nightlife and beautiful beaches in Cyprus, located in its eastern part, 8 kilometers from Protaras. The coastal part of Ayia Napa attracts many tourists, thanks to its golden sandy beaches and the purest blue waters. Unlike the rest of the island of Cyprus, only in the area of ​​Ayia Napa there are so beautiful beaches with Caribbean sand. There are such kinds of water sports as water skiing, windsurfing, diving. All the beaches of Ayia Napa are awarded with the blue flag of the EU for their level of cleanliness, and the development of infrastructure that meets the standards of the European Union.

3.Cape Greco
Cape Greco - a cape and a small peninsula on the south - west Cyprus, in Famagusta, between Ayia - Napa and Paralimni. The area has a national forest park. In the waters of Cape Greco has a submarine canyons and caves, and it is popular for diving. This is an important station for migratory birds and there live 71 species of fauna and flora. In the waters of Cape Greco found several shipwrecks, some of more than 2 000 years, and the cape has a lighthouse.

4.Nissi Beach
Nissi Beach is a beach in Cyprus. The beach is about three kilometers from the center of Ayia Napa in the east of the island. The name comes from the Greek Nissi, which means "small island".

5.Fig Tree Bay
Fig Tree Bay - sandy beach at the resort of Protaras, Cyprus. The third best beach in Europe. Suitable for water skiing, windsurfing and parasailing. The water is clean enough, and fig trees grow along the beach.

6.Coral Bay
By Evgeniy Isaev CC2
Coral Bay is a popular tourist resort in the municipality of Peyia, 6 km north of Paphos. The beach of Coral Bay is certified by the Blue Flag, meaning that the beach meets all standards, rules and regulations established by the relevant committee of the European Union. This fact makes Coral Bay a popular holiday destination for foreign tourists and locals.

7.Paphos
Paphos - the city in the southwest of Cyprus. According to mythology, this is the birthplace of the Greek goddess of love and beauty Aphrodite. Since the days of Ottoman rule in the city preserved Turkish fortress built in 1572. The city has several UNESCO sites: Villa Dionysus, built by the Romans in the 3 century; Villa of Theseus and his mosaics depicting Theseus fighting the Minotaur and the first bath Achilles Roman Theatre 2 centuries; Grotto of St. Solomoni royal tombs; Saint Paul's Pillar. The town consists of two parts: the upper town is represented by historic sites, and the bottom has a lot of beautiful beaches.

9.Troodos Mountains
Troodos Mountains - the highest mountain range in Cyprus with peaks at an altitude of 1951 meters. Troodos is a major tourist attraction and holiday destination. Troodos has a rich flora, which includes many endemic plants. The mountains offer trekking, agro-tourism, picnics, sports, leisure, cycling. In addition, during the winter months in the region Chionistra operates a ski resort.

10.Stavrovouni Monastery
Stavrovouni Monastery - Monastery of the Cyprus Orthodox Church, which was built in the year 327. It is the oldest monastery in Cyprus. Entrance is allowed only to men. Women are allowed to visit the Church of All Saints of Cyprus, located on the square in front of the entrance gate to the monastery.
