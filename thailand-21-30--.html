21.Wat Rong Khun White Temple
Wat Rong Khun White Temple and Temple of the White - unconventional Buddhist temple, located in the province of Chiang Rai, Thailand. Among the tourists, he is better known as the "White Church". The owner of this white temple, as well as its creator is a famous Thai painter - Chalermchai Kositpipat. White Temple combines the perfect blend of traditional Buddhist art with contemporary design solutions. Absolutely white walls and sculptures sparkle, reflecting the hues of dawn and evening events. The walls are decorated with small pieces of mirror glass, which gives the structure of celestial light and magical views. Around the complex visible intricate sculptures of demons, skulls, plaster casts of severed heads on trees and other fanciful objects. White Temple is a symbol of national pride and northern Thailand, which attracts many curious tourists who want to enjoy the fabulous views of the palace.

22.Wat Arun
Wat Arun is a Buddhist temple in Bangkok, the capital of Thailand. It is located on the western bank of the Chauphraya river. Another name is Vata Arun - the Temple of the Morning Dawn, at this time it looks most spectacular. Known for its 79-meter pagoda, decorated with ceramic tiles and multi-colored porcelain, which once served as ballast on junks, come from China. Wat Arun is also known for a very important for all Buddhists ceremony "Kathin", which takes place there annually in November. As part of this event, the country's honorary monks are awarded special traditional clothes "Kathin", which they receive in the temple of Arun from the hands of the king himself or another member of the Royal Family.

23.Prasat Phra Thep Bidon
Prasat Phra Thep Bidon or Wat Phra Kaeo (Temple of the Emerald Buddha) - is considered the most sacred Buddhist temple in Thailand. Located in the heart of Bangkok at the Royal Palace. At the entrance to the temple statues hufiv - terrible demons - the temple guardians. On the territory of the temple there are all the traditional buildings of the temple, stupa, statues and a library. But the main shrine, around which the entire temple complex was built - the figure of the Emerald Buddha. Carved from a single piece of blue-green jade Buddha sitting on a golden throne. The statue of the Emerald Buddha, though small in size (height 75 cm), it is very important to every Buddhist in Thailand and for the whole Thai culture.

24.Elephant Nature Park
Elephant Nature Park is a shelter and center for elephant rescue located in the Mae Taeng area (Chiang Mai Province) in Thailand. It is home to more than 30 domesticated elephants from all over Thailand. The park is located in the valley, next to the river, mountains, which is a good condition for the development of elephants, and also an excellent place for visitors. In addition to preserving and preserving elephants, the park is also a place for visitors to learn about the lives of elephants, tigers and mountain people and enjoy the scenery of the majestic mountains in Northern Thailand.

25.Karon Beach
 By Brunner Hans Rudolf CC2  
Karon Beach is one of the longest beaches in the island of Phuket - its length is about 5 kilometers. The beach is located on the west coast of the island at 45 km from the international airport of Phuket and 20 km from the city of Phuket (Thailand). Karon is the second largest center of the island, after the beach of Patong. The coast of Karon is divided into two beaches. The first is the small Karon (Karon Noi Beach). The second is the big Karon (Karon Beach), which extends to Cape Sai, separating Karon beach from Kata Beach. The beach of Karon is famous for its "singing sand", which creaks because of the large content of quartz in its composition. In the south of the beach you can admire a lot of coral reefs, and in the north - restaurants, cafes, shops.

26.Ko Lipe
By Vyacheslav Argenberg CC2
Ko Lipe is a small Thai island in the Adang-Ravi archipelago, located 70 kilometers south-west of the coast of Thailand, near the border with Malaysia. Administratively belongs to the province of Satun. The southernmost island of the country. Koch Lipa has many dive sites and many dive shops and resorts that organize boat trips. Ko Lipe has three main beaches: Hat Pramong, Hat Chao Ley and Pattaya Beach. Quiet, clean water makes Ko Lipe an ideal place for scuba diving, 25 percent of the world's tropical fish species are in the area.

27.Ko Tao
Ko Tao - is an island in Thailand, part of the archipelago of Krabi on the west coast of the Gulf of Thailand. The economy of the island is almost entirely focused on tourism, especially popular was the diving. Name Koh Tao means "Turtle Island" and comes from the time when the sea around the island was inhabited by turtles. In addition to the main occupation in the Tau - diving - the island is also free diving, rock climbing (bouldering), Thai boxing, Thai massage.

28.Terminal 21
By Sry85 CC3
Terminal 21 is a 177 meter tall skyscraper built in Bangkok, Thailand from 2008 to 2011. It is located on the edge of Sukhumvit Street. There is a hotel with 186 rooms, 19 elevators, as well as a shopping center "Terminal 21" with sales area of ​​55,000 m 2. Terminal 21 is a shopping center that has airport design with their typical signs. On each floor there is another city that is represented through its architecture and typical shops such as Paris, Rome, Tokyo, London, Istanbul or San Francisco. The shopping center has the largest escalators in Thailand up to 36 meters high.

29.Lumphini Park
Lumphini Park is located in Bangkok, Thailand. The park was founded in the twenties of the 20th century, then it was the outskirts of Bangkok. Now, because of the continuous growth of the Thai megapolis, the park has appeared in one of the most popular business and shopping districts of Bangkok. On the territory there are two lakes, sports grounds for playing tennis, fitness, running and cycling paths, numerous zones with shells and exercise equipment, playgrounds, the first public library and a garden - a collection of palm trees. In the palm garden from February to March, musical evenings are played, on which a symphony orchestra plays, and around it there are waltz venues. Lumpini is famous for its lizards and turtles, which live in the lakes and canals of the park.

30.Railay Beach
Railay Beach is a Krabi peninsula in the south of Thailand. It is located in Amphoe Muang Krabi and extends to the south of Ao Nang Bay in the Andaman Sea. This is one of the most impressive tourist destinations in the south of Thailand. From within grow high jungle green limestone formations. They partially restrict the peninsula. Accommodation ranges from cheap and popular bungalows with tourists and climbers, to the famous Rayavadee station for wealthy people. The four main directions of Railay consist of Pranang, Railay West, Railay East and Tonsai, the latter of which is mainly intended for climbers.
