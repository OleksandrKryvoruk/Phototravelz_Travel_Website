1.Copenhagen
Copenhagen - the capital and largest city of Denmark. For 800 years, Copenhagen has gone from a fishing village to the capital. The oldest and most famous theater in the capital Theatre Royal (1748). The theater has a big stage under the name of Old Stage, which can contain about 1,600 spectators. Museums: The National Museum, National Gallery of Art is the largest art museum with large collections, Thorvaldsen Museum, Natural museum presents the Botanical Garden, Geological Museum and the Zoological Museum. Copenhagen also has specialized museums: the Museum of the workers, the Museum of resistance, City Museum. The city has a number of large parks, some reaching more than 60 hectares.

2.Aarhus
Aarhus - the second largest city in Denmark. The city is located in Eastern Jutland near Aarhus Bay with views of the peninsula Mols.Orhus chosen as the European Capital of Culture in 2017. Aarhus Festival is the largest cultural festival in Jutland, with the participation of local, national and international artists. In addition to the large agencies have a lot of small venues, galleries and art workshops, which provide a wide variety of cultural life. Among the champions stand out: the art museum ARoS, Aarhus Theatre, Concert Hall, Aarhus Symphony Orchestra and Aarhus Art Building. In urban forests and parks can be found sculptures Jorn Rønnau. There are about 45 active theaters and about 30 have their own scene. Worth a visit: Old Town, Museum of Women in Denmark, Ancient Art Museum, the Viking Museum, the Botanical Garden Memorial Park Marselisborg forest, lake and Brabrand Arslev Engsø, Marselisborg Castle.

5.Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek
Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek - Art Museum, located in Copenhagen, Denmark. Based son of the eponymous brewer. Carl Jacobsen was an avid collector of works of art, which are assembled from antiques and contemporary art from around the world.Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek has the largest collection of Scandinavian antiquities, some objects more than 8,000 years. In the halls 1-4 you can experience a magnificent collection of Pharaonic Egypt with an emphasis on large sculptures. These rooms contain over 300 exhibits from all over Egypt from 3000 to 75 BC In the halls of 6-18, more than 800 Greek and Roman artifacts.
