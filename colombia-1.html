1.Bogotá
Bogotá is the capital of Colombia, the largest city. Bogota is located in the basin on the western slope of the eastern Cordillera, at an altitude of 2610 m above sea level. The main museums of Colombia are concentrated in Bogotá. In the city there is a botanical garden, in which many kinds of exotic plants are represented. The annual and the most important sport spectacle of the capital are bullfights, gathering from 100 to 150 thousand spectators. Modern Bogota is a city of parks, office buildings and shopping centers.

2.Medellin
Medellin - one of the most populated cities of Colombia is located in the Valle de - Aburrá. Located at an altitude of 1500 m above sea level. Among the main attractions is necessary to allocate the Museo de Antioquia, International Convention and Exhibition - Plaza Mayor, theater Pablo Tobon Uribe, the Church of Veracruz, Torres de la Plaza de la Libertad, the Museum of Modern Art, El Castillo Museum, the Interactive Museum the EPM, Planetarium, Explora park, a botanical garden, Cerro el - Volador, Cerro Nutibara, Arvi regional park (20 000 ha). Local Christmas lights National Geographic included in the 10 most beautiful in the world. In 2013, the city won the best urban design awarded by Harvard University. The local zoo has more than 1,000 animal species. The city hosts cultural events: Flower Fair (end of July), the International Poetry Festival (July), the International Tango Festival, International Jazz Festival (September), Festival of humor, International Horse Fair (October).

3.Barranquilla
Barranquilla - seaside town in Colombia. It is home to one of the biggest festivals of the Carnival of Barranquilla Colombia, which is an intangible object of UNESCO held in late February. In the north of the city many luxury hotels. It is worth visiting the Magdalena River, the port, the Muelle de Puerto Colombia - the longest in the world port length of about 4000 m, Barranquilla Zoo features more than 500 animal species, North of the city - the most developed area has many parks, hotels and monuments. Also in the north-west of the city there are a few resorts where you can enjoy water sports, hiking and camping. The city hosts many festivals, fairs, carnivals, cultural and gastronomic events.

4.Cartagena
Cartagena - city by the sea in the north of Colombia. The city was founded in 1533. One of the most important tourist destinations in Colombia. The city combines the natural beauty, the Spanish colonial architecture and marine leisure. Among the main architectural attractions worth mentioning San Felipe Castillo de Barajas, Clock Tower, San Castle - Philip, the Inquisition Palace, Bocagrande Beach, San - Pedro Claver, Fort San Fernando, Plaza de la Aduana, Fort San Sebastian, Teatro Adolfo Mejia. Coastal areas of the city include beaches, sand dunes, thickets mango. The historic city center is protected by UNESCO.

5.Bucaramanga
Bucaramanga - city in Colombia. The World Bank has called the city of the most prosperous and competitive city in Latin America. In the city a popular eco-tourism. The city center is represented by the old buildings of the colonial era. At the level of government the city is included in the 7 main areas of the country's tourism. Worth a visit Chicamocha National Park (includes Chicamocha river canyon and the cable car), Girón (the old town with zolotymy mines and museum), the Museum of the Casa - de - Bolivar, the Cultural Center of the East, Museum of Contemporary Art, Neomundo (new generation science park equipment) natural history Museum, Planetario UIS, Auditorium, Water Park, Embalse de Bucaramanga, Provincial viaduct, festival de Teatro Santander, the International Piano festival, the festival of music and ecology Búcaro, International Theatre festival and the Street of Cirque - de - Santander.

6.San Andres
San Andres-Providencia is an archipelago near Nicaragua in Colombia. The main attractions include Blow Hole, Cueva de Morgan, Laguna Big Pond, Parque nacional natural Old Providence McBean Lagoon, Botanical Gardens and local beaches. In 2000, the whole territory of the archipelago, including reefs, atolls and sandbanks, representing about 10% of the Caribbean Sea, was declared a biosphere reserve by the planet. Warm azure waters, amazing nature, wide sandy beaches, extensive coastal coral reefs, incredibly rich marine life - all this makes the archipelago the most popular resort area of ​​Colombia.

7.Rosario Islands
Rosario Islands - is a small archipelago formed by 28 islands, which is part of the island region of Cartagena, Colombia. They are located in one of the natural national parks of Colombia, created to protect one of the most important coral reefs on the Colombian coast of the Caribbean Sea. Today Islas del Rosario with its crystal clear waters and coral reefs is a popular place for beach recreation and diving. Activities such as swimming, diving, fishing and others are offered.

8.San Gil
San Gil is a municipality in the department of Santander in Colombia. The capital of tourism of Colombia. The city is especially famous for various kinds of extreme sports, such as paragliding, rafting, river descent, canyoning, bungee jumping, mountain biking and motocross at Rio Fonce and neighboring Rio Soares. In the urban area, Parque Natural El Gallineral is also a popular place on the island between Rio Fonce and two streams. The park is known for its special vegetation. It has waterfalls, rivers, natural caves, mountains and the great Chikamocha canyon, located just an hour's drive from San Gil.

9.National Coffee Park
By Uria Ashkenazy CC2.5
National Coffee Park is a theme park located in the village of Pueblo Tapao, the municipality of Montenegro in Quindio, Colombia. It features a cable car, a show, a world coffee garden, three roller coasters, coffee-based food stalls, Colombian folk architecture, a total of 40 rides that are divided into: mechanical, water and themed. There are attractions for all ages, there is broca, one of the largest roller coasters in Latin America with a length of 1050 meters. In the second part of the park you will find a funicular and 25 new attractions, which are combined with fun, architecture, gastronomy, history and cultural past of coffee.

10.Parque Nacional Natural Tayrona
Park near the Caribbean - Tayrona National Park, Colombia. One of the most important national parks. The area of ​​15 000 hectares. In the park you can see more than 350 species of algae, 770 species of plants. Also in the park are many orchids, white beaches, mangrove thickets, rocks, coral, tropical forests. In the park are the archaeological excavations. It is worth visiting the Archaeological Museum Chairama, Playa - del - Muerto or Crystal Beach, Reef Sector (zone with accommodation), Pueblito Chairama (ancient settlement), Nudist beach.
