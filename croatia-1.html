3.Dubrovnik
Dubrovnik - a town in Dalmatia, in the south of Croatia, on the Adriatic coast. Well-known international resort, a major seaport. Architecturally Dubrovnik is divided into two unequal parts: the Old and New Town. The historic core of the city, Old Town, located within the massive defensive walls, formed his appearance in the XVII century. The old town is an example of medieval Mediterranean town and protected by UNESCO. The new city, despite the presence of some old buildings, mostly built up in the late XIX - early XX century, but because it is dominated by modern, as a rule, low-rise architecture. The main architectural attractions: Minceta Fortress, Onofrio's Fountain, Church of St. Blaise, Skočibuha Palace, Pozza Palace, Orlando's Column, a municipal swimming pool in the old town. Festivals in the city: Libertas Film Festival (end of August), Dubrovnik International Film Festival, Quarantine (drama festival). Also, the city has several museums.

9.Korčula
Korčula - Croatian island in the Adriatic Sea off the Dalmatian coast in the south. Attractions: Ripe Vela - a cave near the town of Vela Luka, which found numerous artifacts dating back to the Mesolithic and Neolithic. Korcula Old Town - a well-preserved example of a medieval Dalmatian town with narrow streets and old buildings. Korkyra Baroque Festival.

10.Brač
Brač is an island in the Adriatic Sea, in the southern part of Croatia, near the Dalmatian coast. The island of Brač is known for its mild Mediterranean climate, sandy and pebble beaches, pine groves and beautiful scenery, which makes it an attractive resort. Particularly famous for the island were quarries near the town of Pučišće, where they produce a white "brach stone", which is a special limestone of white color. Many famous buildings around the world are faced with the Brač stone, among others the Diocletian's Palace in Split and the White House in Washington. Bol is a popular resort, lying at the foot of the Vidovaya Mountain. Near Bol is the Golden Cape with its famous beaches, and also known for changing its shape depending on winds and currents.
