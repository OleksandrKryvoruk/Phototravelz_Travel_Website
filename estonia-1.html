Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Estonia.

1.Tallinn
Tallinn - a city on the coast of the Tallinn Bay. Tallinn Old Town is one of the best preserved medieval towns in Europe and is included in the UNESCO list. Tallinn receives over 4.3 million visitors a year. Local museums: Estonian History Museum, Estonian Maritime Museum, Museum of Occupation, Art Museum of Estonia. Architectural attractions: Kadriorg Palace, Toompea Castle, Alexander Nevsky Orthodox Cathedral, Botanical Gardens.

2.Toompea
Toompea is a limestone hill in the central part of Tallinn, Estonia. Toompea Hill is part of the Old Town of Tallinn, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The history of Toompea is closely connected with the history of rulers and authorities in Estonia. Today it is the seat of the Estonian government and the Riigikogu (parliament), both of which are often also called Toompea. The Riigikogu is located in Toompea Castle, located in the south-western part of the hill. The tallest part of the castle is the tower "Long Herman", the state flag on top of which is one of the most famous symbols of the current state power.

3.Lahemaa National Park
By Unique Hotels CC2
Lahemaa National Park is Estonia's largest national park, which is located 70 km east of Tallinn. The southern part of Lahemaa National Park extends over the broad forests and swamps of Kiervemaa. Lahemaa has become home to numerous species of animals and birds, as well as flowers and plants. Of the animals presented, in particular, you can see bears, wolves and lynx. From the shores of the beach along the park, you can often see white swans and other migratory waterfowl. The park also has a large number of rare species. The encompassing historical-natural and hiking routes laid through the park will be a digression into the ancient history of Estonian settlements, the cultural heritage of the Baltic Germans, the rich ecosystems of the North Estonian marshes and, finally, the world of beavers. Not far from the village of Altja, also in the park, is the only open-air geology museum in Estonia. Below - the most famous in the park is a small village on the beach offering opportunities for tourist accommodation while relaxing. You can stop and in several mines in the park.

4.Tallinn Botanic Garden
By Bernt Rostad CC2

5.Kadriorg Palace
By Guillaume Speurt CC2
Kadriorg Palace is a baroque palace and park ensemble in Tallinn. First had an area of about 100 hectares. The park is planned to be restored in the style of the XVIII century, including shorn trees. In this style, the palace garden has been restored. In the park there is the house-museum of Peter I, on the limit - the modern building of the Estonian Museum of Art (Kumu Museum). The stakes in the park were dug out immediately when laying both for revitalizing the landscape and for draining the soil.

6.Hermann Castle
By Aleksander Kaasik CC4
Hermann Castle is a medieval castle in the Estonian city of Narva on the banks of the Narva River (Narova). It was founded by the Danes in the middle of the 13th century. In the year 1347. During the Second World War, it was severely damaged, as was most of Narva. Today the castle is restored, it houses the Narva Museum. Opposite the castle of Herman, on the opposite bank of the Narova River, is the Russian Ivangorod fortress.

10.Soomaa National Park
By Vinayak Hegde CC2
Soomaa National Park is a national park in the south-west of Estonia, founded on July 10, 1957. Soomaa in translation means "country of swamps". The park area is 398.4 km2 - it is the second largest Estonian national park after Lahemaa. Park Soomaa was created to protect marshes, flowering meadows, forests and winding rivers. What makes this park unique is, so-called, the "fifth season" - the spring flood, which causes the water level to rise to 5 meters. During this period, flooding spreads everywhere - into meadows, fields, forests and paths, even houses in some places are flooded with water. The maximum area of ​​the spill reaches 7-8 km, however, floods also occurred, reaching 150 km2. Therefore, rafting on canoes and kayaks is sometimes the best way to get acquainted with the terrain and the way of development of the Soomaa park spaces. This service is offered by local travel companies to anyone who wishes. Along with excursions to the park by canoe, there are also many hiking routes.
