11.The White House
The White House is the residence of the US President in Washington. It was the residence for all US presidents, except for George Washington, because during his reign he was only being built. This mansion includes 6 floors: two for the president's family, two for public receptions and two socle. In the White House there are excursions where you can see: Green room, Blue room, Eastern room, Red room, Dining room and Oval office. Accepts up to 5000 visitors daily. It is depicted on a banknote with a face value of 20 dollars.

12.Atlanta
Atlanta is the capital and largest city of Georgia, USA. A large industrial, trade and financial center of the South of the USA. In recent years, Atlanta has acquired the status of one of the international business centers, which gave a powerful impetus to the economic and social development of the city. The main attractions include Atlanta Botanic Garden, CNN Headquarters, Road Atlanta Auto Track, Coca-Cola World Museum, Olympic Park, Fox Theater, Georgia Aquarium Oceanarium, Martin Luther King Museum, Stone Mountain Park, Atlanta Zoo, Pidmont Park, Museum Complex Atlante History Center. The city has many skyscrapers, the highest Bank of America Plaza (312 m.).

13.Houston
Houston is the most populated city in the state of Texas in the USA. The official nickname of Houston is "Space city", which can be translated as "space city", "city of cosmonautics" or "cosmograde". This name is due to the fact that there is a space center named after Lyndon Johnson. It is worth visiting: the world's largest Texas Medical Center, Houston Theater District, Houston Livestock and Rodeo Festival, Houston Motor Vehicle Parade, Houston Chinatown, Mahatma Gandhi district, Hermann Park, Hershey Terry Park, Houston Town Hall and NRG Stadium.

14.Boston
Boston is a city in the northeastern part of the United States, the capital and largest city of Massachusetts, founded in 1630. Museums: Computer Museum, Isabella Stuart Gardner Museum, public buildings and cathedrals of the 18th-19th centuries; Residential buildings of the XVII-XIX centuries., Museum of Science, Boston Museum of Fine Arts. Boston Common Park is the oldest US park. The largest park in the city is Franklin Park, which includes a zoo. The main architectural sights of the city include: Longfellow Bridge, Massachusetts State Capitol, Old Capitol, Paul Revere House and Boston Public Library.

15.Yosemite National Park
Yosemite National Park is a national park located in the vicinity of Mariposa and Tuolemy in California, USA. It is 5 hours drive from the city of San Francisco and about 6 hours drive from Los Angeles. The park covers an area of ​​3,081 km2 and is located on the western slopes of the Sierra Nevada mountain range. The park is visited by about 3 million people a year. In 1984 the park received the status of World Heritage under the auspices of UNESCO, and since then enjoys international recognition for the impressive granite rocks, waterfalls, rivers with clear water, groves. The highest point of the park is 4000 m, it has about 15 waterfalls with a height of 100 to 700 m. Over 1300 km of trails are available for travelers. Mountaineering and winter sports are also developed.

16.Arches National Park
The Arches National Park is located in Utah, USA. There are a lot of natural arches in the park (about 2000). The park is world famous thanks to Delicate Arch. The entrance to the park is five miles from the city of Moab and is easily accessible by car. The highest point of the park is 1723 m., The lowest one is 1245 m. Unfortunately mountaineering in the park is prohibited. In the park live such animals: red fox, peregrine falcon, yucca moth, sparrows and lizards. Next to this park is the Canyonlands National Park, which offers similar rock formations, but is much more inaccessible.

18.Seattle
Seattle - the largest city in the state of Washington, USA. The visiting card of the city is the Space Needle height of 181 m. It is also worth visiting the Pike Place Market, Skyscraper Smith Tower, Coffee Starbucks. More than 150 monuments of the city are included in the National Register of Historic Places. The city hosts film festival Seattle International Film Festival, Folk Festival Northwest Folklife, dance festival Bon Odori, a culinary festival of the Bite of Seattle, an exhibition of computer games Penny Arcade Expo, Seattle to Portland Bicycle Classic and has more than 15 thematic festivals. The most common types of active recreation in the city include cycling, skiing and snowboarding, sailing, climbing and swimming.

20.Portland
Portland is a city in the US, the center of Multnomah County, Oregon. The city is located in the north-west of the state, at the confluence of the Villametta River in the Columbia River. The fictional American city of Springfield, in which the action of the animated television series The Simpsons takes place, according to Graining, has much in common with Portland, the city in which he grew up. Museums: Oregon Historical Society Museum, American Advertising Museum, Portland Art Museum. Other cultural sites: Portland Center for the Performing Arts, Forest Park - the largest urban forest in the USA, opera, state symphony orchestra. There is an annual festival and an exhibition of roses (in June). In Portland there are more than 40 breweries - this is more than in any other city in the world. The city usually takes one of the first places in the ratings for quality of life.
