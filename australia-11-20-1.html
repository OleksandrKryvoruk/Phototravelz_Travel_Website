11.Sydney Harbour Bridge
Sydney Harbor Bridge - Bridge - a bridge connecting the north and south of Sydney, Australia. The length of the bridge is 1150 m, width 50 m, the bridge was opened in 2007. It is included in the list of national heritage of Australia. Near the bridge is the famous Sydney Opera House, and you can also watch New Year's fireworks. Since October 1, 1998 on the bridge regular excursions are carried out for people over 10 years old - an ascent along the side arch of the bridge to its peak, from where a breathtaking panorama of the city opens. For ascent, only shoes with rubber soles and a special suit with insurance, which is issued on the spot, are required, the instructor will be able to cope with everything else.

12.Sunshine Coast
By Chic Urban Living CC2
Sunshine Coast, Queensland is the region and third largest city in the Australian state of Queensland. Sunshine Coast is the center of tourism, which attracts more than 3.2 million visitors a year. Here are attractions such as the Australian zoo Steve Irwin, the marine park "Underwater World", "Big pineapple", the market in Eumundi and the theater in Pomona. Sport tourism is supported by several annual sporting events, such as "Mululaba Triathlon", "Triathlon Nusi" and "Sunshine Coast Marathon". In the Sunshine Coast area, there are more national parks than any other region of Queensland. On several stretches of the Sunshine Coast, the beach is inseparable from Sunshine Beach at Nusi Beach to Coolum Beach (17 km) from Arkwright Point to Muddimby (11 km); from Maroochydore to Mooloolaba (5.6 km) from Buddin to Pelican Waters (22 km).

13.Byron Bay
Byron Bay is a city in Australia, and is also the easternmost point of the country. Its name was given to the city by James Cook. A popular place for tourism, close to the city is a large number of beautiful beaches, where you can surf and swim under water. The Cape Byron Lighthouse is one of the city's attractions that help to monitor whales, which in turn is a very important industry and is a source of local economy. Byron Bay is also located near the subtropical forests.

14.Coolum Beach
Coolum Beach - the city is located along the beach and is ideal for those who like a quiet beach relax, located in the Sunshine Coast in the Australian state of Queensland. The nearest major city of Brisbane is 119 kilometers away. In the city a lot of places with stunning views - this is the place for rest of the rich and famous. Local beaches are very well suited for surfing. Not far from the city is Mount Columbus, as well as the homonymous national park. Julian Wilson, a world-famous surfer, was born and raised in Coolum Beach.

15.Airlie Beach
Airlie Beach is a small resort town in Australia with many beaches, hotels, cafes and restaurants. The main attraction of Earlie Beach is the Great Barrier Reef, which can be seen using aerial excursions. For those who like to swim underwater, there are special diving tours. Erli Beach is considered a very popular place for sailing, every year there are races on yachts. Near the city there are many islands that are national parks, as well as exotic resorts on their territory.

16.Blue Mountains
The Blue mountains received their name because of the blue fog that appears there due to the evaporation of the eucalyptus trees. The mountains are located near Sydney (Australia) and stretch for 50 km. The highest point is 1360 m above sea level, the deepest canyon is 800 m. In the air of the Blue Mountains, there are a lot of smells of essential oils of more than 90 species of eucalyptus. On the territory of the mountains there is a reserve, which is part of the UNESCO world heritage. Also on the territory of the reserve there are many caves, waterfalls and a railway with the biggest slope in the world of 50.7 degrees.

17.Alice Springs
Alice Springs is a city in the south of the Northern Territory of Australia. The most important factor for attracting tourists to the city is Mount Uluru. Alice Springs has a large number of tourist resorts, a casino, an Aboriginal art museum, nightclubs, pubs, many restaurants and cafes. For visiting tourists, the following programs are also available: visiting the Desert Park, the old telegraph station, the reptile center, the botanical garden and the cinema, as well as camel tours, hot air ballooning and much more. To accommodate tourists are available several hotels, hostels, as well as special parks for stopping people coming on their own cars. During the year, many different festivals and performances are held in Alice Springs, including races in the desert and along the Todd River.

18.Phillip Island
Phillip Island is an island in the state of Victoria (Australia), located in the Bass Strait to the southeast of Melbourne, it is visited by more than 3.5 million tourists annually. The most famous landmark of the island is the "Penguin Parade" - whole flocks of small penguins, who every morning go to sea to fish. On the island are the competitions of the Australian Grand Prix for motorcycles, as well as the World Superbike Tour. In the homonymous reserves you can watch koalas, seals, sharks and dolphins. In addition, the island created a wildlife park in which you can feed animals from their hands. The park has more than 300 animals (mainly representatives of the Australian fauna). Here live wombats, koalas, kangaroos, Tasmanian devil, various kinds of birds, snakes and reptiles.

19.Bondi Bay
Bondi Beach - one of the most popular beaches are in the suburbs of Sydney. In 2008, the beach has been added to the Australian National Heritage List. On the beach operates Wi-Fi zone. On the territory of Bondi Beach every January a festival of short films, sculptures sea in November, the World Environment Day in June. The beach is one of the best rescue club and skate park in the world. On the beach there is a contest Miss Bondi and still many festivals and events.

20.Adelaide
Adelaide is the capital and industrial center of South Australia, a port in the Gulf of Saint Vincent of the Indian Ocean. Adelaide is located in a picturesque area on the shores of the Indian Ocean. There are art and historical museums, a conservatory, an observatory, an art gallery, many parks and gardens, a zoo. In addition, in Adelaide tourists are attracted by the third largest island in Australia, Kangaroo Island - a wildlife sanctuary with a sea lion colony and a well-fit fishing coast. Many tourists are also attracted by the Barossa Valley, the winemaking center of Australia, which lies a few kilometers from Adelaide.
