21.Portovenere
Portovenere - a city in the Liguria region, province of La Spezia (Italy). In 1997 Porto Venere, together with the islands of Palmaria, Tino, Tinetto and Cinque Terre was inserted into the UNESCO World Heritage List. The city has many monuments: The parish church of San -Lorentso, Church of San - Pietro, Abbey of San - Venerio, Doria Castle, San - Venerio Lighthouse, Fort Umberto, Varignano Fortress, Roman Villa Varignano. Natural attractions: the caves Byron Bay Small and Large Marine cavity Colombi. The city has award FEE-Italy for the high standard of living. It is worth visiting a museum: Antiquarium on Varignano Roman villa and the Church Museum of San - Lorenzo. In summer, the city is visited by many celebrities.

22.San Gimignano
San Gimignano is a city and a municipality in central Italy, in the province of Siena. The historical center of the city is on UNESCO's list. The main attractions: Piazza della Cisterna Piazza Duomo, Pekori Square, Palazzo del Popolo, Church of St. Augustine. The main architectural "highlight" of San Gimignano, of course, are fourteen medieval towers, built by the most notable families, who wanted to emphasize their social standing, in the XI-XIII centuries. In total, according to various sources, from 70 to 76 towers were built. In the computer game Assassin's Creed II in 2009, San Gimignano is a game location (the game takes place at the end of the XV century).

Annually Stresa is welcoming "Settimane Musicali" - one of the most famous classical music festivals. And the funicular (funivia) can be in the botanical garden Alpinia and reach the top of Mount Mottarone.

24.Lake Garda
Lake Garda - is the largest lake in Italy. Today it is a large tourist center with developed infrastructure of resorts and hotels along its banks. Garda is a well known area of ​​winemaking. On the shores of the lake are the resorts, the most famous of which are Garda, Sirmione, Desenzano del Garda, Bardolino, Malcesine, Riva del Garda. On the eastern shore of the lake, the Gardezana-Oriental reserve was created. On Lake Garda is the largest park of children's entertainment in Italy - Gardaland, as well as the Seaworld aquarium, Canevaworld water park, Mouviland amusement park.

25.Gardaland
By Roberto Ferrari CC2
Gardaland is the third most popular entertainment park in Europe, built on the eastern shore of Lake Garda in Italy (the commune of Castelnuovo del Garda). The park is considered the most popular in Italy. Now it has 7 main attractions: Magic Mountain, Sequoia Adventure, Raptor, Blue Tornado, Orto Bruco, Mammut and Fuga da Atlantide. In June 2008, an oceanarium was opened across the road from the park. In 2015, the park opened the first in Italy "diving" attraction - "Oblivion - The Black Hole".

26.Montepulciano
Montepulciano - Italian town in the province of Siena in Tuscany. He is known for its wealth of beautiful vineyards that produce Vino Nobile di Montepulciano DOCG. Architectural Objects: Jesus Church, Monastery of St. Francis, the Oratory of San Giovanni Battista in Il Poggiolo, Church of Saint Lucia, Palazzo Cervini, Palazzo Grugni, Palazzo Blacks-Orselli, Palazzo Ricci, Medici Fortress. Also worth a visit Montepulciano Lake Natural Reserve.

27.Lake Como
Lake Como - Lake at the foot of the Alps in the north Italy . Located at an altitude of 198 m in the basin tectonic-glacial origin. The banks mostly high and rocky. In the center of the lake, on the eastern shore, in a fraction of the municipality of Perledo is Vezio Castle. Going down to Lecco, you can admire the Castle Lierna. On the west coast is Musso Castle. But the biggest Lariano castle on the island of Comacina and was destroyed by Frederick Barbarossa. Belfry Xenodochio Santa Maria Maddalena Ossuccio and Sacro Monte di Ossuccio included in the UNESCO World Heritage List.

28.Piazza dei Miracoli
Piazza dei Miracoli - is located in the old part of the Italian city of Pisa. On the square there are four masterpieces of medieval architecture: the Leaning Tower of Pisa, the Pisa Baptistery, the Pisa Cathedral and the Cemetery of Campo Santo. They belong to the masterpieces of medieval architecture and had a great influence on the monumental art in Italy from the 11th to the 14th centuries. In 1987, the architectural ensemble was listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Vatican Museums is a complex of museums in Rome in Italy, where art collections are collected, collected over the centuries by the Pope. About 4 million visitors visit the exposition annually. Vatican Museums were founded in the 16th century by Pope Julius II. The museum's fund is a collection of classical works, which since 1503 was exhibited by Pope Julius II. 47 000 m 2 are available for the public today, endurance on the routes of 7 km. Inside the museums there is also the world-famous Sistine Chapel.

30.Pompeii
Pompeii - the Roman city, the ruins of which are located within the modern commune of Pompei in the Campania region of Italy, near Naples. Pompeii were killed in the eruption of Vesuvius August 24, 79, which buried the city under tons of dust. In the excavated parts of Pompeii it is equipped with an open-air museum. Many of the finds are kept in the National Archaeological Museum (Naples). The ruins of the city by hundreds of tourists visit daily. During the eruption of Vesuvius killed three cities - Pompeii, Herculaneum and Stabiae - as well as the adjacent ville . Excavations found the city under a layer of lava multimeter as they were before the disaster: the street, the house fully furnished and the people and animals that do not have time saved (on the streets, killing about 2,000 people).
