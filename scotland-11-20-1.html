11.Palace of Holyroodhouse
Palace of Holyroodhouse - the official home of the British monarch in Scotland and is the main residence of Scottish monarchs since the 15th century. The palace as it looks today was built mostly in the 17 - th century. An exception is the north - west tower of the 16th century. Local park covers 4 hectares.

12.Rosslyn chapel
Rosslyn Chapel - the chapel of the fifteenth century, built in the Scottish village of Roslin. Included are many legends about the treasure of the Knights Templar and Freemasonry traditions. It became popular because of the movie Code of Leonardo da Vinci by Dan Brown. Now the Protestant church.

13.Fairy Pools
Fairy Pools - waterfalls and lakes on the island of Skye. These are bright blue and green lakes of unnatural origin. Considered one of the most beautiful places of the island of Skye and one of the most amazing spectacles in all of Scotland. Fairy pools are a cascade of waterfalls, popular among travelers and lovers of swimming in natural water bodies, if they are not afraid of ice water. All the lakes are crystal clear and surrounded by large rocky cliffs and walls, waterfalls and lush foliage.

14.Glencoe
Glencoe - literally tears Valley - a mountainous valley in the west of Scotland, where there was a historical Massacre of Glencoe (1692). Valley can be seen in several films, such as Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban. Glencoe is also a favorite place for relaxation, self-catering, with many cottages that are available for weekly rest.

15.Loch Lomond
Loch Lomond is a lake in Scotland, in the administrative areas of Sterling, Argyll and Bute and West Dunbartonshire. According to the area of the mirror (71 km2), Loch Lomond occupies the first place among the lakes of Scotland, in volume (2.6 km3) - the second place after Loch Ness. The lake is surrounded by mountains, the highest peak of Ben Lomond is in the south and reaches 973 meters. The western shore of Loch Lomond is only 32 km from the center of Glasgow.

17.Isle of Mull
Isle of Mull - the second largest island in the archipelago of the Inner Hebrides, is a member of the administrative units of Argyll and Bute, Scotland. The island is hilly - the highest peak of 966 meters above sea level. The island has only one town of Tobermory. Attractions on the island of Castle hummocks, Duart Castle manor Clan McLean, Scotch whiskey (Scotch).

16.Corgarff Castle
Corgarff Castle - sixteenth century castle located in Corgarff, Strathdon, in the Scottish region of Aberdeenshire. In the eighteenth century the castle was turned into a military barracks. The castle was built around 1550. In 1607 Corgarff castle was captured by a group of local thugs. In 1802 Corgarff castle was leased to James McHardy, who used it as a farm. In 1748 the buildings around the courtyard have been demolished and replaced by two pavilions. In the courtyard there is a well on the west side. Corgarff Castle Historic Scotland is managed by the organization.

Luskentyre beach - a beach on the coast of Harris in the Outer Hebrides, Scotland. Luskentyre Beach was voted the best beach of the United Kingdom. Wildlife in the area includes the Common Scoter, scoter, eider duck, wigeon, long-tailed duck, red-merganser, and the grebe.

20.Tantallon Castle
Tantallon Castle is the ruins of a medieval 14th century castle located about 5 km east of the Scottish city of North Berwick. The castle is a tourist attraction, open all year round, which attracted more than 30,000 visitors. There are, including many sea birds that nest in ruins: terns, cormorants.
