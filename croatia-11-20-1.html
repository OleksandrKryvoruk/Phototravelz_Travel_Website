11.Cavtat
Cavtat - a town on the Adriatic Sea, Croatia. The city was founded in antiquity as a Greek colony Epidaurus around the 6th century BC. E A significant event in the cultural life in Cavtat - International Festival of Music and Arts "Epidaurus", which begins every year in late August and lasts for a month. The festival offers a variety of events: classical music concerts, jazz, courses for young artists, exhibitions, theater performances and lectures. In the vicinity of Cavtat are located the ruins of Illyrian, Greek and Roman settlements. During the excavations there found amphorae, parts of buildings, mosaics and the like.

12.Makarska
Makarska is a city in Croatia on the Dalmatian coast in the Adriatic Sea. The city is a tourist resort, located in a secluded bay of the Adriatic Sea at the foot of the mountain Viokovo. The city is characterized by a pedestrian zone with palm trees. The city is the center of the Makarska Riviera, the length of the coastline is 60 km. In the city there are three museums.

14.Hvar
Hvar - an island in the Adriatic Sea, in the southern part of Croatia, near the Dalmatian coast, as well as the city of the same name on it. The name of the island comes from its Greek name Pharos. The island of Hvar is known as the sunniest island of the Adriatic (349 sunny days per year). A lot of attractions, beautiful nature and mild climate make it an attractive resort. The main occupation of the local population is tourist service, fishing and viticulture. On Hvar in a large number of cultivated lavender, which has become a kind of symbol of the island and the most popular souvenir from tourists.

15.Rab
Rab is an island in Croatia in the Adriatic Sea. The island of Rab has many historical monuments, places for tourism and beaches. The slave is also a small town, located on a long and narrow spit. Like the town of Pag, in the Middle Ages the city was rebuilt according to the drawings of Yuray Dalmatinets. Among the many ancient buildings of the city there are four Romanesque bell-towers of different periods, a Benedictine monastery of the 11th century, the Church of St. Mary (XIII century). Sup Draga - a village in the northern part of the island on the shore of a picturesque bay. In the village church - the oldest bell of the Adriatic, dating from 1059 year.
