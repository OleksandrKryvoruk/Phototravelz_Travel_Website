2.Kathmandu
Kathmandu - the capital and largest city in Nepal. The city is located in the Himalayas at over 1,350 meters above sea level. Bhaktapur - a city in Nepal, located in the Kathmandu Valley, 16 km from the city of Kathmandu. The city was founded in the ninth century. As a result of the earthquake that occurred in Nepal in April 2015, much of the city was destroyed. 5.Chitwan National Park
Chitwan National Park is a national park in Nepal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is located 200 km to the south-west from the capital of Kathmandu. Until 1973, Chitwan was a favorite hunting place for Nepalese kings. The area of the park is 932 sq.km, mostly covered with jungle. There are many picturesque rivers and small lakes in the park. Of the representatives of the fauna, the greatest interest is represented by rhinoceroses, elephants, deer, buffaloes, and tigers. Chitwan is open for tourists. The main tourist center in the vicinity of the park is the village of Sauraha.

7.Boudhanath
Boudhanath is a Buddhist temple in Kathmandu (near the village of Sankha) in Nepal. The date of its construction is unknown, but it is believed that this may be the age of Emperor Ashoka. It is considered one of the most important Buddhist temples. This is a place of pilgrimage for Buddhists all over the world. It is included in UNESCO list.

8.Pashupatinath Temple
Pashupatinath Temple is a large temple complex located on both banks of the Bagmati River in Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal. The temple is the sanctuary of the patron saint of Nepal, the god Pashupathi (Shri Pashupatinath) - one of the avatars of Shiva and is revered as one of the most holy temples among all siivists. Entrance to the inner rooms and courtyards of the temple is allowed only to Indians, the rest of the visitors can only look at the ceremony from the opposite bank of the river. Together with other temples of the Kathmandu Valley, Pashupatinath is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
