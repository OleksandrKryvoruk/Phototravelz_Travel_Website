21.Karlsruhe Palace
Karlsruhe Palace - the residence of the Margrave Karl Wilhelm was built in 1715 in Germany. Currently, the building has a state museum of Baden. In the northern part of the castle, the opposite side of the city, Christian Thran garden built in the Baroque style, with numerous species of rare plants, sculptures, monuments and fountains of different periods, from Baroque to modern. In 1967 a small railway was built to guide visitors through the park.

22.Rhine Valley
Rhine Valley or the Middle Rhine - Rhine Valley between Bingen and Rüdesheim and Bonn, in which there are numerous monuments of medieval architecture in Germany. Included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. The main attractions on the banks of the river: Geyser Andernach, wine Middle Rhine, Castle Pfalzgrafenstein, Castle Rheinfels, Marksburg, Stolzenfels Castle, Castle Klopp Castle Sooneck, Heimburg Castle Niderhaymbah Castle Furstenberg Castle Stahleck, Schönburg Castle, Stolzenfels Castle, voters Palace Koblenz, Castle Marienfels, Boosenburg Castle, Maus Castle, Ehrenbreitstein Fortress, Drachenburg Palace.

23.Tropical Islands Resort
 By Technouwe CC3
Tropical Islands Resort is a tropical amusement park located in Halbe in the land of Brandenburg, Germany. It is contained in the former Elinsi (hangar for airships), previously owned by Cargolifter AG and called Aerium. It is the largest self-supporting hall in the world, and also occupies the fourth line in the list of the world's largest buildings and structures - 5200000 m3. The park is divided into the following thematic areas: Tropical village - with buildings in the style of Thailand, Borneo, Samoa and Bali. The tropical forest - more than 50 000 plants of more than 600 species. South Sea - Swimming pool 140 m long, with an area of ​​4400 m 2 and 1.35 m in depth with a sandy beach, a width of 200 m, 850 wooden deckchairs that decompose. Water temperature 28 C. Lagoon with Bali - more than 1200 m 2 of water surface of variable depth, water temperature 32 C, fountains, artificial currents, water slides.

24.Kehlsteinhaus
Kehlsteinhaus - a tea house built from 1937 to 1938, as a gift to the NSDAP for the 50th anniversary of Adolf Hitler. The house is built at an altitude of 1834 m. It is located near his now ruined residence Berghof in the Bavarian Alps in the district of Berchtesgaden, Bavaria (Germany). Currently, traffic to the Eagle's Nest is permitted only for special transport and special tourist buses. Buses leave from Berchtesgaden station, as well as from the "Oberzalzberg Exhibition" in Hintrek every 25 minutes. Buses stop moving at 16.50. From the end of October to the end of April (depending on the amount of snow in the mountains) excursions are not conducted.

25.Linderhof Palace
Linderhof Palace is located in Germany, in the south - west of Bavaria near monastery. It is the smallest of the three buildings constructed King Ludwig II. In contrast to the palace Herrenchiemsee, Linderhof is not a copy of an existing building. Front of the palace there is an artificial geyser, which erupts every 30 minutes. Garden covers an area of ​​about 50 hectares and is perfectly integrated into the surrounding natural Alpine landscape. In the park you can visit: Grotto of Venus, Hundingská hut, Gurnemanzova Hermitage stand Maori Moroccan Pavilion.

26.Hohenschwangau Castle
Hohenschwangau Castle is located right in front of Neuschwanstein Castle in the village of Schwangau in Bavaria (Germany). For the first time the castle is mentioned in the 12th century as a castle Schwanstein, Schwangau place nobility. The castle served as a royal family as a summer residence and place of upbringing Louis II. The castle is visited by about 300 thousand. Tourists from around the world each year. The castle is open all year round (except Christmas Day). You can hire a guide.

27.Rugen Island
Rugen Island - the largest island in Germany. It is located in the north - eastern edge of the Mecklenburg-Vorpommern on the Baltic Sea. The coast is characterized by numerous bays and lagoons and awarded by UNESCO. Rügen is one of the main tourist centers in Germany. On the island it was about a quarter of all overnight stays in Mecklenburg-Vorpommern. In 2014, the island recorded 5.89 million overnight stays. Main attractions: bathing resorts, spa resorts, a unique natural and cultural landscape attracts tourists wishing to explore the island by bicycle, on foot or on a sailing ship. Also on the island there are beech forests, chalk cliffs, the National Park Centre Königsstuhl, theater, Sand Sculpture Festival.

28.Ingolstadt
Ingolstadt - German city located on the river Danube in the federal state of Bavaria. The city preserved many monuments of architecture representing styles from Gothic to modern historical styles. The most valuable are: Herzogskasten - old Prince Castle, New Castle, now the site of the Military Museum, the fragments of the medieval walls, the Old Town Hall, Church of Sts. Maurice, Church of the Blessed Virgin Mary, Franciscan church, the headquarters - flat German Museum of the History of Medicine.

29.Bamberg
Bamberg - a city in Germany, located in the northern part of Bavaria (Germany) Regknits on the river, near the confluence of the Main, about 63 kilometers north of Nuremberg. Notable buildings in the city, one of the largest Romanesque churches in Germany, which was built around the 10 - th century, the old yard and picturesque tower Altenburg 10 - th century. Old Town is the largest intact historic center in Germany and part of the UNESCO World Heritage List. In addition, Bamberg is known for its diverse culture of manufacturing and consumption of beer. The city has about 10 theaters, as well as many museums: Studio Museum Bamberg Diocesan Museum Bamberg Historical Museum, Natural History Museum, Brewery Museum and Symphony Orchestra.

30.Schwerin Castle
Schwerin Castle is a castle located on the Museum Island in the center of Schwerin in Germany. This place is the parliament of Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania. The ring form of this building dates back to 965 year. The castle is a mixture of architectural styles. Previously, on the site of the palace on a small island on Zverinom lake since 965 there was a fortress of the Slavic-obedrites Zverin. The modern palace, eclectically mixed several architectural styles, was built in 1845-1857 by order of the Grand Duke Friedrich Franz II by the architect Georg Adolf Demmler. Today, the castle is considered a key element of Romanticism.
