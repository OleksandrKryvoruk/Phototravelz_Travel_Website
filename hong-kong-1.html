3.Lamma Island
Lamma Island is an island in the South China Sea, the third largest island in Hong Kong. Lamma Island in the background of Hong Kong looks like a quiet place and attracts tourists. On the island it is forbidden to build buildings above three floors, there are almost no cars. The total length of the beaches of the island is about 15 kilometers. The beaches are widely represented in various sports - yachting, tennis, cricket and squash. On Lamme constantly live not only Chinese, but also foreigners. The island has the reputation of a "quiet place", but it draws the attention of hippies and other non-formals. A major attraction of Lamma are the tunnels located near the Sok-Quo-Van, the so-called Kamikadze Grottoes. These are tunnels built by the Japanese, who occupied Hong Kong during the Second World War.

4.Ngong Ping 360
Ngong Ping 360 is a tourist project on the island of Lantau in Hong Kong, which consists of a cable car called Ngong Ping. Ngongping 360 connects the Tunchung area located on the northern coast of Lantau Island with the Ngongping area, located on the hills in the center of the island. In this area is the Po Lin Monastery and Tian Tang Buddha - the most important tourist attractions of Lantau Island.

6.Tian Tan Buddha
Tian Tan Buddha - is the world's tallest statue of a sitting Buddha. The statue is located on Lantau Island (Hong Kong). The total height of the statue is 34 meters, it is made of 202 bronze pieces with a total weight of 250 tons. The complex is a matter of great tourist interest. There is a cable car.
