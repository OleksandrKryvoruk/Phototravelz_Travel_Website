Azores - an archipelago of 9 mountainous volcanic islands and several reefs in the North Atlantic Ocean to the west of the Iberian Peninsula, Portugal. All the islands are mountainous terrain, with peaks up to 2000 m The highest point of the archipelago. Mount Pico on the eponymous island with a height of 2351 m The largest resort towns. Ponta Delgada, Angra do Heroísmo and Horta. On the islands, several international airports built. The climate is subtropical, oceanic, equal and soft, with small amplitudes of temperatures. The average January temperature is +14 C, in July - +22 C.

7.Sagres
Sagres - community in the extreme south-west coast of Portugal. Sagres Coast has several beaches - all located on the territory of the Natural Park of Southwest Alentejo and Visentinskogo coast. The most famous beaches are: Praia do Beliche, Praia do Tonel, Praia da Mareta, with excellent conditions for the practice of modern water sports: diving, jet skis and windsurfing. Among the architectural monuments of particular interest is the old fortress of Fortaleza de Sagres was built in the 15th century on the orders of Don Enrique Infante. Especially captures huge wind rose, created in stone that the diameter reaches 43 meters. Other Sagres fortress - at Cape St. Vicente Fortaleza do Cabo de São Vicente and Belisha Fortaleza de Santo António do Belixe. At Cape San Vicente originates the so-called "ecological route of the Algarve" - ​​a panoramic bike path that extends along the Atlantic coast region to the Villa Real de Santo António (border with Spain). This new kind of ecological tourism every year is becoming increasingly popular among tourists in the region. There is an information center to receive a free tourist information in the community.

8.Coimbra

Coimbra is also a city of art, there are 31 art galleries and dozens of museums that have collections of anthropology, zoology, botany, mineralogy, and scientific instruments collections of the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries, as well as about 10 theaters. Sights and attractions: the Monastery of San Francisco, the College of Saint Benedict, the Botanical Garden of the University of Coimbra, Bishop's Palace, which established the National Museum Machado de Castro, Botanical Museum, Museum of Science, Mondego Green Park.

10.Belém Tower
Belém Tower - fortress in the Portuguese capital, Lisbon, one of the symbols of the city. Located on the right bank of the Tagus River. In 1983, recognized by the UN World Heritage Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO), and the July 7, 2007 has been selected as one of the seven wonders of Portugal. The structure of the castle consists of two main elements: the tower and trees. In the corners of the terrace and the shafts are cylindrical guard towers, decorated with stone tower works. Quadrangular medieval tradition rises five stories above the bulwark, as follows: First floor - Governor Room, second floor - a room of the Kings, Third Floor - Court room, fourth floor - Chapel, Fifth floor - Terrace tower.
