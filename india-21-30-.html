21.Victoria Memorial
Victoria Memorial - a monument in honor of Queen Victoria, built in Calcutta in India. This is a huge building made of marble, surrounded by gardens in English style. Today it houses a museum. Today it is used as a museum, it is one of the tourist attractions of Calcutta. The plan of the memorial was designed by Sir William Emerson of the building going on to the Belfast Town Hall building. The building was to be sustained in the style of the Italian Renaissance, but the architect introduced some oriental elements into the composition.

22.Hyderabad
Hyderabad is a city in India, on the Dean plateau, the administrative center of the states of Telangana and Andhra Pradesh. In the city there are 3 universities, the cultural center of Telugu, museums, monuments of Muslim architecture of the XVI-XVIII centuries. (the triumphal gate-mosque and Hindu temple Charminar, other temples, palaces), the film industry of Tollywood. The city is notable for the large mosque Mecca-Masjid (XVIII century), built on the model of the famous mosque in Mecca; triumphal gates Charminar ("four minarets"), which are sometimes called the Arc de Triomphe of the East. In the city of Hyderabad - the fortress Golconda (XVII-XVIII century) with several mausoleums. Fort Golconda, located on a high hill.

23.Kerala
Kerala - a state in the south - west of India on the Malabar Coast. The largest city - Kochi, capital - Trivandrum. The most important monuments include Padmanabhapuram Palace, Aranmula, Kaladi, Mannarsala, Sabarimala, National Park Erawikulam, National Park Quiet velley. In capital: Sri Padmanabhaswamy Temple, the botanical and zoological garden with museums, Napier Museum (with products from the Bronze Age), Shri Art gallery. On the waterfront: Varkala - Janardhana temple on the beach, nature reserve with wild elephants in Thekkadi. In Cochin Portuguese-Dutch Palace Matancherry, Fort Kochi, Wayanad National Park (elephant, buffalo, peacocks, tigers).

24.Hampi
Hampi - is an Indian village in Karnataka. It is located on Vidžajanagar city ruins on the south bank of the river Tungabhadra. In 1500 Vidžajanagar had half a million inhabitants and was one of the most densely populated cities in the world. The village is known mainly due to local attractions and the famous Virupaksha temple. In 1986, adopted by the UNESCO World Heritage List. Currently, the excavations of the ancient settlement dating to the 3rd century BC. It is considered to be evidence that the area was densely populated before the creation Vidžajanagárské Empire. Worth a visit: Virupaksha Temple, the temple gopura, ceiling murals in the temple Virupasha, Stone Temple Small temple in Mantap Vitalli.

25.Varanasi
Varanasi is a city in India, in the state of Uttar Pradesh, located on the left bank of the Ganges, a sacred city for Hindus, Buddhists and Jains. According to local tradition, the city is considered one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities on Earth. The culture of the city is closely connected with the Ganges River and its religious importance. Varanasi has remained the cultural center of Northern India for many centuries. Here the musical style of Benares-gharana arose, from here many Indian philosophers, poets and writers lived or lived here. Here Tulsidas wrote his poem Ramacharitamanasa, in Gautam Buddha held his first sermon here.

26.Rishikesh
Rishikesh - the city and the community center in Dehradun County, State of Uttarakhand, India. The sacred river Ganges flows through Rishikesh. Along the banks of the Ganges there are few Hindu temples. The city attracts thousands of pilgrims and tourists from India and other countries each year. In Rishikesh there are many yoga centers, which attract a lot of followers of the spiritual discipline, earning the city the glory of the world capital of yoga. Also on the river rafting.

27.Shimla
Shimla - the capital of the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. Since 1864 the town was the summer capital of British India, and is now a popular tourist destination. The city is often referred to as the "Queen of Hills". It is located on the foothills of the Himalayas at an altitude of 2128 m above sea level. It is surrounded by pine, rhododendron and oak forests, the summer is very pleasant, but the winters are cold. The city is also known for its architecture in the style of the Tudor Revival and Gothic Revival style reminiscent of the colonial era buildings. The city is connected to the city of Kalka one of the longest narrow-gauge railways in India Kalka-Shimla Railway.

28.Ellora Caves
Ellora Caves is a settlement in the Indian state of Maharashtra, about 30 kilometers west of the city of Auranabad. Since 1983, the cave system and numerous castles are listed by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site. The creation of caves dates from about 6 to 9 centuries BC. e. From the 34 caves of Ellora, 12 caves in the south are Buddhist, 17 in the center are dedicated to Hinduism, 5 caves to the north are Jainism. One of the greatest of these is the Kailasanath temple. According to "ESBE", the most remarkable of the temples is "Kailash" (also known as "Kailasanath"), a magnificent, perfectly preserved specimen of Dravidian architecture, one of the most precious monuments of India.

Auroville should be a place of physical and spiritual research. Auroville is a place of continuous learning, continuous progress and unfading youth.

30.Humayun's Tomb
Humayun's Tomb - is the tomb of Emperor Humayun, located in Delhi in India. It is on the UNESCO World Heritage List. Architecturally it is the link between Gur Emir, where the grandfather Humayun Tamerlan is buried, and Taj Mahal, built by order of his great-grandson Shah Jahan. The construction of the mausoleum began in 1562 and ended 8 years later. Said Muhammad and his father Mirak Giyakhuddin are considered to be the architects, the project of which is obviously strongly influenced by the Samarkand constructions of the Timurids. Near the mausoleum are also: Isa Khan Complex, Afsalvala Mausoleum Complex, Saray Arabian Way, Goumpat.
