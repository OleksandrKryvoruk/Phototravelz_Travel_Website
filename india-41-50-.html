41.Jim Corbett National Park
Jim Corbett National Park is the oldest national park in India, named for the hunter and later the nature defender Jim Corbett. It is located in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand and serves as one of the conservation areas where the Bengal tiger population remains. In the park there are about 110 species of trees and 378 species of other plants, 50 species of mammals, 580 species of birds. The dense tropical rain forests of the park consist mainly of such species as sal, galdina, sacred ficus and mango, they cover about 73% of the park area.

42.Kanha National Park
Kanha National Park - one of the national parks of India, has the status of a tiger reserve since 1973. It is located in the districts of Mandla and Balaghad in the southeast of the state of Maharashtra. The nearest major city is Mandla, located 70 km from the park. The area of Kanha is 940 km2 and 1009 km2 is occupied by the buffer zone. The best time to visit the park is from February to June. It is believed that this area inspired Rudyard Kipling to write the "Jungle Book".
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Kanha National Park: Multi-day & Extended Tours
 

43.Periyar National Park
Periyar National Park is a national park in the Indian state of Kerala. The highest point of the territory is Mount Kottamalai (2019 m). In the park there is a picturesque lake Periyar (26 km2), created in 1895 in connection with the construction of a dam on the river Periyar. About 75% of the territory is covered by evergreen tropical forests. There are 62 species of mammals in the park, among them: tigers (a population of 53 individuals according to 2010 data), elephants (900-1000 individuals), gaurs, Indian zambaras, leopards, muntjacks, deer, mongoose, vander, Indian macaque There are also 320 bird species, 45 reptile species, 27 amphibian species, 38 fish species and about 160 butterfly species in the park.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Periyar National Park: Multi-day & Extended Tours
 

44.Jog Falls
Jog Falls is the largest waterfall in India and one of the biggest waterfalls on the planet. Located on the river Sharavati in the western part of Karnataka, near the mountain range of Western Ghat in southern India. The Gersoppa is a system of four cascades with a total height of 255 m. It is one of the twenty highest waterfalls in the world. Its height is about 253 meters. By this indicator, it is about 6 times higher than Niagara and 2.5 times higher than Victoria.
 
Where to stay Weather
 

45.Bogmalo Beach
Bogmalo Beach is a small beach in Goa, India. The beach is about 4 km (2.5 miles) from Dabolim Airport and 9 km (5.6 miles) from the port city of Vasco da Gama. It is located in a small bay, around which there is a kilometer-long sandy beach. The beach is known for its beautiful sunsets and the presence of about 5 cows. Boat trips to the islands and for detection of dolphins can be booked at the Full Moon restaurant in the southern part of the beach.
 
Where to stay Weather
 

46.São Jacinto Island
São Jacinto Island is an island in Goa, India. It is a clean, beautiful green zone and nature. Most of the island is represented by forests. Regular excursions are taking place on the island. You can visit: a lighthouse, local temples and forests. The island is closest to the southern shores of the estuary and is connected to the mainland via a dam. Travel agencies regularly organize various tours and excursions to the island. San Jacinto can be reached by a leased boat.
Where to stay Weather
 

47.Varkala Beach
By Nikolas Becker CC3
Varkala Beach is located about 50 km north of Trivandrum in India. Varkala was known for the popular beach of Papanasam or the beach of Varkala, Kovalam and the beach of Cheray. The center of the religious leader of Kerala, Sri Narayana Guru, is also located in this city and is considered a place of pilgrimage for his followers. The famous temple of Janardana Swami is located near the beach. Varkala Beach is known for its adventures and entertainment, such as boating, parasailing and horse riding. It is believed that the immersion in the waters of the beach of Papanasham washed away all the sins in life.

48.Dudhsagar Falls
Dudhsagar Falls - waterfalls located on the Mandovi River in the eastern part of the state in Goa, India. They are in Taluka (county). 310 meters in height, four levels and an average width of 30 meters - are among the highest waterfalls in India. The water volume fluctuates very much, during the monsoon rain from June to September, the cases are particularly watery, and from March to June, respectively, low in water. Waterfalls are located in the mountain range of Westgates. They are located in the wildlife sanctuary of Bhagwan Mahavir, just a few kilometers from the Kollem. Good accessibility by train also promotes tourism. In the surrounding park there are many plants and animals, and also attracts many visitors.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Dudhsagar Falls: Day Trips & Excursions
 

49.Rohtang Pass
Rohtang Pass is a mountain range in the Himalayas with an altitude of 3979 m. It is located in India, Himachal Pradesh. It is located about 50 km from Manali. On the south side of the pass, the river Bea emerges from under the earth and flows to the south. On the north side, the Chandra River, the tributary of Chenab, runs west from the eastern Himalayas. Since the spring of 2010, at an altitude of 3,100 m, a tunnel of 8.8 km in length with two lanes was built. The tunnel is integrated into the tunnel cross section below the main strip.

50.Gokak Falls
Gokak Falls - known as Niagara northern Mysore are located on the Ghataprabha river six kilometers from the city of the same name in the Belgaum area, India. Height 52 m, width 177 m. There is a suspension bridge across the river, and the waterfall itself is in the center of the picturesque gorge. Similar to the Niagara Falls, but, obviously, on a smaller scale. The leap has the shape of a horseshoe on a crest with a width of 177 m. During the rainy season, the reddish-brown water falls with a dull roar that can be heard from afar. There is a suspension bridge that crosses the river, covering a distance of 201 meters to a height on a rocky bed of 14 meters. There is an old power station that has been operating since 1887.
