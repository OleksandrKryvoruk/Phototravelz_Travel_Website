3.Orava Castle
Orava Castle is a castle in Slovakia, located on a 112-meter cliff above Orava, near the village of Oravski Podzamok not far from Dolnogo Kubina. It is included in the list of national cultural monuments of Slovakia. The castle was laid in the middle of the 13th century on the site of an old wooden fort. The last stone was laid in the XVII century. It was first mentioned in 1267. In 1370 he became a dense castle of Orava. In the 16th century a palace was built on the territory of the Orava Castle. In 1800 the castle burned down and was later rebuilt. After a complete reconstruction in 1953-1968, the castle housed an exhibition of the Orava Museum. Here, many scenes from an early film about Dracula were shot - "Nosferatu: Symphony of Terror". The castle is open to tourists for a whole year except January - March.

5.Trenčín Castle
Trenčín Castle is a castle located on a high hill above the center of Trenčín in western Slovakia. Currently in the castle is the Trenchinsky Museum. It is included in the list of national cultural monuments of Slovakia.

6.Bratislava Castle
Bratislava Castle is the central and most important castle in Bratislava, which for centuries has been an integral part of the city panorama. The monumental castle, reconstructed from the ruins in the second half of the 20th century, symbolizes more than a thousand years of Slovak history, is located on the southern spur of the Lesser Carpathians, on the cliff above the left bank of the Danube, at the crossroads of international roads. Hail - one of the main dominants of the city, is included in the list of national cultural monuments of Slovakia. On the territory of the palace there are expositions; and also the historical and partly archaeological section of the Slovak National Museum. In the city there are historical exhibitions containing valuable exhibits of people's history from antiquity to the present day (including valuable archaeological and numismatic collections), it is a public treasury of folk culture. Historical exhibitions tell about the development of material and spiritual culture, and about the social and political struggle in Slovakia. Since the time of feudalism, there are unique arts and crafts, documents on the development of cities and the life of the population.
