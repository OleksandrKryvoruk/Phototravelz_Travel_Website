Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Slovenia.

EUROPE > South Europe
1.Ljubljana
Ljubljana - the capital and largest city of Slovenia. In 2010, the city had 10 theaters, 14 museums, 56 galleries, 4 professional orchestras. The largest collection of historical and artistic value are kept at the National Museum (founded in 1821) and the National Gallery (founded in 1918). Among the other major cultural institutions: the Slovenian Philharmonic Symphony Orchestra and the Slovenian National Theatre Opera and Ballet. National and University Library of Slovenia - the largest scientific library of the country, was founded in 1774.

3.Triglav National Park
Triglav National Park is the only national park in Slovenia. Includes the summit of Triglav, is the national symbol of the country, and its environs, including the Mezhaklu Plateau. It has quite interesting architectural solutions - houses built of wooden boards. Also Pak has a museum in Old Fujin, caves, valleys, churches and a botanical garden.

4.Lake Bled
Lake Bled - lake in the Julian Alps in northwestern Slovenia, which is situated on the shore of the town of Bled. The lake is a popular place for tourism and is located at the foot of the plateau Pokljuka 35 km from the International Airport named after Jože pucnik and 55 km from the capital - Ljubljana. The lake has a mixed glacial and tectonic origin. It has a length of 2120 m and 1380 in width, with a maximum depth of 29.5 m. In the middle of Lake Bled is the island on which there are several buildings, the main one being piligrimska Church of the Assumption of the Virgin Mary. The lake is well known academic rower because it has very good conditions for this sport.

5.Postojna Cave
Postojna Cave - a system of karst caves 20,570 m long on the Kras plateau, is located near the town of Postojna in Slovenia in the region of Notranja-Krashka. The longest cave system in Slovenia and one of the most visited places in this country, since the XIX century is equipped with electric lighting and a railway. The cave was formed by the waters of the Pivka River, which flows through the cave for 800 m. The temperature is around 10 C throughout the year. 5.3 km of the cave is open for visiting - this is the largest length of cave space open for tourists to visit in the world.

6.Lake Bohinj
Lake Bohinj is the largest permanent and natural lake in Slovenia. Along the northern edge of the lake are more underwater karst springs, among which a famous waterfall. The lake can offer many hiking trails, landscape climbing, paragliding, swimming, rowing, sailing and scuba diving and triathlon.

7.Soča
Soča is a river that flows through western Slovenia (96 kilometers) and north-east Italy (43 kilometers). It originates from a karst spring at an altitude of 1100 m above sea level. A popular place for rafting with stunning nature.

8.Predjama Castle
Predjama Castle - a castle in Slovenia, which is located 9 kilometers from Postojna Caves. This 123-meter-high stone wall opens up a complex karst underworld. The entire underground system is about 6.5 km long and partly arranged for tourist visits.

9.Žička kartuzija
 
Žička kartuzija - is the name of the former Carthusian monastery in the hidden valley of St. John the Baptist in Slovenia. The monastery was founded around 1160. Today, the monastery belongs to the tourist attractions of this unique cultural and historical monument, which is visited annually by about 20 thousand visitors. At the entrance stands Gastuž, the oldest restaurant in the country. In the summer time on the territory of the monastery hosts various music events, including the most famous - a musical evening in Žiče Charterhouse. Also, the museum operates.
