41.Las Médulas
Las Médulas - mine is formed for the extraction of gold, located in the immediate vicinity of the homonymous locality, in the El – Bierzo in Spain. It is considered the largest gold mine in the Roman Empire. In 1997, he was added to the UNESCO World Heritage List. He was named one of the ten most spectacular natural places of Spain.

42.Aqueduct of Segovia
Aqueduct of Segovia - Aqueduct Roman times, the largest in Western Europe. Aqueduct is one of the main symbols of Segovia, and even depicted on the emblem of the city. In 1985 included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Built in the second half and on the verge of I and II century AD, that is, its age is almost two thousand years. The length of the arch over the ravine, and it is for this indicator segovia aqueduct keeps the championship, 728 meters. It consists of 162 arches. The total length of the water pipeline from the river to where the Sierra Guadarrama to Segovia 17 km.

43.Temple of Debod

Templo de Debod is the lowest in the current of the Nile and the ancient Egyptian temple closest to the high Aswan dam, transferred to the West Park of Madrid after it was presented to Spain by the Egyptian authorities in 1968 in gratitude for the help rendered in saving the temples of Abu Simbel from flooding during the construction of the Aswan Dam. 44.Casa Milà
Casa Milà is a house built in 1906-1910 in Barcelona in Spain by the architect Antoni Gaudi for the Mila family, one of the attractions of the Catalan capital. The building is located at the intersection of Passeig de Gràcia Boulevard with Carre de Provence Street. The construction of the Mila Cashier was not completed completely due to disputes with the investor. Despite this building is universally considered the best work of Gaudi, in which he fully realized the fruits of his experience. The building is part of the UNESCO World Heritage List.

45.Montserrat
Montserrat - mountain in Catalonia in Spain, 50 km from Barcelona in the direction of the Pyrenees. The height of the mountain 1236 meters, length 10 km, width 5 km. At the level of 720 meters above sea level on the hill is the famous Benedictine monastery of Montserrat mountain Monserrat. On laid a lot of hiking trails. Mountain slopes along the trails is the de facto open-air museum - they are decorated with all kinds of statues, memorial plaques and signs, majolica. Between the rocks growing forest. The flora of this nature corner covers more than 1250 species. The surface of the rock is covered with a special type of oak, yew trees and pines. The fauna is diverse, typical of the Mediterranean. It is home to swifts and their variety - royal swifts, pikas, marten, a rare species of eagles.

46.Sierra Nevada
Sierra Nevada - mountains in Spain, along the Mediterranean coast, the height is up to 3478 m (Mouassin is the highest peak of the Iberian Peninsula). Sierra Nevada is a popular tourist destination, because on its snow-covered slopes is the southernmost ski resort in Europe, and the Mediterranean climate gives warm weather and many sunny days. At the foot of the ridge is the city of Granada, and a little further - Malaga and Almeria. Some parts of the ridge are included in the national park, besides the whole ridge is included in the biosphere reserve. On the northern slope at an altitude of 2800 m is the Sierra Nevada Observatory.

47.El Teide
El Teide is a volcano on the island of Tenerife, Spain's highest point, also the third largest volcano in the world. The volcano is a part of Teide National Park. On the volcano there is a cable car, which is the highest in Spain. The lower station is located at an altitude of 2356 m above sea level, and the upper one is located on Teide volcano at an altitude of 3555 m. The cableway is equipped with two cabins designed for 44 passengers, and the lifting time is about 8 minutes.

49.Timanfaya National Park
Timanfaya National Park is a Spanish national park located on the island of Lanzarote, in the archipelago of the Canary Islands. It is located in the southwestern part of the island. It has an area of 51 km2 and was founded in 1974. The park is a volcanic area covered with vegetation. It has a hilly and mountainous character with a height of about 500 m. The color of the volcanic rocks resembles an area called Montagnas del Fuego, as well as the surface of Mars. In 1730, and then in 1824, a large volcanic eruption occurred. Lava covered about a third of the island.

50.Embalse de Guadalest
Embalse de Guadalest - a reservoir near the village of Guadalest, in the Valencia area in Spain. The reservoir receives water from the Guadalest River, the tributary of the Algar River. The reservoir is 0.86 km2 (86 ha), and the water masses that discharge have a volume of up to 16 million M3. The dam is 77 meters high and 285 meters long and is a gravity dam. Water is used for drinking water and agricultural purposes in and around Alfaz del Pi, Altea, Benidorm, Finestrat, Polop, La Nucia and Villajoyosa.
