Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Laos.

ASIA > South Asia
1.Luang Prabang
Luang Prabang - a city located in the northern part of Laos on the Mekong River. Until 1975 it was the capital of Laos. At the peak of its splendor the city in Louangphrabang were 66 Buddhist temples. Historical upheavals experienced only 32 of them. Phrabang Statue of Buddha - the most revered Buddha image in Laos. City events: January: Boun Khoun Khao - harvest festival, April: Boun Pimai - a three-day celebration of the New Year, May: Visakha Bone Bous'a - celebration of birth, death and enlightenment of Buddha, August: Bone Souang Heua - an important religious holiday of Agriculture .

2.Vientiane
Vientiane - the capital of Laos, located on the Mekong River, in the central-western part of the country, near the border with Thailand. Architectural attractions: Buddha Park, Haw Phra Kaew Temple, National Museum of Laos, patuxai, Buddhist Stupa Pha That Luang, That Dam, Wat Ong Teu Mahavihan, Simiang Wat, Wat Sisaket, Wat Sok Paluang. Vientiane has a mosque, several churches, as well as hotels, clubs and discos.

3.Wat Xieng Thong
Wat Xieng Thong is a Buddhist temple located in the city of Luang Prabang in Laos. It is located on the northern tip of the peninsula formed by the Mekong and Nam Khang rivers. The temple building is an embodiment of the classical temple architecture of Luang Prabang, when the roof slopes, gradually curving, descend almost to the ground. On the back wall of the structure are mosaics on a red background images of various birds and animals, imprinted in silver, lilac and green colors. Inside, in addition to the wall images on wooden pillars carrying the ceiling, there are gold images of dharmachakr, or "dharma wheel." Today this place is a tourist attraction.

4.Buddha Park
By GuillaumeG CC3
Buddha Park is a religious theme park located 25 km southeast of Vientiane, Laos, on the banks of the Mekong River. In the park there are more than 200 Buddhist and Hindu statues. One structure stands out especially against the others, it is essentially a three-story building, the floors symbolize hell, earth and paradise. The entrance to the building is the mouth in the 3-meter head of the demon. Visitors can go through all floors and see sculptural compositions corresponding to the subject matter.

5.Kuang Si Falls
Kuang Si Falls is 29 km south of Luang Prabang city, Laos. Quang Si is the most visited place for tourists coming to Luang Prabang. The main Kuang Si waterfall is a cascade of height 54 m, and the lower side of the cascade is 3 to 5 m in height.

6.Bolaven Plateau
By Satbir Singh СС2
Bolaven Plateau is a region in southern Laos, Champasak province. It is located at an altitude of 800 to 1200 meters. During the year, air temperature on the plateau is lower than other regions of the country, creating a favorable climate for agriculture. National reserve, impressive waterfalls, dense jungle, exotic tribes and coffee plantations make the region attractive for tourists. Tourists arrive in groups or individually with Pakse where you can rent a motorbike for a day trip.

7.Phu Chi Fa
Phu Chi Fa is a nature reserve in Thailand and Laos. It is located on the territory of two amphae Txoeong and Vuang-Kaen of Chiang Rai province. The forest reserve covers the middle of the Doi Pha Mon mountain range, which stretches along the border between Thailand and Laos. This area is located at an altitude of 1,200 - 1,628 meters above sea level. Vegetation Phu Chi Fa is represented throughout the area of ​​the reserve, except for the top of the mountain. Mostly grow: cannanoids, cinnamon, chimpaka magnolia, plums, moss, ferns. The fauna is diverse, here you can see such animals as wild boar, civet, deer, tigers, cane cat, cat Temminka, hares, porcupines, bats, squirrels, teledos, wool.

8.Vat Phou
By Ken Marshall СС2
Vat Phou - the ruins of the Khmer temple complex in southern Laos. The temple is located at the foot of Phu Kao Mountain, 6 km from the Mekong River, to the south of the city of Tyampatsak in the province of Tyampasak. As early as the 5th century there was a temple on the territory of the complex, but the structures that survived belong to the 11th-13th centuries. First, the temple complex was associated with Shivajistkimi cults, but then became the center of Theravada Buddhism, the temple still operates.

By Alexander Blecher CC4
