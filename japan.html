5.Sapporo
Sapporo is the fifth largest city in Japan, the largest city of Hokkaido. Winter sports resort, host of the Snow Festival and the 1972 Winter Olympics. It is surrounded by mountain ranges and, despite the demographic growth, has preserved numerous green areas both in the core of the city and on the outskirts; has a total of 2,700 parks, the Botanical Garden and Shikotsu Toyo National Park. Regularly in the city hold the stages of the World Cup in ski jumping and international hockey matches. In 2012, more than 12 million tourists arrived in the city, mainly from Japan, China, the United States and other East Asian countries.

6.Kyoto
Kyoto is a city in Japan, in the prefecture of Kyoto. In the city there are numerous monuments that have the status of a UNESCO World Heritage Site and national treasures of Japan - the Imperial Palace in Kyoto, Nijo Castle, Kiyomizu Monastery. In Kyoto, there are 1660 Buddhist monasteries and temples, and about 400 Shinto sanctuaries. In the city there are more than 2000 masterpieces of Japanese art, crafts and architecture, two hundred of which have the status of national treasures of Japan. Also in Kyoto there are about 120 historical and natural monuments, parks and species of national importance.
