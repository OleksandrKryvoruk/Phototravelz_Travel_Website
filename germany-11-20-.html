11.Berlin Wall
Berlin Wall - a system of fortifications with a length of about 156 km, which used to separate Berlin and Germany into two zones of influence. It was one of the most famous symbols of the Cold War and the division of Germany. The memorial complex "Berlin Wall", which occupies four hectares, was completed in 2012. The memorial is located on the street Bernauer Strasse, along which the border between the GDR and West Berlin passed. Part of the memorial complex "Berlin Wall" was the chapel of Reconciliation, built in 2000 on the foundation of the Church of Reconciliation, which was blown up in 1985.

12.Brandenburg Gate
Brandenburg Gate in the heart of the German capital, before the city gates, and today it is the most recognizable symbol of Berlin. The building is located on Parizer area. The Brandenburg Gate has a height of 26 m., Length of 65.5 m. And a width of 11 m. The gates are connected to many important events of German history.

13.Heidelberg
Heidelberg is a city in the land of Baden-Württemberg (Germany). Heidelberg is located on the left bank of the river Neckar, bordering the mountains of Königstuhl (568 m) and Gaisberg (375 m). The city is one of the most popular tourist places of the country. This is a world famous place - its ruins of the castle, the historic center of the city, but especially the University of Ruprecht-Carl in 1368 - the oldest university in modern Germany. Worth visiting: Old Bridge, Church of the Holy Spirit, Remains of medieval fortifications, Main Street, Church of Divine Providence, Baroque palaces and houses, Charles Gate, Ruins of the Gothic Renaissance Castle, Königstuhl mountains, Old Town Handschuhsheim, Zoo, Botanical Garden of Ruprecht University Karl, Kurpfaltsky Museum, Deutsches Apotheken, the museum castle, the Museum of Sacred Art.

14.Memorial to the Murdered Jews of Europe
Memorial to the Murdered Jews of Europe - a memorial in Berlin, Germany, established in memory of the Jews - victims of Nazism. The memorial is located in the center of Berlin between the Brandenburg Gate and the elements of the bunker of the former leadership of Nazi Germany. It represents a huge field of more than 2,700 gray slabs. Psychologist Collin Ellard, notes the peculiarity of the complex's architecture impact on the visitor. Passages between plates are made too narrow to walk on them alone, while remaining alone, the visitor begins to experience a complex range of feelings.

15.Reichstag
Reichstag - historical building in Berlin, where in 1894-1933 the seat of the same state of the body of Germany - the Reichstag of the German Empire and the Reichstag of the Weimar Republic. In 1945, in the Soviet Union, the building of the German parliament was declared the main symbol of Hitler's Germany. Today the Reichstag building is one of Berlin's tourist attractions. Currently, the dome is open for visitors by appointment in the Bundestag website. Also there are guided tours of the building, exhibitions, there is the possibility of visiting the meetings of the Bundestag. The German Bundestag is the most visited parliament in the world.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Reichstag: Day Trips & Excursions Shore Excursions Walking & Biking Tours
 

16.Mercedes-Benz Museum
Mercedes-Benz Museum - a car museum of the brand Mercedes-Benz, located in the city of Stuttgart, Germany. It is part of a whole complex located on the same territory, which includes: the headquarters of the concern Daimler AG; shop of retro and modern cars of Mercedes-Benz brand; Mercedes-Benz-Arena - home stadium of the football club "Stuttgart" and other facilities. One of the largest corporate museums in the world and the most visited museum of Stuttgart as of 2015.
Where to stay Weather Webcams Things to do in Stuttgart: Kid Friendly Shopping & Fashion Sightseeing Tickets & Passes Theme Parks Tours & Sightseeing Transfers & Ground Transport
 

17.Europa-Park
Europa-Park is the largest amusement park in Germany and the second most visited amusement park in Europe after Disneyland in Paris. It is located in the town of Rust in the federal state of Baden-Württemberg in the south-west of Germany. Numerous attractions are found in 16 thematic sections of the park, representing the 13 states of Europe: Holland, Russia, Spain, Switzerland, Austria, Germany, France, Italy, Greece, Portugal, Iceland, England and Scandinavian countries and 3 thematic sections - Enchanted Forest, Kingdom of Minibuts and the Children's World or the Land of the Vikings.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Europa-Park: Private & Custom Tours Sightseeing Tickets & Passes Transfers & Ground Transport
 

18.Sanssouci
Sanssouci - a palace in Potsdam, near Berlin, the former summer residence of the Prussian King Frederick II. The palace is often called the German rival of Versailles. Although Sanssouci built in the modest Rococo style and much less than his French rival, he is widely known for numerous temples and gardens. In 1990, Sanssouci, with its palaces and large park, was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. The Art Gallery of the Palace is one of the best German museums.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Potsdam: Cultural & Theme Tours Private & Custom Tours Tours & Sightseeing Transfers & Ground Transport Walking & Biking Tours
 

19.Hohenzollern Castle
Hohenzollern Castle is a castle not far from Stuttgart (Germany). The medieval castle fortress was first mentioned in 1267, but it is supposed that it was built earlier in the 11th century. Among the historical artifacts of Prussian history kept in the castle today, it is worth noting the crown of Wilhelm II, some of Friedrich the Great's personal belongings, as well as a letter from US President George Washington to Baron von Steuben, descendant of the Hohenzollern House, for his assistance in the US struggle for independence. Today the castle is a popular tourist attraction. The castle is visited annually by about 300 thousand tourists, making it one of the most visited castles in Germany.

20.Neuschwanstein Castle
Neuschwanstein Castle - Castle in the Allgäu (Bavaria, Germany) in the village of Schwangau near the town of Fussen. This is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Germany. Inside, the interiors are decorated with precious slots in the panels in the style of neo-romanticism, Neo-Gothic and historicism. Probably the most famous and the most beautiful rooms in the throne room of the palace, which was built in the likeness of the Hagia Sophia Mosque in Istanbul.
