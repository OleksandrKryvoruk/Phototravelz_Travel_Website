41.Mount Vesuvius
Mount Vesuvius is an active volcano in the south of Italy near Naples. The height of 1277 meters, the diameter of the crater is about 750 meters. One of the three active volcanoes of Italy, the only active volcano of mainland Europe. Known by the fact that during the Plinian eruption destroyed the Roman cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum in 79 AD. e. On the lower part of the slopes there are fruit orchards and vineyards, above, to a height of approximately 800 meters - pine groves. Currently, the mountain is open to visitors only on an equipped walking trail. From the foot of the volcano to the trail leads a road that ends with parking at an altitude of 1000 meters. Entrance to the trail is open from 9:00 to 15:00 from December to February, until 16:00 in March and October, until 17:00 in April-June and September, until 18:00 in July-August.

42.Trevi Fountain
Trevi Fountain - the largest fountain in Rome (Italy), with a height of 25.9 meters and 19.8 meters in width. There is a more "ambitious" fountain in Rome in the Baroque style. The majestic facade of the palace and the fountain are perceived as a single whole, and therefore the fountain seems even more grandiose. From the central niche of the palace, as on a chariot, the Ocean is riding, sitting on a sea shell, which is pulled by newts and hippocamps. In the niches on the sides of Neptune placed allegorical figures, and above them - bas-reliefs. On the right bas-relief the young girl indicates the Roman soldiers where the source is. From this source, an aqueduct was laid, along which water entered Rome, and the aqueduct, like the spring, was named Acqua Vergine (Acqua - water, Vergine - virgin).

43.Valle dei Templi
Valle dei Templi is an ancient Greek colony on the southern coast of Sicily in Italy. Now in its place is the modern Italian city of Agrigento. In 1997, the archaeological site of ancient Agrigent is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The archaeological zone includes the Temple of Hephaestus, Kolymbetra, the Syatilis of the chthonic deities and the temple of Dioscuri, Olympia, Theron's Tomb, the Temple of Asclepius, the Temple of Hercules, the Temple of Concord, the Temple of Hera, the Basilica, the Sanctuary of Demeter, the Demetric Temple, the Hellenistic and Roman Quarter, Museum, Ecclesiastry and oratory of Falari, Buleuterion, Temple of Athena, Temple of Zeus.

44.Lake Iseo
Lake Iseo - Italy's largest natural lake and the highest lake in Europe. The maximum depth of 251 m. It is located 180 m above sea level in the foothills of the Alps. The lake level is regulated by the dam Sarnico. The main activity is tourism. The tourist season from May to September, while there are many events: regattas, concerts and dances. They practiced a variety of sports: rowing, swimming, some surfing, the fishing, activities for diving and sailing. The lake is rich with trout and pike.

45.Scanno
Scanno - a commune in Italy, located in the region of Abruzzo, is subordinate to the administrative center of L'Aquila. Worth visiting: Lake Scanno, 10 km cross-country skiing track, Sarrakko fountain and Pisjarello fountain, Frattura Vecchia medieval village, Palazzo di Rienzo, Saint Giles chapel, Santa Maria della Valle church, dating from 1483, Rocco of Madonna del Carmine consecrated in 1661, the Church of Sant'Eustacio consecrated in 1693, the Palace of De Angelis, the Wool Museum and the Ju Catenacce Festival - the traditional annual costume parade of Scanno.

46.Villa Borghese gardens
Villa Borghese gardens is a Roman landscaped park in a natural English manner, occupying the hill of Pinch. It is the third largest public park in Rome in Italy (80 hectares or 148 acres) after the park of Villa Doria-Pamphili and Villa Ada. In the buildings scattered throughout the park are: Borghese Gallery with art collections of the princely family Borghese; National Museum Villa Julia with the largest collection of Etruscan art; National Gallery of Modern Art, which gives an idea of ​​all the major artistic trends of the XIX-XX centuries; The Globus Theater named after Silvano Toti, specializing in plays by W. Shakespeare; House-museum of Pietro Canon, sculptor, painter, composer (1869-1959); Museum Carlo Bilotti with works, mainly D. de Chirico, as well as temporary exhibitions of contemporary art.

47.Orta San Giulio
Orta San Giulio - the village is part of a chain of the most beautiful villages in Italy. The main part of the city is located on the lake in front of the island of San - Giulio. The center of Orta, completely pedestrian, is characterized by very picturesque narrow streets: the main one of which runs parallel to the shore of the lake and intersects with some steep streets that lead to the holy mountain of Sacro Monte (Unesco World Heritage). In the center of town is Piazza Motta - room overlooking the lake, from which boats depart to the island of San - Giulio.

48.Isola del Garda
Isola del Garda - is the largest island on the Italian lake Garda. Located 200 meters from the cape of San Fermo (Capo San Fermo), which separates the bay of Salo from the bay of Smeraldo. Dimensions of the island of Garda reach a kilometer in length and an average of 600 meters in width. To the south of the island lies a chain of reefs and shoals, at the end of which there is the island of San Biagio, also known as the island of rabbits. The island has the Monastery of Longobardo. Tourists can get to the ferry from Salo, Barbarano, Portese, Gardone Riviera, Bardolino and Garda, accompanied by excursions around the island and the palace in Italian, English and German.

49.Favrio
Favrio is a village near the town of Fiago and is located on the northern slopes of Mount Misone. Its geographical location means that in some winter months, the sun does not illuminate the village for a whole day. On February 3, when the sun returns to light Favrio, the village festival takes place. Inside the village there is the church of San Biagio, built in the sixteenth century. There are also many rural houses typical of Giudicarie Esteriori.
Where to stay Weather
 

50.Castello scaligero
Castello scaligero (Sirmione) - one of the most complete and well-preserved castles in Italy, as well as a rare example of a lake fortification. The castle is bathed on all sides by the waters of Lake Garda. Today the castle functions as a museum and has collection Roman and medieval objects. Legend has it that even today, you can see the soul of a man who wanders the castle. Included in the 25 most visited sites in Italy.
