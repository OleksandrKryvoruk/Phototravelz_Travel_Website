1.Cairo
Cairo - Egypt's capital and largest city of the Middle East. Old Town is located on the east bank of the river and is a World Heritage Site, where you can see a lot of old houses, mosques and the old tower. The city has many monuments: Amr Mosque (641 years), Ibn Tulun Mosque (876 years), Minaret (1468), the mausoleum of Al-Shafi'i (1211), a mausoleum and madrasa of Sultan Hassan (1356), the mosque of Mohamed Ali (1830). There are also many museums: the Egyptian Museum, Cairo Children's Museum, Museum of Islamic Art, the Ethnographic Museum, Military Museum. Also worth visiting the Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx, Giza Zoo, Botanical Gardens, in the city there are about 9 parks.

3.Sharm El-Sheikh
Sharm el-Sheikh - a resort town on the Red Sea coast, Egypt. The city has more than 200 hotels in different price categories. Almost all the hotels have swimming pools. Local beaches are well equipped and offer snorkeling, scuba diving, water skiing, scooters, boats and boating with a transparent bottom. The main attractions include: the Sinai Mountains National Park Ras Mohamed National Park Nabq, Old Town entertainment complex 1001 nights, Waterpark, Cleo Park and Dolphinarium.

5.Alexandria
Alexandria - the second largest city of Egypt, and the sea port in the Nile Delta. The city founded by Alexander the Great in 332 BC One of the most famous and important monuments of ancient culture. Main attractions: The ruins of ancient Alexandria, the Khedive's Palace, the Palace of Ras el-Tin, Fort Kite Bay, Mosque El-Mursi Abul Abbas, Alexandria National Museum, Pompey's Pillar, Sadat Museum and the Lighthouse of Alexandria - one of the 7 wonders of the world (though he destroyed the Egyptian authorities of its plan to rebuild). In the city a lot of beaches, hotels and resorts.

10.Great Sphinx of Giza
The Great Sphinx is the most ancient monumental sculpture in the world. Length 72 m, height 20 m. Located on the west bank of the Nile River in Giza near modern Cairo, Egypt. It is carved from a monolithic calcareous rock in the shape of a large lion with a human head - a sphinx lying on the sand. It is believed that his face is the image of the pharaoh of the Fourth Dynasty, the Old Kingdom (circa 2558-2532 BC) - Khafra, whose pyramid is nearby. Dimensions Great Sphinx: length 73,5; width 19.3; height of 20.22 m; Between the forelegs there was a sanctuary.
