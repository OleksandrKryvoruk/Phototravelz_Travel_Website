1.Horseshoe Bay
By Jeremy T. Hetzel CC2
Horseshoe Bay is the most popular beach in Bermuda. Corals and sand on the beach have a pink color. It is within walking distance of public transport links, you can also rent a moped or taxi to get there. It is included in the top 10 beaches in the world. All the equipment needed for entertainment is rented on the beach of Horseshoe Bay. Children can go boogie boarding and beach volleyball.

2.Crystal Cave
By Andrew Malone CC2
Crystal Cave is the most famous of many underground caves in Bermuda. It is located in the parish of Hamilton, near the castle - Harbor. The cave has a length of about 500 m and a depth of 62 m. The tourist attraction since 1907 was opened in 1905 by Karl Gibbons and Hollis Edgar, two 12-year-old boys who were searching for the ball. The cave was formed above sea level, and as the sea level rises, many speleothems formed in the air are now below the water level.

3.Church Bay
By Craig Stanfill CC2
Church Bay - is the most popular snorkeling beach in Bermuda. The reef is near the shore and has many colorful fish. It is located in the Gulf of Jerusalem from the southern road in the suburb of Southampton on the main island. He was always one of our best favorites for scuba diving. The beach area is very small and practically disappears during the tide. The beach park is on top of a small hill.

4.Bermuda Aquarium
By Garden State Hiker CC2
Bermuda Aquarium (Bamz) - located in Flatsom village in Bermuda, focuses on showing species of animal oceanic islands and scientific research related to these species, and contains an aquarium, a natural history museum and a zoo. The aquarium contains more than 200 species of fish and invertebrates. Seals and green sea turtles are placed on open exhibits. At the exhibition "Friends of Madagascar" there are animals from Madagascar. Local Tails show animals that are native to Bermuda.

5.Bermuda Botanical Gardens
By slgckgc CC2  
Bermuda Botanical Gardens - 36 acres of flowers, bushes and trees. A short drive from the center of Hamilton, Bermuda. The largest local public garden. Steel sculpture in front of the Museum of Art Masterworks honors John Lennon, who visited the gardens in 1980 and named his album "Double Fantasy" after he saw a flower here.

By schnaars CC2
Mid Ocean Club is a private 18-hole golf course in Tucker, Bermuda. Mid Ocean Club attended George Bush, Winston Churchill, Dwight Eisenhower, and the Duke of Windsor. Located in the scenic Tucker district of Bermuda, it offers unrivaled opportunities for the international community of members and their guests. Club hotel overlooking the 18th fairway has a dining room, a bar, two private rooms for work, a locker room for men and women and a golf shop. The beach pavilion, located on the beautiful South Coast, with direct access to the beach, offers its guests and guests a swimming pool.

7.Admiralty House Park
By Peter Burka CC2
Admiralty House Park is a public park with a small beach and steep cliffs, about two miles from Hamilton's capital, Bermuda. Behind it is a panoramic view of the North Shore at the shipyard in the west and St. George in the east. The shallow beach of Clarence Cove has a dock in which local children like to dive. Another beautiful water area is Deep Bay, located to the east of the bay on the northern shore and popular for swimming. Nearby is the cave of the admiral.

8.Royal Naval Dockyard
By mike.viscardi CC2
Royal Naval Dockyard is a city in the municipality of Sandys, in Bermuda. In the past, the naval military port of the country's navy. It is also the place for the only prison in Bermuda. Currently the port for cruise ships. The retention area is now part of the Bermuda Maritime Museum. To serve visitors, several former warehouses were turned into art shops, and a pedestrian center opened in the tower of the clock.

10.Moongate Lane
Moongate Lane - Chinese lunar gate establishment in Bermuda in the late 19th century. A popular place among newlyweds, it is believed that the couple who pass through the gates will gain eternal happiness and luck. The Moon Gate is a traditional architectural element of Chinese gardens with a multitude of spiritual meanings for each part of the gate and its shape. The purpose of these doors is to serve as a very attractive entrance to the gardens of the rich upper classes in China.
