11.Abel Tasman National Park
Abel Tasman National Park is a national park located in the far north of the South Island, in the southern part of Golden Bay Bay in New Zealand. Abel-Tasman is famous for its sandy beaches with long spits, stone outcrops and preserved estuaries of untouched rivers. At a distance from the shore, the altitude rises to a kilometer. Here is located one of the "Great Routes" - Coastal route "Abel-Tasman", walking along the forest more often and along the coast; the length of the route is 51 km, its passage takes from three to five days and does not require special preparation. In addition, tourists can sail, on boats and kayaks, as well as beach rest Totharanui - a beach, one kilometer long is located Abel-Tasmania. There are also a number of private cottages, hostels and hotels.

12.Nelson
Nelson is a city and a one-tier region small in territory in New Zealand, located in the north of the South Island on the south-eastern shore of the Tasman Bay. This is a good starting point for the nearby three national parks and caves. National parks Abel Tasman National Park, Nelson Lakes National Park and Kahurangi National Park. One of the attractions of the city is the World of WearableArt. Two arts festivals and a jazz festival, which are also held annually, are another attraction of the city.

13.Napier
By Robyn Gallagher CC2
Napier is a city in New Zealand, located on the west coast of the North Island, the center of the Hawkes Bay area. The city is located on the southern shore of Hawk Bay, 320 km northeast of the capital city of Wellington. The city is part of the Art Deco Foundation, which promotes this style of art. Particularly noteworthy is the Masonic Hotel, the Criterion Hotel, the daily telegraph building, the nationwide bank, the A & B building, whose dome became the landmark of Napier, and the theater with its Egyptian columns and arches.

14.Wakatipu
Wakatipu is a lake in the Otago region on the South Island in New Zealand. The length is 80 km, the deepest point is 420 m. The longest lake in New Zealand and the third in terms of area. The shape of the lake resembles the letter S. On the shore of the lake is Mount Cecil Peak. The area around the lake is the center of extreme sports, such as bungee jumping. There are also extensive ski resorts, hiking trails around the lake and the old steamer, TSS Earnslaw. Lake Wakatipu - station of the southern scenic route.
Where to stay Weather Webcams Things to do in Wakatipu: Classes & Workshops Cruises, Sailing & Water Tours Day Trips & Excursions Food, Wine & Nightlife Private & Custom Tours
 

15.Franz Josef Glacier
Franz Josef Glacier is a glacier located on the Westland National Park in the west of the South Island of New Zealand. In turn, part of the national park is part of the World Heritage site of Te Wahipounamu. Along with the Fox glacier 20 km to the south, this glacier is unique in that it descends to a height of only 240 m above sea level, passing through a moderately moist forest around it. From the point where the glacier ends, the Vaihe River begins. The length of the glacier is 12 km.

16.Fox Glacier
Fox Glacier - a glacier in the south of New Zealand is located in the Westland National Park. The place received its name in honor of Prime Minister William Fox. Every day the glacier slips by 40 cm due to global warming. It is not difficult for tourists. Near the glacier is Franz Josef. Its final stop is 5 km from the village of Veheka. It is a major tourist attraction, which takes about 1000 people a day.

17.Kepler Track
Kepler Track - represents three or four days back and forth in the national park of the fjords and passes through the southern peninsula of New Zealand. The track is protected by the Department of Conservation of Nature of New Zealand. This is a moderate walkway, which lasts from three to four days. Every year there is a marathon along the entire length of the track (60 km). The maximum elevation of the zone is the peak of Lake Victoria, a height of 1710 meters at sea level. There are many places for camping.

18.Mount Aspiring National Park
Mount Aspiring National Park is a national park located in the Southern Alps of the South Island in New Zealand. The park is named after the second highest mountain in the country - Aspiring. Mount Aspiring was founded in 1964, it is the tenth National Park of New Zealand. It covers an area of 3,555 km2. It belongs to the group "Te Vahipunamu" - four national parks of New Zealand, inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. It is popular for hiking, hiking and mountaineering.

19.Waiotapu
By Eli Duke CC2  
Waiotapu is an area with high geothermal activity in the south of the Okataine volcanic center, north of the Reporoa caldera, in the Taupo volcanic zone of the North Island of New Zealand. In this area there are many picturesque hot springs, as well as the Lady Knox geyser. Prior to the European occupation, the Ngati Vaoa tribe residing on this territory traveled on their arava and waka canoes. Since ancient times, the area of ​​Wai-O-Tapu is popular among tourists. Since 1931 the territory is under protection, but in part it remains a tourist attraction, operating under the name Waiotapu Thermal Wonderland.

20.Coronet
Coronet Peak is the peak and winter sports resort located in Otago, 18 km northeast of the city of Queenstown and seven kilometers west of Arrowtown, New Zealand. Height 1649 m. A popular ski resort in the southern hemisphere, Coronet Peak offers a large ski season, good snow and ski lifts. The mountain is a place for training world-class athletes, also there are ski races.
