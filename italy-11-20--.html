11.Vatican City
Vatican City is a small sovereign state. Built on the west bank of the Tiber River in the heart of Rome. In the Vatican are world-famous masterpieces of architecture - St. Peter's Cathedral with interior decoration, Vatican museums, including the Sistine Chapel and others, as well as the famous Vatican Library. The largest work of art are the frescoes of the Sistine Chapel of Michelangelo's work. For centuries, a huge art collection was collected, which is now on display in the Vatican Museums.

12.Capri
Capri - an island in the Tyrrhenian Sea (part of the Mediterranean Sea), part of the Italian province of Naples in the region of Campania. A popular seaside resort since the time of the Roman Republic. The main city is the eponymous settlement - Capri, it is adjoined by two harbors - Marina-Piccola and Marina Grande. Another significant city is Anacapri. On the north coast is one of the most popular attractions of the island - the Blue Grotto. From Anakapri to the mountain leads a cable car. On the mountain you can climb the "Phoenician ladder" (921 steps), presumably dated VII-VI centuries BC. e. From its top you can see the view of Naples, the bay and the southern extremity of the Apennines.

13.Cinque Terre
Cinque Terre is a name for five villages in the Ligurian coast of Italy: Monterosso al Mare, Vernazza, Corniglia, Manarola and Riomaggiore. It is one of the most picturesque and famous Mediterranean coasts. It is an object of UNESCO. Wine, the famous Cinque Terre product, you can also taste typical Ligurian dishes. In Cinque Terre, the shore is rocky and steep, with stone or pebble beaches, except for the sandy beach in Monterosso. In the villages preserved medieval defenses from pirates. Manarola and Riomaggiore connects the picturesque Road of Love.

14.Amalfi Coast
Amalfi Coast - the south coast of the peninsula Sorrentina  in Italy, which forms the southern part of the Naples Gulf. The main settlement on the coast - Salerno. Coast due to its rugged terrain, beauty and diversity of settlements, since 1997 part of the UNESCO World Heritage List. Some communities, such as Amalfi and Ravello are the architectural and artistic works of great significance. The most beautiful coasts: Salerno, Positano, Atrani, Villa Cimbrone Ravello, Praiano, Conca Bay to the entrance to the Emerald Cave.

15.San Marino
San Marino is a country in Europe with an area of 61.2 square kilometers. San Marino is one of the oldest republics in the world and is the fifth smallest country in the world. The historic center of San Marino and Monte Titano since July 2008 are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Attractions: medieval houses, squares and fortifications, La Rocca Guaita Fortress, Museum of ancient weapons, Piazza Della Libertà, City Hall facade is decorated.

16.Sardinia
Sardinia - an island in the Mediterranean Sea, located to the west of the Apennine peninsula between Sicily and Corsica, is the second largest island of the Mediterranean Sea. It is part of Italy. One of the most important sights of the island is the archeological monument of Su-Nurakshi in Barumini, which since 1997 is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Other attractions: The Tomb of the Giants (more than 300 monuments of this type have been identified), the Domus de Janas (prehistoric graves of Carbonia), the Tuvikeneddu Necropolis (3rd-6th century BC) In Cagliari, the Roman Amphitheater in Cagliari (2 in .), The Sardinian ziggurat in Sassari, the Grotto of Cala Gonone (Dorgali), the Grotto of Neptune near Alghero.

17.Sicily
Sicily - the largest island in the Mediterranean Sea in Italy. Capital - Palermo. On the island there is: Mount Etna (the highest volcano in Europe) and Mount Rocca Novara. Regional natural parks: Alcantara, Etna, Nebrodi, Madonie, Makalube di Aragon. The island is rapidly developing tourism industry, which contributes to an attractive warm climate, sea beaches and many historical monuments. Most tourism on the island has a province of Messina, which is annually visited by 5 million tourists. The island has many UNESCO World Heritage sites: Valley of the Temples in Akraganta, Villa Romana del Casale, the Aeolian Islands, the Necropolis of Pantalica, together with the city of Syracuse. The outstanding architectural monuments in Sicily built in ancient times, among them Syracuse Cathedral, Cathedral San Nicolo, Noto, Fountain Vergon in Palermo, Piazza Duomo in Catania Castello Ursino in Catania, Castle of Erice. Sicilian cuisine belongs to the oldest and richest in the diversity of dishes of regional cuisine of Italy.

18.Siena
Siena is a city and municipality in Italy, in the Tuscany region, the capital of the province of Siena. In the Middle Ages - the capital of the Siena Republic, the focus of the artistic life of the Trecento era. The well-preserved medieval center of Siena is protected by UNESCO as a World Heritage site. The Siena Cathedral (Duomo) is a generally recognized standard of Italian Gothic. Many of the works of the Renaissance can be seen directly in the Siena churches and palazzo, as well as in the rich collections of art museums of the city, such as the Siena Pinakothek. In the vicinity of Siena, in the Chianti region, Italy's most famous wine is produced, which is called Chianti.

19.Syracuse
Syracuse is a city and municipality in Italy, in the Sicily region, the capital of the province of Syracuse. Built on the site of the main city of ancient Sicily - Syracuse (ruins - a monument to the World Heritage). The city was founded as an ancient colony of Greek Dorians from Corinth in 733 BC. on the site of an earlier settlement. In Syracuse there are many prehistoric and historical sights from antiquity to baroque. Two main ensembles are in the archaeological park and on the island of Ortigia. Worth visiting: Temple of Apollo, Temple of Santa Lucia Alla-Badia, Greek Theater, Fonte Aretusa, Archaeological Museum of Paolo Orsi and Museo Bellomo.

20.Portofino
By Pierre-Selim Huard CC4
Portofino is a municipality in Italy, in the Liguria region. Portofino is located about 380 km south-west of Rome, 25 km southeast of Genoa. One of the most picturesque Mediterranean ports. Part of the Italian Riviera. It is worth visiting the Church of San Giorgio (owned by Salita San Giorgio), which is said to be the remains of St. George, the lighthouse, Parco Naturale di Monte di Portofino, a landscape park comprising a hill above the city, Castello Brown 1557 and the Underwater Statue Il Cristo degli Abissi.
