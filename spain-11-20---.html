11.Granada
Granada is a municipality in the province of Granada in Spain. Granada is known for many architectural monuments, inherited from the Moorish era, as well as Gothic and Renaissance. In addition, the city is famous for its workshops and guild manufacturers. Granada Attractions: The Alhambra, mosques and palaces, Generalife Palace, Albayzín or Albaicín - the old Arab quarter, Medrese, Federico García Lorca Park, Castril Palace. The Alhambra and Albaicin (an ancient residential quarter of the Moors) are now recognized as part of UNESCO's world heritage. In addition, in Granada there is an archaeological museum, an observatory and a science park.

12.Canary Islands
Canary Islands - a group of volcanic islands in the Atlantic Ocean in Spain, located off the northwest coast of Africa. Island Attractions - pyramid in Gyuimar and the highest peak of Spain - the Teide (3718 m). On the island of La Palmayavlyaetsya Observatory of the Northern Hemisphere (1981, conditions for observing the extremely favorable - no artificial light, because there is no mist). Canar climate is tropical, temperate hot and dry. The northern part of the archipelago moist south - dry. On the islands there are several national parks, many of which are protected by UNESCO: Caldera de Taburiente National Park Garajonay National Park Timanfaya, Teide National Park. Interesting museums: Nature and Man Museum, Museo Canario, the Archaeological Museum of Puerto - de - la - Cruz, Tenerife's Museum of Anthropology, the Museum of Science and Space. Also, events are: Festival of El Hierro, Teguise Lanzarote Holiday Diabletes, Carnival Las - Palmas, Carnival of Santa - Cruz - de - Tenerife.

13.Ibiza
Ibiza - the island located in the Mediterranean Sea and is part of the Autonomous Region of the Balearic Islands, Spain. The capital of the island of Ibiza -City. Two major settlements outside the capital - Sant Antoni and Santa gave Aulaliya-Rio. Today, the reference point for young people around the world with its lively nightlife. The island also offers numerous coves and beaches for those seeking tranquility. Worth a visit: Ibiza Cathedral, Necropolis of Puig des Molins, clubs and discos as Privilege, Eden, Es Paradis, Amnesia, Space, Pacha, Gala Night, Ushuaïa Ibiza.

14.San Sebastian
San Sebastian - city and municipality in the north of Spain, in the Basque Country. The city is located on the coast of the Bay of Biscay and in the immediate vicinity of the border with France. San Sebastian is one of the most important tourist destinations for Spaniards and for foreigners. British publication The Guardian chose San Sebastian as one of the top five cities in the world for summer holidays. Bay in the shape of a shell and beaches on its banks La Concha - the main attraction of San Sebastian.

15.Costa Blanca
By Rodriguillo CC3
Costa Blanca - a strip of the Mediterranean coast in Spain, the province of Alicante, the autonomous community of Valencia. Tourism and recreation area. The average air temperature in the winter in the south of the region is 19 C, in summer 31 C, the water temperature is 13 C and 30 C. Costa Blanca is visited annually by about 6 million tourists, local hotels are able to accommodate more than 150 thousand people. The resort capital is the city of Benidorm with the beaches of Playa de Levante and Playa de Poniente. In 2000 Terra Mitica was opened, more than 1 million people visited it in one season.

16.Costa Brava
Costa Brava is a strip of the Mediterranean coast in the northeast of Catalonia, in the province of Girona, as part of the Spanish kingdom. The Costa Brava zone includes the territories of the Alt-Emporda and Bash-Ampurda areas. Extends 162 km from the town of Blanes to the border with France. The most visited beach in this region of Spain is because of the sandy beaches and proximity to the rest of Europe. The most famous resorts are: Ampuria-Brava, Blanes, Cadaques, Lloret de Mar, Palamos, Playa de Aro, Portbou, Puerto de la Selva, Rosas, Sagaro, Santa Cristina de Aro, Feliu de Guixols, Tossa de Mar.

17.Costa del Sol
By Konstantin CC3
Costa del Sol is a region of southern Spain, located in the autonomous community of Andalusia and includes the coastal towns of the province of Malaga. Thanks to a mild warm climate with an average annual temperature of 19 C, Costa del Sol has gained popularity as a tourist destination in Spain, accounting for 35% of Andalusia's tourism business. Costa del Sol - the southernmost Mediterranean resort of the country. Costa del Sol is a popular tourist region that receives millions of tourists every year. Usually they arrive at the airport and port of Malaga and then follow in numerous resort towns and villages located along the coastline from Gibraltar in the west to Nerja in the east.

18.Camp Nou
Camp Nou - stadium of FC Barcelona. The capacity of the Camp Nou is 99,354 spectators; This is the largest stadium in terms of capacity, not only in Spain, but throughout Europe. Repeatedly on the field were held the finals of the largest European Cup tournaments. In addition, the stadium is often used as a concert venue. There is a museum at the stadium, where you can see the numerous awards won by the clubs Barcelona (both football and basketball), with documents, videos, photographs and other materials, as well as go down to the stadium's locker room and visit the commentary booths. The museum is very popular with tourists visiting Barcelona.

19.Santiago Bernabeu
Santiago Bernabeu - the football stadium in Madrid, the second largest stadium in Spain, the home arena of the Real Madrid football club. It has the status of a five-star on the UEFA scale. The stadium, built in 1947, was later named after the president of Real Madrid, Santiago Bernabéu, during the reign of which the club won six European Champions Cups and many domestic trophies. "Santiago Bernabéu" hosted the finals of the European Championship in 1964 and the 1982 World Cup.

20.Tibidabo
Tibidabo is a mountain in the territory of the municipality of Barcelona (Spain). Tibidabo is part of the Collserola mountain range. The height above sea level is 512 m, it is the highest point of the city and the whole range. The mountain is popular with tourists, as it reveals the view of Barcelona. Nearby, on the mountain of Pico de la Vilana, towers Collserola towers at a height of 288 m. The mountain is the end point of the famous tourist route "Blue Tram (1276 m) - Funicular Tibidabo (1130 m) - Tibidabo", starting in the city center at 7 line of the Barcelona Metro. On the slopes of the mountain there is the Tibidabo Park with an observation tower and attractions. A temple of the Sacred Heart is built on top.
