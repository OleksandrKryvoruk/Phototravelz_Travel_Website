Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Kenya.

AFRICA > Central Africa
1.Nairobi
Nairobi is the capital of Kenya and the largest city in East Africa. The main attractions of the city include: Nairobi National Park (400 species of animals), the National Museum of Kenya (the main exhibit of homo erectus - the remains of a boy who died almost 2 million years ago), Karen Blixen Museum, Nairobi Railway Museum and Africa's largest ice rink Solar Eis Rink.

2.Lake Nakuru
By Nikkolo CC1
Lake Nakuru is one of the lakes located along the Great Rift Valley in Kenya, 156 km from the capital of Nairobi. Its area is about 40 km2, and the maximum depth is about 3 m. Nakuru is a high mountain lake, it is located at an altitude of 1759 m above sea level. It is fed by two large rivers - Nyiro and Nderit. "Nakuru" in Masai language means "dusty place". Lake Nakuru is super salty because of the high content of carbonates and bicarbonates in the water, so only rare plants and living things can be found that can adapt to such conditions: a few algae, several zooplankton species, small crustaceans and just one species of fish. Surprisingly, this seemingly meager range of aquatic flora and fauna attracts countless birds to the lake, the most notable of which are small flamingos.

3.Maasai Mara
Maasai Mara National Reserve is located in the southwest of Kenya in the Great Rift Valley at 275 from Nairobi, at an altitude of 1650 meters above sea level. The park borders the Serengeti National Park of Tanzania in the south, with the slopes of Syria in the west and the Masai villages in the north, east and west. Arrows and ceramics of the Neolithic Age (about 2000 years ago) were found in the Masai Mara Park. Masai occupy the territory of a modern park from the 17th century - of course, along with wild animals, which in fact are the true masters of the park. Masai are a people who have preserved their way of life and traditions, although they live outside the reserve, but on deductions from park fees (20%). Near the park you can visit the maniata - Masai village of shepherds-nomads: to inspect the housing, to communicate with the population. Accept hospitably - additional earnings (photos, souvenirs, archery for money, etc.) Maasai themselves can hunt in the territory of the national park. The entire territory of the park can be seen from a balloon, but this is not a cheap pleasure.

4.Amboseli National Park
Amboseli National Park - a park in the south-eastern Kenya. The most visited national park in Kenya. The park is home to 400 species of birds and 80 species of animals. The park is home to 900 elephants and rhinos can be seen, lions, cheetah, giraffe, zebra, antelope and buffalo.

5.Diani Beach
By Łukasz Ciesielski CC3
Diani Beach is about 25 km long, a strip of white sandy beach in the Indian Ocean in Kenya. It is located about 35 kilometers south of Mombasa. Diani is a tourist center of the southern coast.

6.Tsavo
Tsavo - one of the oldest and largest national parks in Kenya, as well as one of the world's largest parks. Relief is represented by shrouds. In the park live leopards, lions, giraffes, ostriches, hyenas, impalas, zebras, elephants, buffalo and over 500 species of birds.

7.Thomson's Falls
Thomson's Falls is a 74-meter waterfall on the Evaso-Ngiro River in central Kenya, near the town of Nyahururu. The water is at an altitude of 2360 m in the Aberdare mountains. The name of the waterfall comes from the name of the discoverer, who was Joseph Thomson.

9.Kit-Mikayi
Kit-Mikayi is a 40 m high rock in western Kenya. The legend says that the rock was the place of sacrifice of local tribes. This agglomerate is part of the weeping rocks that appear in this region. Although the site is not an area protected by the Kenyan government, it is the most sacred site in the Lo tradition, which ensures its preservation. The place is also a place of pilgrimage for the followers of the religious movement "Legio Maria", which leaves a permanent custodian there.
