1.Madrid
Madrid - is the capital of Spain. Madrid is the historical capital of the Spanish Empire and the current political and cultural center of Spain. Its most famous attractions are the Royal Palace in Madrid, the Plaza Mayor, Puerta del Sol, Paseo del Prado, Encarnación Monastery, the Cathedral of Santa Maria la Real de la Almudena, the Battle Arena bulls "Las Ventas", Royal Theater (Royal Theater in the center), Buen Retiro Park (founded in 1631), the National Library of Spain (established in 1712), the world-famous archaeological museum and three art museums of the first class.

2.Barcelona
Barcelona - is a large city in the northeast of Spain on the Mediterranean Sea. The city is famous for its numerous art collections, for example, Picasso and buildings designed by architect Antonio Gaudi. It is the largest city on the Mediterranean coast. Today Barcelona is one of the most attractive cities for tourism in Europe and has a good quality of life. Barcelona has nine buildings from the UNESCO World Heritage List. Local beaches are one of the best in the world.

4.Seville
Seville - a city in southern Spain. According to legend, it was founded by the mythical Greek hero Heracles. Seville Architecture contains various architectural styles: Moorish (the so-called Mudejar style), Gothic, Renaissance, Baroque. Worth a visit: Torre del Oro, Seville Cathedral, Alcazar Palace, Plaza de España. Museums: Museum of Fine Arts, the Archaeological Museum, the Museum of folk costumes, Flamenco Museum, Maritime Museum. Seville bullring Maestranza is an oval amphitheater in which 18 thousand people fit, and is the second largest arena in Spain after Madrid.

6.Málaga
Málaga is a city in the south of Spain. Malaga has many attractions from different historical periods. There are remnants of the Roman theater and amphitheater. The city has preserved the city walls and the fortress of the Alcazaba. Interesting house and museum of Pablo Picasso. Tourists today still most attract beaches on the Costa del Sol. The city is famous for its numerous festivals. A popular walk up the slopes to the castle of Gibralfaro, from which it is good to view the views of the city. The castle is located near the Moorish fortress of Alcazaba, from which it is very near the center of Malaga. The path will run through Paseo Park (an alley that stretches along the park, decorated with palm trees and sculptures) to the bay, ending with the main shopping street of the city - Calle Larios.

7.Majorca
Majorca is a Spanish island, is the largest island of the Balearic Islands. The capital and the largest city of Palma de Mallorca. An important factor, turned Mallorca into one of the most attractive corners of the globe, is the amazingly rich and diverse nature of the island. In the Serra da Tramuntana mountains (the highest mountain is Puig-Major, 1445 m) you can enjoy typical mountain landscapes. The northern part of Mallorca, where for 100 km stretches a mountain range, known for its beautiful beaches. The southern part of Mallorca is hotter and drier, here are the most beautiful beaches of the island: Ses Covetes, Es Trenca, Mondragó, Figera bay and Santanyi bay.

Urban beaches from north to south: San - Juan, La - Almadraba, Albufereta, Postiguet, Agua Amarga, el Saladar, and Tabarka.
