41.Lake Tōya
Lake Tōya - volcanic lake in Shikotsu-Toya National Park. The lake is almost circular, its diameter is 10 km from east to west and 9 km from north to south. The largest city on the lake, Toyakoonsen. On the southern edge of the caldera is a volcano. On the island of Naka Shima, located in the middle of the lake, there is a museum Toya Lake - Forest. At the center of The Windsor Hotel Toya Resort, Spa & located on the lake was a G8 meeting.
Where to stay Weather
 

42.Kenroku-en
Kenroku-en - one of the most famous scenic Japanese gardens, located in the city of Kanazawa. The garden covers more than 8000 trees and 183 species of plants. Here you'll find the oldest fountain, which runs on natural water pressure. Yuga-TEI is the oldest building in the garden. It was built in 1774. The gardens are open all year round, during the day, and they are known for their beauty in any season.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Kanazawa: Private & Custom Tours

43.Tsukiji fish market
Tsukiji fish market is one of the largest wholesale markets for fish and seafood, although you can find here any other products. The market is in the central part of Tokyo and is one of the main attractions for foreign tourists. The market sells more than 2 thousand tons of fish and seafood per day. In the market you can find more than 400 different kinds of seafood, from small sardines to 300 kg tuna, from cheap seaweed to expensive fish caviar. About 700 thousand metric tons of seafood passes through the counters of three fish markets in Tokyo, which in monetary terms reaches 600 billion yen (approximately 5.5 billion US dollars).
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Tsukiji fish market: Food, Wine & Nightlife Private & Custom Tours
 

44.Kurashiki
Kurashiki is a city in Japan, in Okayama prefecture. Worth visiting: Kurabo Memorial Hall, Ohara Art Museum, Kurashiki Folk Art Museum, theme park called Tivoli, Seto Bridge. In the city is the Museum of Art Museohara, the first Western Art Museum in Japan, founded in 1930. Also in the city there is the Bikan region, a historic district of wooden warehouses of the 17th century, called the kura and surrounded by canals with koi fish.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Kurashiki: Multi-day & Extended Tours Private & Custom Tours
 

45.Niseko
Niseko is a settlement in Japan in Abuta county district of Siribesi, Hokkaido Prefecture. Due to its location between the mountains and the sea, Niseko attracts many tourists. Niseko consists of six ski areas: Niseko Hirafu, Niseko Higashiyama (also known as the village of Niseko), Niseko Annupuri, Niseko Hanazono, Niseko Mojwa, Niseko Weiss. Niseko is also the name of the volcano in the north of the district of Niseko-ch</s>. It is included in the list of the 10 best ski resorts in the world. Niseko also offers golf, tennis, fishing, horse riding, sea kayaks, white water rafting, trekking and cycling.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Niseko: Transfers & Ground Transport
 

46.Kamikochi
Kamikochi - mountain valley within the National Park Chubu-Sangaku in Japan. Kamikochi high mountain valley about 16 km long. Its average elevation is about 1400 m. The surrounding mountains reach 3190 meters above sea level. The surrounding mountains are Mount Hotaka, Mount Yari, Mount Yake, Mont Cho, Mount Jonen, Mont Kasumizawa. The park is officially open from mid-April to mid-November.
Where to stay Weather

47.Mount Aso
    By STA3816 CC3
Mount Aso is the largest active volcano in Japan and one of the largest in the world. Located on the island of Kyushu. The contour of the caldera is about 128 km, although the exact value will vary depending on the source. Height 1592 m. The caldera has 50 000 inhabitants, a railway has been laid. The Nakadake Crater of Aso is a popular tourist destination in the region. In the winter, the caldera is sometimes covered by snow, and in its valley they ride on sledges and skis. The most active crater is in the Nakadake caldera, it has been active for more than 1500 years.
Where to stay Weather
 

48.Mt. Amakazari
Mount Amakazari - Japanese mountain located on the border between Nagano and Niigata prefectures. It is among the 100 most famous mountains in Japan. The height of 1963 m. Two hiking trails allow you to climb Mount Amakazari. From the top views of the Northern Alps. In winter you can go skiing.
Where to stay Weather
 

49.Mount Azumaya
Mount Azumaya - is one of the 100 most famous mountains in Japan. The height of 2354 m. There are about 4 routes for climbing the mountain. Also near the mountain there is the High beautiful Yonago waterfall 80 meters high. Azumaya Mountain was formed between 900,000 and 300,000 years ago. The famous Sugadaira Cohen ski area is located in the southwest of the Mountain. The eruption 340,000 years ago formed a caldera with a diameter of about 3 kilometers. Also on the side of Gunma there is a ski area.
Where to stay Weather

50.Mount Tate
Mount Tate - the mountain is located in the south - east of Toyama Prefecture, Japan. This is one of the highest peaks of mountains Hida with altitude 3015 meters. Mountain consists mainly of granite and gneiss. Through the mountain passes excursion route «Tateyama Kurobe Alpine». On Murod plateau is a shopping area and onsen hot springs. On clear days you can see the mountain waterfalls Sёmё - the highest waterfalls in Japan.
