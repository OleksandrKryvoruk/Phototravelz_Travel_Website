1.Paris
Paris - is the capital and the largest city in France. Sightseeing: Arc de Triomphe, Notre-Dame de Paris Cathedral, Conciergerie, Eiffel Tower, Paris National Opera, Pantheon, Saint-Chapelle, Luxembourg Garden Luxembourg Palace, National Center d'Art Moderne, Rodin Museum, Natural History Museum, Branly Quay Museum . The Seine embankments in 1991 became a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The suburban Fontainebleau Palace and the Palace of Versailles were listed in 1979 and 1981 respectively. According to a survey conducted by Forbes magazine among specialists in 2010, Paris was in twelve most beautiful cities in the world.

2.Nice
Nice - a tourist town and port in the south of France, 32 km from the border with Italy. This resort is the Côte d'Azur. Nice was founded around 250 BC A modern city with a population of about 350 000 inhabitants and includes the old, picturesque town with its narrow streets, the hill of Le Château, a commercial port and the new town, with stunning waterfront and avenue de Angklai promenade (Promenade des Anglais). The main economic activity of the city, in winter and summer, is tourism. A variety of attractions: museums, churches, flea market, beaches and Carnival, which is held since 1873.

3.Lyon
Lyon is the capital of the Rhône-Alpes region, located in the southeast of France. After Paris and Marseille, Lyon is the third most important city in the country. The historical center of the city is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. The old part of the city is located near two high hills and is known as Vieux Lyon, which is characterized by the presence of tight little passages, the so-called narrow alleys, allowing to pass from one street to another, through the courtyards of houses. The northern part of the city contains the Tet d'Or Park (Parc de la Tête d'or), next to which is the headquarters of Interpol. Museums: Museum of Natural History, Museum of Fine Arts, Galatoromaikou Culture Museum, Lumiere Institute, Museum of Textiles and Arts Decor, Museum of Modern Art, International Miniature Museum.

5.Cannes
Cannes - a city on the Mediterranean coast in the south of France, the department of the Maritime Alps, 36 km from Nice, 55 km from Monaco. It is one of the most popular and famous resorts of the Côte d'Azur. Here, annually held the world famous Cannes Film Festival. The center of Cannes is Antibes street, the Freedom alley and the Croisette promenade, 3 km from the Old Port, the Congress Palace to Cape Palm Beach. On Croisette are prestigious hotels, such as Carlton, Martinez, Majestic, boutiques with famous names of the world of fashion. In the center of the old city you can see the Tower of the Syuquet, its height is 22 m, and behind it is the church of Our Lady of Hope (Notre-Dame de l'Esperance).

6.Bordeaux
Bordeaux - a city and municipality in the southwest of France. Bordeaux is located on the banks of the Garonne River, 32 km from the Atlantic coast. UNESCO Committee made Bordeaux's historic center, which is called Port of the Moon (Porte de la Lune), a World Heritage Site in France for a unique urban ensemble. Old Bordeaux is concentrated around a quarter of St. Peter, which diverge from the narrow streets and where a number of old churches are located and extensive estate with wrought iron balconies and arcades. Bordeaux features unique architectural monuments, including the remains of a Roman amphitheater (3rd century), the Roman-Gothic church of Saint-Seren (ages 11-13), St. Croix (ages 12-13) and the Cathedral of Saint-André (middle of 12 - the beginning 14 century), as well as the Gothic church of Saint-Eulalie (ages 12-14) and St. Michel (ages 14-16).

7.Strasbourg
Strasbourg is a city and commune in the east of France, the historical capital of Alsace and the prefecture of the Lower Rhine department. The central part of Strasbourg, the so-called "Big Island", bounded by the arms of the river Il, is listed by UNESCO in the list of world cultural heritage due to the uniqueness of the architectural appearance. This is the first case in France (1988), when the entire city center was included in the list of world cultural heritage. Worth visiting: Strasbourg Cathedral, Kammertzel House, Covered Bridges, Strasbourg Museum of Fine Arts, Strasbourg Archeological Museum, Alsace Museum, Museum of Decorative and Applied Art.

8.Nantes
Nantes is a city and port in the western part of France, the capital of the prefecture of the Loire. The city was founded by the Namnetous (Gallic tribe) around 70 BC. The main attractions are the ruins of the old towers of the Breton Dukes, the Church of St. James (12th century), the Church of Saints Peter and Paul and the Museum of Fine Arts, the Nantes Cathedral, the Ducal Castle, the Archaeological Museum of Nantes. The magazine "Time" refers to it as "the most livable city in Europe".

10.Eiffel Tower
Eiffel Tower is one of the most famous buildings in the world in the center of Paris, France. It weighs 10100 tons, and the length is 325 meters. There are three levels, each of which is accessible by a ladder or an elevator. In order to rise to the first level, you need 300 steps (the same for the second one). The third and largest level is accessible only by the elevator. And the first and second level has a restaurant. The Eiffel Tower is called the most visited paid landmark of the world and the most photographed.
