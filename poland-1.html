Warsaw is located approx. 30 large permanent theaters. The largest metropolitan museums include the National Museum, the Royal Palace, King John III Palace Museum in Wilanow, Warsaw Museum, Royal Baths, Warsaw Uprising Museum, and Museum of the History of Polish Jews, the largest gallery -Pooschrenie and Center for Modern Art Castle Uyazdovskiy.In the city about 30 pools and beaches. In 2000, the town was named European Capital of Culture, and in 2014 UNESCO city of literature. Every year the city attracts more than 8 million tourists. 8.Auschwitz
Auschwitz - a complex of German concentration camps and death camps, located in 1940-1945 to the west of the Governor General, near the city of Auschwitz, which in 1939 by decree of Hitler was annexed to the territory of the Third Reich, 60 km west of Krakow, Poland . About 1.4 million people, of whom about 1.1 million were Jews, were killed in Auschwitz in 1941-1945. On the territory of the camp in 1947 a museum was established, which is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. The museum is registered in the State Register of Museums of Poland.
