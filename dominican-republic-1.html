Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Dominican Republic.

NORTH AMERICA > Island сountries
1.Punta Cana
Punta Cana is an area on the south-eastern coast of the Dominican Republic, famous for its resort and tourist complex Puntacana Resort and Club. A unique place with beaches that are covered with white coral sand. Diving provides an opportunity to enjoy the beauty of coral reefs. The region has developed infrastructure - all thanks to American businessmen.

2.Santo Domingo
Santo Domingo is the capital of the Dominican Republic. The climate is tropical, the wet average annual temperature is 24-28 degrees Celsius. The architecture consists of a mixture of Renaissance, Gothic and Romanesque style. Among the monuments of architecture it is necessary to note the Torre del Omena tower, the castle of Alcazar, the Casa de Engombe palace, the fortress of Osama, the church of San Nicolos. The city is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. In Santo Domingo, there are many museums: the National Gallery of Fine Arts, the Columbus Lighthouse, the Museum of Contemporary Art, the National Museum of History and Geography.

3.Catalina Island
By Kevin Kelley CC2
Catalina Island is a tropical island about 2.5 km from the southeast coast of the Dominican Republic, near the province of La Altagracia and La Romana. There is a cruise ship stop on the island. Currently, the island is uninhabited, on its western side there are beautiful white sandy beaches for tourists with the appropriate infrastructure. Catalina Island can be reached on pleasure boats. Also there are guided tours with the possibility of scuba diving.

4.La Romana
La Romana is a port city opposite Catalina Island in the Dominican Republic. In the city there is a private airport and the area of ​​Casa de Campo, home to millionaires. The town is quiet and well suited for rest with children. A lot of oranges, lemons and sugar cane grows. Near the city there is a national nature reserve, where rare species of animals and more than 500 species of plants live. The local beaches are very clean and they grow coconut trees.

5.Bávaro
Bavaro is a tourist zone in the east of the Dominican Republic. Initially, the city was conceived as a home for workers of local resorts, but along the coast began to grow hotels and the city became a full-fledged resort area. There are a number of golf and country clubs, as well as a shopping center. In the local beaches there are bars.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Bavaro: Air, Helicopter & Balloon Tours Cruises, Sailing & Water Tours Day Trips & Excursions Private & Custom Tours Water Sports
 

6.Samaná Bay
By Šarūnas Burdulis CC2
Samaná Bay is a bay in the Atlantic Ocean off the north-eastern coast of the Dominican Republic to the south of the same city and cape. The main cities on the coast are Santa Barbara de Samana, Sanchez, Sabana de la Mar. In Samana, there are dolphins, as well as humpback whales during the mating season (from late January to early March). Also, pirates have repeatedly appeared here.

Where to stay Weather Things to do in Samaná: Cruises, Sailing & Water Tours Cultural & Theme Tours Day Trips & Excursions Outdoor Activities Tours & Sightseeing Transfers & Ground Transport
 

7.Los Haitises National Park
By Anton Bielousov CC3
Los Haitises National Park is a national park on the northeast coast in the Dominican Republic. He preserved the untouched forest. After 2000, it became popular among ecotourists. It has many caves with petroglyphs and it is not known what kind of culture they made. The national park is accessible only with excursions. It can be reached by boat from different places (Sanchez, Samana, Sabana de la Mar). Most tours depart from Sabana de la Mar via Bahia de San Lorenzo to some islands or peninsulas, with caves to visit (for example, Cueva de las Linias with petroglyphs and Cueva de la Arena with colony of bats), Excursions are conducted on motor boats. Sabana de la Mar also has a visitor center.

8.Los Tres Ojos
Los Tres Ojos - a cave that has a fresh water lake, divided naturally by three lakes is in the city of Santo Domingo in the Dominican Republic. The caves are open from 9 am to 5 pm, and at night are lit by many colored lights. The surrounding vegetation is lush and abundant. The depth of the lake remains unknown. The fauna is also very diverse and includes fish, bats and turtles.

9.Altos de Chavon
Altos de Chavon - a restored medieval area - a copy of the Spanish villages of the 15th century in the Dominican Republic. The city is on the banks of the Chavon River in the resort of Casa de Campo. In addition to its historical value, the city has a number of other attractions: an art gallery, souvenir shops, craft workshops, a Greek amphitheater, an archaeological museum, the Church of St. Stanislaus and the New York School of Design.

10.Columbus Lighthouse
By Kitaviys CC4
Columbus Lighthouse is an architectural monument and the main attraction of the Dominican Republic. It is located in the capital of the country - the city of Santo Domingo. The lighthouse is named after the Spanish navigator Christopher Columbus. On the roof of the lighthouse there are 157 searchlights, which shine up and project the cross. A powerful column of light is visible at a distance of several tens of kilometers. The building also houses a small museum of countries that helped in the construction of the lighthouse. Inside the lighthouse there is a marble mausoleum with remains, as it is believed, Christopher Columbus, where guardsmen bear an honor guard in a beautiful white uniform. The price of construction is about 70 million dollars.
