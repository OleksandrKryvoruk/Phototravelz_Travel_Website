11.Pyramid of Djoser
Pyramid of Djoser - the oldest stone building in the world, Egypt. Tomb of the Pharaoh of the same name. The dimensions of the pyramid: height - 62 m, width 125 m, length 115 m is the first pyramid in Egypt. The pyramid was built in 2650 BC.

12.Valley of the Kings
The Valley of the Kings is a gorge in Egypt, where tombs of pharaohs and a number of important officials are built. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Necropolis has 63 graves. This territory became the center from the end of the XVIII century, first - treasure hunt, then - archaeological and Egyptological studies, and till now its tombs continue to attract public attention. Nowadays, the Valley of the Kings became popular due to the discovery of the tomb of Pharaoh Tutankhamun (KV62).

13.Nile
The Nile is the largest river in the world in terms of the length of river systems. The most part is in Egypt. The length of the river system is 6852 km. In the river there are snakes, turtles, crocodiles, perch, catfish, tiger fish and carp. Nile - the only river in North Africa that passes through the Sahara and brings its waters to the Mediterranean Sea, is the source of life in a waterless desert. On the Nile are the large cities of Khartoum, Aswan, Luxor (Thebes), the urban agglomeration of Cairo-Giza; in the delta - Alexandria. The Nile River in the north of Aswan is a popular tourist route.

14.Great Pyramid at Giza
Giza Necropolis - one of the largest necropolis of the Old Kingdom, Egypt. Included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. The complex consists of a sculpture of the Great Sphinx, the Pyramids of Cheops, Chephren, Menkaure, Khafre's mortuary temple, the Temple of Khafre in the valley, Tomb of Queen Hetepheres I, Temple of the Sphinx, tombs carved into the rock, village artisans, Menkaure quarry and more.

Karnak temple - a temple complex included in UNESCO World Heritage List, Egypt. Major sights: Temple of Amon-Ra, the temple of Khonsu, the Sacred Lake, caryatids Hall, Temple Ipet, Aton temple, the temple of Amenhotep II and the central colonnade of the temple.

16.Monastery of St. Catherine
St. Catherine's Monastery - one of the oldest Christian monasteries in the world, Egypt. The monastery is located at the foot of Mount Sinai, at an altitude of 1570 m. Since 2002 is included in the list of UNESCO World Heritage sites. Construction of the complex robots began in the year 330.

17.Siwa Oasis
By Heksamarre GFDL
Siwa Oasis is an oasis in Egypt, located in the Governorate of Matruh, 50 km from the border with Libya. The oasis is located about 300 km southwest of the Mediterranean coast, in the Kattara Basin, so the oasis is 18 meters below sea level. In 2007 in Siva, fingerprints of human feet were found, whose age is estimated at 3 million years. These are the most ancient human traces in the world. Oasis is known, mainly because of the fact that in ancient times had a temple of the oracle of Amun, whose ruins are very popular among tourists.

18.Red Sea
Red Sea - one of the most salty sea in the world, Egypt. Salinity of 40 g of salt to 1 liter. The sea is famous for its coral reefs, which here are yellow, pink, brown and blue colors. In the sea are found dolphins, killer whales, sea cucumbers, sharks, moray eels, angelfish, butterfly fish and clown fish. The most important lessons in the sea is a recreational diving.

19.Ras Muhammad National Park
Ras Muhammad National Park is Egypt's first national park, which was opened in 1989. Ras Mohammed National Park is the pride of Egypt. Located on the very south of the Sinai Peninsula, 25 km south-west of Sharm el-Sheikh (along the coast), between the Gulf of Suez in the west and the Gulf of Aqaba in the east. It is considered one of the most picturesque places on the entire Red Sea, and the southernmost part of it is among the ten best in the world beaches for scuba diving. Various types of geological rocks are located here and accessible to visitors. The reserve is home to over a thousand different species of fish, and about 150 species of corals grow.

20.Lake Nasser
Lake Nasser is a reservoir on the Nile River, mostly located in Egypt and partly in Sudan. Named after Nasser, the President of Egypt in 1956-1970. The construction of the reservoir required large-scale works to transfer such monuments of Egyptian antiquity, as Abu Simbel, the Temple of Isis (Isis) on the island of Phyla, the temple from Tafis outside the flood zone. The lake is about 550 km long and 35 km wide at its widest point, located next to the Tropic of Cancer. It has a total area of 5250 km2 and a capacity of about 157 km3 of water.
