11.Ko Chang
Ko Chang - the third largest island in Thailand. It is a mountainous island with lots of waterfalls. 85% of the island, along with 50 of its smaller islands is part of the National Marine Sanctuary. The average temperature of 20 C to 33 C. The island offers: white sand beaches, coral reefs for diving and attractions, such as waterfalls and other attractions for tourists. The island has no airport, but the ferry runs regularly.

12.Ko Samui
Ko Samui is an island in the Gulf of Thailand in the Pacific Ocean in Thailand. Modern Samui is the second island in Thailand by popularity, after Phuket. Samui became famous among divers due to beautiful corals and fish. Altogether on Samui 34 beaches, the island is known for its natural attractions: snow-white sandy beaches, coral reefs and coconut palms. Each beach of the island has its own characteristics, and there are only about 10 of them. Also on the island there is a national park, an oceanarium and many temples and pagodas.

13.Similan Islands
Similan Islands is an archipelago in the Andaman Sea in Southeast Asia in Thailand. They are protected as the Mu Ko Similan National Park. The national park consists of 11 islands. The islands are one of the tourist destinations in southern Thailand for swimming and diving. Between May 16 and October 31, the islands are completely closed to the public. Similan Islands are considered one of the best places for diving not only in Thailand, but all over the world.

14.Nang Yuan Island
Nang Yuan Island is a small island near Koh Tao in the Gulf of Thailand, Thailand. It is famous for its places for diving, swimming and a large beach. Ko Nang Yuan is located directly opposite Koh Tao Island, from where boats run regularly. Near Ko Nang Yuan there are two more rocky islands connected by a sandy beach. To the north of Ko Nang Yuan is the tiny island of Ko Nang Yuan, where the diving school is located. Since 1986, several tourist bungalows, a restaurant and a diving center have been built. In the immediate vicinity of Ko Nang Yuan and Ko Tao there are areas suitable for scuba diving.

15.Phang-nga
Phang-nga - the gulf of the Andaman Sea coast of Thailand, between the island of Phuket, the island of Phi Phi. The bay is located northeast of Phuket, north of the Yao islands. From the north it is bounded by the continental part of the country (the provinces of Phang Nga and Krabi). Has many caves. One of these caves became famous thanks to the James Bond movie 007. Also the bay has the Nga Marine National Park and a beautiful natural beach. The islands and waters of the bay have rich flora and fauna.

16.CentralWorld
CentralWorld is a shopping center located in Pratum Van district in Bangkok, Thailand. It is one of the largest shopping centers in the world. In addition to the shopping center itself, there is also an office building with an area of ​​205 m and a 5-star Centara Grand @ CentralWorld. The whole complex has an area of ​​more than 1 000 000 m2. Other nearby shopping centers include Siam Paragon, Gaysorn, Central Chidlom Department Store and MBK. In the shopping center more than 500 shops and restaurants are located on eight floors. On the outside of the building, along the facade 400 meters long, concerts and major events are often held, such as the countdown on New Year's Eve.

17.Dhammakaya Temple
Dhammakaya Temple is a Buddhist temple to the north of Bangkok, in Thailand. To date, this is the largest temple of the community in Thailand. The temple is known for its modern approach to management and iconography. The temple is known for its emphasis on meditation, especially samatha meditation (quiet meditation). Its architecture is like a large stadium or space ship. On his dome is installed 300 thousand gold statuettes of Buddha. Another 700 thousand is located inside the temple. The dome is surrounded by a platform for meditation. In the temple there are about 3000 monks, novices and laymen.

18.Phra Nakhon Si Ayutthaya
Phra Nakhon Si Ayutthaya - extinct city, which was in 1350 -1767 center Siamese kingdom. Ayutthaya is currently one of the most visited tourist attractions in Thailand. The city has more than 400 Buddhist monasteries. The historical part of the city (of Ayutthaya) was declared a historical park in 1976, and since 1991 is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Since the territory of the historical part of a fairly large, offer tourists rent a bike.

19.Phimai Historical Park
 By Arian Zwegers CC2
Phimai Historical Park - includes the ruins of Phimai, an ancient city with Khmer temple area - Rich from Angkor. It is located in the northeast of Thailand in Phimai district of Nakhonratchasim province. It was fortified in the 11th century and became the spiritual center of the Khmer empire. In the National Museum of Fimai, located in the area of ​​the temples, various works were found during archaeological excavations in Fimae and around, including the famous statue of Jayavarman VII. In 2004, the Phimai complex, the ancient Phimai road of the Khmer Angkor and related Phanom Runga and Muangtam temples were included in UNESCO's nominations.

20.Wat Saket
By tjabeljan CC2
Wat Saket is a Buddhist temple in Bangkok, Thailand. Located in the eastern part of the Old Town (Phra Nakhon district), opposite Wat Ratchanadda, goes to Boriphat street. The temple dates back to the Ayutthaya era when it was named Wat Sakai. King Rama I repaired the temple and renamed it Wat Sacket. This building became a popular attraction in Bangkok, however, the rest of the temple is visited less. At the end of the 19th century, cotton was used for cremation, mainly for the poor. Before the construction of the temple of Wat Arun (79 meters), the Golden Hill, 76 meters high, was the tallest building in Bangkok.
