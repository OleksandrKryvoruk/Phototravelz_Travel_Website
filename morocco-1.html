Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Morocco.

AFRICA > North Africa
1.Rabat
Rabat - the capital of Morocco. Monuments of the city included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Main attractions: The Royal Palace of Rabat, the Mausoleum Mohamed V, Hassan Tower, Kasbah Oudayas, Andalusian Gardens, Ruins of Shell, Royal Residence in Rabat. It is worth visiting the museums: Museum of Moroccan Arts, the National Museum of Crafts, Folk Art Museum, Museum of Antiquities, the Archaeological Museum of Rabat.

2.Marrakesh
Marrakech - a city in the Atlas Mountains, Morocco. The old town is on the list of UNESCO World Heritage Site and is made up of ancient buildings built of clay. The city is famous for its gardens and parks. There is a large palm grove behind the old town. The main attractions include: the Jemaa El Fna Square, local markets dried fruit and nuts, city walls and gates, Menara Gardens, El Badi Palace, the Royal Palace, Marrakech Museum, the Museum of Moroccan Arts, Theatre Royal - de - Marrakech International Film Festival in Marrakech.

3.Casablanca
Casablanca - the largest city and port of Morocco. The city is located on the Atlantic coast. The main attractions include the Hassan II Mosque which has the tallest minaret in the world (210 m), United Nations Square, Old Medina, Twin Center Twin Towers, the Cathedral of Notre Dame de Lourdes.

4.El Jadida
El Jadida - a port city near the Atlantic Ocean, Morocco. UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city was founded by Portuguese navigators in 1502. Coast town abounds beautiful beaches and big hotels and villas. The city is actively developing tourism infrastructure, there is a 5-star Mazagan tourist resort, which has a casino, golf courses and shopping centers, and more.

5.Agadir
Agadir - a historic city in the south-west Morocco. The city is located on the Atlantic coast, where you can visit a lot of cafes, restaurants and lodge in the hotel. The old part of the city includes a factory, a museum and an exotic garden. In the city of festivals: Timitar, Film Immigration, International Festival of Theatre of the University of Agadir, the Festival of laughter, the International Documentary Film Festival, Berber music and Morocco Movement association carries out in the city a lot of concerts, exhibitions of art, photography, design, music. To the north of the city you can visit the many beaches.

6.Asilah
Asilah - in ancient times the city of Carthage Zilis, Morocco. The town was founded in 1500 BC when the Phoenicians used it as a trading center. The city is a popular seaside resort with modern tourist infrastructure. In the city of festivals: mural-painting festival, music festivals, arts festivals and the International Cultural Festival.

7.Essaouira
Essaouira - city on the Atlantic coast, Morocco. Medina Quarter is listed as UNESCO World Heritage Sites. One of the most favorite places of Moroccans and Europeans. Along the beach there are many hotels. Due to the strong trade winds city is well-suited for kite-surfing and windsurfing, and there are guided tours on camels and quad bikes. The city hosts a festival Gnawa music.

8.Aït Ben Haddou
Ait Ben Haddou - a city with fortifications, which is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List, Morocco. The city is located on the banks of the river Ouarzazate. Ait Ben Haddou is an example of adobe architecture. All the houses are built with a red clay, the streets are narrow and connected by arches. It is worth visiting the museum, the mausoleum Ben Haddou and granaries.

9.Archaeological Site of Volubilis
Archaeological Site of Volubilis - in ancient times, the most south-western city of the Roman Empire, Morocco. The city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The main attractions Arch of Caracalla, Basilica, the House of Orpheus, The ruins of the Forum, city gates, mosaic floor bath.

10.Hassan II Mosque
Great Mosque of Hassan II - the mosque in Casablanca, Morocco. One of the biggest mosques in the world. The mosque and the area around it can hold more than 100,000 people. It has the tallest minaret in the world 210 m. The construction cost is $ 800 million. Access permitted non-Muslims.
