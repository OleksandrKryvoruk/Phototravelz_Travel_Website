Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Honduras.

NORTH AMERICA > Continental countries
1.Tegucigalpa
Tegucigalpa - the capital and largest city of Honduras. The main attractions include the church Iglesia de San Francisco, the park Parque Central, Antigua-Paraninfo Universitaria museum, art gallery Paraninfo, Military History Museum in the Park Valle, Parque de las Nasiones-Unidas and Parque La Concordia, which are copies of the Mayan sculptures.

2.Cayos Cochinos
By Hector Abouid CC2
Cayos Cochinos - a group of coral islands located 30 km northeast of La Ceiba on the northern coast of Honduras. The islands do not have roads, so there are no cars or motorcycles. The islands can offer: beaches, diving, marine park, huts and hostels. The National Geographic magazine writes: "The waters around these coral bays are just a dream for the marine biologist: protected by the state, closed to divers and fishermen and full of creatures that do not yet have names."

3.Roatán
Roatan - Caribbean island is a part of Honduras. The island is covered by tropical greenery. Beach presented by beautiful sandy beaches, and the island itself is surrounded by a ring of coral reefs. The island will be interesting to fans of diving due to the rich underwater world, which is represented: tropical fish, stingrays, turtles, moray eels and corals. Here you can also see whales.

4.Río Plátano Biosphere Reserve
 By José Ignacio Huerta Gray CC2
Río Plátano Biosphere Reserve is a biosphere reserve located on the river Rio Platan in the departments of Gracias a Dios, Colón, Olancho, on the Caribbean coast of Honduras. The reserve includes both mountainous and low-lying tropical forests full of wild flora and fauna, where tribes of local Indians who lead a traditional way of life also live. The reserve was founded in 1980, in 1982 it received the status of a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

5.La Tigra National Park
La Tigra National Park is the first national park of Honduras. It is located at an altitude between 1800 and 2185 meters, 25 km from Tegucigalpa. The fauna consists of 3 species of amphibians, 13 species of reptiles (2 rare and 2 poisonous), more than 200 species of birds (migrating and sedentary) from 39 families and 13 orders and 31 species of mammals, 6 of which are endangered, 2 threatened and 2 are considered rare. The fauna is represented by bromeliads, ferns, mosses, mushrooms and giants of the forest: oaks, pines, erythrina - the sacred tree of the Maya. In addition, here grow avocados, which are the main source of food for the kvezal.

6.Copan
Copan - the largest Mayan settlements on the territory of Honduras. Included in the World Heritage list. The city was founded in 1stoletii BC In the 7-8 century AD here was the capital of the kingdom Shukuup. Palace complex on the bank of the Copan River includes temples, squares, houses and acropolis. Now the city acts as an archaeological reserve with its museum.

7.Pico Bonito National Park
By Denis Fournier CC2
Pico Bonito National Park - National Park in Honduras southwest of the Caribbean coastal city of La Ceiba. The park has a mountainous landscape, rich flora and fauna and tropical forests. In the mountain forest is full of primates of monkey-howlers, as well as Pico Montaña Corozal (2480 m). This area is very popular for the practice of bird watching, rafting, kayaking and tourism, and a number of tour operators offer transportation, tours and accommodation.

8.Talgua Caves
By Dennis Garcia CC3
Talgua Cave - a cave in which valuable archeological artifacts were found: more than a thousand summer human bones were discovered. This cave is the oldest in the country it is about 2 million years old. Located in the Olahoe Valley, in the city of Katakamas, in the north-east of Central America, the country of Honduras. It is sometimes called the "Cave of Shining Skulls" because of how light reflects off the calcite deposits found in skeletal remains.
Where to stay Weather

9.Lake Yojoa
By Yamil Gonzales CC2
Lake Yojoa is the largest lake in Honduras. Located in the northwest of the state on the border of the departments of Santa Barbara, Comayagua and Copan. On the lake, fishing, kayaking and swimming are popular. Around the lake, pineapples, citrus fruits and coffee are grown. The nature around the lake is characterized by high biodiversity: 400 bird species and 800 plant species.

10.Gumbalimba Park
By Lowell Hendrix CC2
Gumbalimba Park is located in West Bay, Roatan, Honduras. The park offers water entertainment, beach and ocean, various attractions, monkeys, free flying exotic birds, a botanical garden, numerous waterfalls and fountains, a picturesque suspension bridge across the pond, fishing, pool cafe, hamburgers, hot dogs, chicken fingers, and Chicken sandwiches, swimming pool with fresh water.
