1.Sofia
Sofia is the capital, the largest city and the cultural and economic center of Bulgaria. One of the oldest cities in Europe. Sofia has the largest collections of museums in Bulgaria and is one of the destinations in Bulgaria with the largest number of visitors. It is worth to visit: the Ivan Vazov National Theater, the Bulgarian National Opera and Ballet Theater, the National Art Gallery, the Church of St. Sophia (VI century), the St. George's Rotunda (VI century), the residence of the Bulgarian kings of Vran, Vitosha National Park and Boyana Falls, tourism and trekking, there are ski stations that meet the European standard. Sailing and rowing and fishing are practiced on the Iskar and Pancherevo dams. Sofia is the heart of the Balkans: the Aegean Sea is 300 km away, the Black Sea 400 km, the Sea of ​​Marmara 500 km, the Adriatic Sea 600 km, the Alpine Mountains Rila 100 km, the Pirin 200 km, Olympus - at 400 km, and to the Danube River, the cities of Thessaloniki, Athens, Belgrade and Istanbul, too, geographically very near.

2.Sozopol
Sozopol - is a small city. Sozopol is the oldest city on the Bulgarian coast. The city has beaches and the annual arts festival "Apollonia". Nowadays Sozopol is one of the most attractive tourist places in Bulgaria. In recent years, many new hotels have been built, the city has a wide beach. In the city there are more than 600 restaurants, taverns, bars, discotheques, entertainment national institutions that take care of the good mood of tourists. The old part of the city is an architectural reserve. In it the wooden houses of fishermen are predominantly built in the XIX century. Sometimes there are stone houses from the beginning of the 20th century. In general, the Old Town resembles the old part of Nessebar. Among the architectural monuments of the Old City, a two-story post office (the beginning of the 20th century) and the building of the Fisherman's School (the beginning of the 20th century) stand out, is now in a state of neglect.

8.Vratzata
Vratzata is a town in the north-west of Bulgaria. Mountains and forests are suitable for the development of various types of tourism - skiing, speleology, hunting and fishing, hang gliding. There are opportunities for a variety of sports activities, such as hiking, cycling and for those who like more risk paragliding, go-karting, motocross, etc. There are also opportunities for recreation and entertainment - swimming pools for children and adults, jet skis, discos, bars, restaurants. There is also a museum of history and ethnography.

10.Cape Kaliakra
Kaliakra is a long, narrow nose of the northern Bulgarian Black Sea coast, in the south-east part on the Dobrudzha plateau. Cape Peninsula resembles and is a natural and archeological reserve. Rocks on the Cape have a height of about 70 m, and down to the beach there are a lot of caves, arches, which were formed by the action of erosion. Cape has an ancient fortress of the 4th century. Now cape acts as a reserve. To the north of the cape has a bird's Bay, home to over 150 species of birds. Also in one of the caves of Cape has a restaurant with 160 seats and the other with a small museum.
