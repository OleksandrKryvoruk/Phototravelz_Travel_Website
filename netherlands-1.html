1.Amsterdam
Amsterdam - the capital and at the same time the largest city of the Netherlands. Amsterdam is located in the western part of the province of North Holland. Multicultural Society city has a reputation around the world. Amsterdam remains a popular destination for tourists, and is the most popular of all the cities in the Netherlands. Amsterdam is visited 4.5 million tourists a year from around the world. Amsterdam is very popular among people who love the architecture of the historic buildings. Amsterdam is a true city of museums (37 museums). Amsterdam also has a lot of large and small theaters. In addition to drugs, prostitution is also legal in Amsterdam. Amsterdam is also known as the city where homosexual persons are welcome.

5.Utrecht
Utrecht - it is a municipality and the capital of the Dutch province of Utrecht. It is the fourth largest city in the Netherlands. It is the hub of the Dutch rail network and place a staff - apartments Nederlandse Spoorwegen. Buildings Utrecht (regular in the south, more freely in the north) is lifted up above the water level, embankments - 2 tiers. In the 1920s in architecture Utrecht there was a transition to functionalism [numerous works of Gerrit Rietveld (Schroeder house, complexes of houses in working-class districts, 1930 and 1950) and other architects]. 6.Delft
Delft - a city in the western Netherlands, in the province of South Holland, on the Schie river, just south of The Hague. Old Town (founded in 1075) on a rectangular plan with a large market (Groote Markt), crossed by a network of wide canals, filled with clear water, surrounded by old poplars and many old houses. He has a number of notable buildings: Nieuwe Kerk (New Church, built between 1397 - 1496) in the Gothic style, the tower of 115 meters and a beautiful tomb of William of Orange, who was killed in a local monastery (now a museum of Bruges); Oude Kerk (Old Church) in 1250, Renaissance Stadhuis (Town Hall) from 1618 - 1620 and others. Lambert van Meerten Museum with a large collection of pottery. One of the oldest cities in the Netherlands.

8.Keukenhof Gardens
Keukenhof Gardens - garden and one of the biggest attractions of the Netherlands, spring, every year hundreds of thousands of tourists from around the world visit these gardens. Situated between Amsterdam and The Hague, in the village of Lisse, the garden covers an area of ​​32 hectares. Spring blooms more than 7 million flower bulbs, tulips, hyacinths, crocuses, daffodils. It is the largest garden of flowering plants in the world. Flowering fields around Keukenhof, over the years can be visited by bike and boat.

10.Kasteel Rechteren
Kasteel Rechteren is located on an island in the river Vecht, the Netherlands. A stone arch bridge gives access to the castle. This is the only castle in the province of Overijssel from the Middle Ages, which has survived. The castle is located in the Rechteren area, which is under the municipality of Dalfsen. On the highest part of the estate, landscape architect Leonard Springer has developed a formal garden in the neo-baroque style. The castle is clearly visible from the road from Wilsteren to Delfsen, but the castle, gardens and park are not accessible to the public.
