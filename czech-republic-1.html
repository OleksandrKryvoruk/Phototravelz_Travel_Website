2.Olomouc
Olomouc is a city in the Czech Republic. In the city there is the Holy Trinity Column, one of the World Heritage sites in UNESCO. It is considered one of the most beautiful ancient cities of the Czech Republic with a rich cultural life. Olomouc is called the second Prague, quieter and quieter. In the city are the residence of the archbishop, the second oldest in the Czech Republic University (Palacký University), the headquarters of the land forces of the Czech Republic and a number of cultural and government institutions. A special highlight of the city - spacious squares with fountains, built in the early XVIII century in the Baroque style.

4.Karlovy Vary
Karlovy Vary - a city in the western part of the Czech Republic. It is the most visited spa - a city in the Czech Republic. In addition, the city's many historic architecture: the church of St. Mary Magdalene (1732), the Church of St. Urban (13th century), the Evangelical Church of St. Peter and St. Paul (1856), The ruins of the Romanesque church of St. Linhart (13th century), the Chapel of the Virgin Mary (1700) , imperial baths, the Karlovy Vary municipal theater. Local Spa treatment is based on the use of mineral water with medicinal properties, which is extracted from a depth of 2000 m. The water has a beneficial effect in the treatment of hepatic and intestinal diseases. There are about 28 Carlsbad springs. The town also has an outdoor pool, a lot of theater, film festival and singing competition.

8.Prague Castle
Prague Castle is located in the district of Chrantsani in Prague, Czech Republic. It is one of the largest and oldest castles in the world. The first known fortress history arose on the site of the Prague Castle in the 9th century. According to the Guinness Book of Records, having a length of 570 m and a width of 130 m, it is the largest castle in the world. Part of the complex is the Cathedral of St. Vitus - the main cathedral of Prague, in which are buried many of the eminent rulers of the Czech Republic, as well as the tomb of St. Yana of Nepomuk.

10.Bohemian Switzerland National Park
Bohemian Switzerland National Park - one of the four national parks in the Czech Republic. Designed for protection of unique sandstone rocks and formations. Forests cover 97% of the park area. The flora of the park are: the European beech, Norway spruce, Scots pine, silver birch, fir, sessile oak, alder, linden, hornbeam. The animals can be seen: the gray wolf, the wild cat, brown bear, the field mouse, deer, wild boar, and about 230 species of birds. The park has several routes for mountain climbing and trekking, as well as the abandoned camps.
