31.Frankston
Frankston - a suburb of Melbourne, Australia. One of the main attractions is the McClelland Art Gallery and Sculpture Park on the Mornington Peninsula. Sculpture Park is located on 16 acres of natural reserve. The park has more than 100 sculptures. Also at the park hosts the annual Sand Sculpture Festival, which is the largest event of its kind in the southern hemisphere. For an important event in the life of the city belongs to the fireworks festival, which takes place each December and attracts more than 45 000 tourists annually.

32.Hunter Valley
By Roderick Eime CC2
Hunter Valley is an area in Australia in the state of New South Wales, commonly known as Hunter Valley. The Hunter Valley is the most important tourist destination in New South Wales. In the area of ​​Pokolbin there are many vineyards, restaurants, shops, golf courses and courses, country houses and horse riding. Other valleys are also known for wine. The area of ​​the Upper Hunter around Scone is known for being one of the areas where horses are bred. Regular events are organized for visitors, including trips on steam trains and regular scenic cruises along the Hunter River and Lake Macquarie.

33.Perisher Blue
Perisher Blue is a ski resort in the state of New South Wales in Australia, located in the Kosciuszko National Park, in the Snowy Mountains region. With 48 lifts on 1,245 hectares and four villages (Perischer Valley (1720 m), Mount Blue Cow (1890 m), Smiggin Holes (1680 m) and Gutega (1,640 m), Perisher - the largest ski resort in Australia. You can rent ski equipment, but do not buy it.Several hotels allow you to stay in Perischer, but there are more possibilities for accommodation in Jindabyne.The ski season starts in early June and usually lasts until October, annually.

34.Fraser Island
Fraser Island is a sandy island off the eastern coast of Australia, which is 120 km long and 23 km wide. The largest sand island in the world. The height of the sand dunes reaches 240 meters. On the west coast of the island grow a mango fox, and on the east there is a beach of white squeaks whose length reaches 100 km. Fraser Island is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site. On the island there are 40 fresh lakes, which are called "hanging" because they are surrounded on all sides by the salt waters of the ocean.

35.Springbrook National Park
By Paul Welding CC2
Springbrook National Park - located in Queensland, Australia, about 80 km south of the city of Brisbane. The park is part of the nature reserve, the tropical forests of Australia Gondwana, inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. In all parts of the park (Springbrook Plateau, natural bridge and barrows) you can go on a picnic, explore nature and enjoy a wide variety of walks in the natural environment and beautiful beauty. On the stretch of the Springbrook plateau there are many points where the views are impressive. Also here you can observe various waterfalls along the short hiking trails.

36.Great Otway National Park
By Lenny K Photography CC2
Great Otway National Park - is a national park in the state of Victoria in Australia, 162 km southwest of Melbourne. It contains many landscapes and species of vegetation. The park was identified by BirdLife International as an important area for bird conservation (IBA), as it supports the populations of Dasyornis widebenti, Calamanthus fuliginosus and Petroica rodinogaster, and countless other species. The park is also a popular destination for tourists from other countries. Many companies carry out tours to the region for tourists and is a very reliable place to inhabit koalas.

37.Daintree National Park
By denisbin CC2
Daintree National Park in the far north of Queensland, Australia is located about 1,502 km northwest of Brisbane and 100 km northwest of Cairns. The park was founded in 1987 and was nominated as a World Heritage Site. Most of the national park is covered by tropical forests. Rainforest already exists for 110 million years and is arguably the oldest existing tropical forest in the world. The popular Mossman Gorge is located on the south side of the park. Cape Tribulation is also in the park. The park is also home to bouncing stones of aboriginal terrain.

38.Grampians National Park
National Park Grampians - is 167 000 hectares of stunning natural beauty in Australia. The park consists of sandy ridges 5 and is part of the Australian National Heritage Site and one with a few places where you can see art and culture of the indigenous peoples of Australia - Aborigines. The reserve is held wine festival Grampians Grape Escape. Also it operates a tourist center where you can learn more about the natural and cultural attractions of the park.

39.Lucky Bay
Lucky Bay is one of the best beaches in Australia. Even kangaroos like to rest here. Near the beach there is a national park. The length of Lucky reaches 5 km, so you can easily find a piece for yourself to relax. From July to October, migratory whales can be observed here. Well developed camping, so you always find where to stay for the night. Its uniqueness lies in the fact that kangaroos are walking along it! They wet their paws in warm water, lie on the sand and swarm in algae in search of delicacies.

40.Noosa National Park
Noosa National Park is a great place for climbing, fishing, surfing, diving and many other sports (Australia). There is an informal nudist beach. In the park there are many animals such as koalas, opossums, parrots, cockatoo, and also here you can see humpback whales. Excursions take place on five tourist routes, the longest reaches 8 kilometers. Tents are banned in the park. It is the most popular national park in Queensland and has more than a million visitors a year.
