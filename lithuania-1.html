Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Lithuania.

EUROPE > North Europe
1.Vilnius
Vilnius - Lithuanian capital and largest city of the country, located at the Neris River, Lithuania. Gates of Dawn (Dawn Guard, or Dawn) - the only ones of the nine defensive gate of Vilnius, surviving. In the chapel there is a major Christian shrine Lithuania and Belarus - Our Lady of the Gate of Dawn, one of the few icons on which the Virgin Mary is depicted without the Infant Jesus. Užupis, or district - an area surrounded on three sides by the River Vilnia. It is a place of creative people: artists, poets, sculptors and craftsmen of alternative art, their studios and museums. Other notable monuments: Tower of Gediminas, Vilnius Town Hall, Church of St. Anne's Cathedral, Church of the Holy Trinity Cathedral of the Theotokos, the Mother of God of the Sign Church.

2.Kaunas
By Adam Jones CC2
Kaunas is a city in Lithuania. The port on the river Neman. The city is surrounded by 12 ancient fortresses, buildings in the time of Catherine II, which are connected by tunnels. The collection of the local art gallery began to be formed even in the times of the Republic of Both Nations (also known as Rech Pospolita). Some of the works in the 19th century were confiscated and taken to Russia by the tsarist government as a punishment for the support of Polish national-liberation uprisings by citizens. In the 20th century, the Lithuanian foreign diaspora supplemented the collection by handing over a number of paintings by European masters, among them: Rubens, Sneijder, Babyuren, Manyasco, Salvator Rosa, Edouard Manet. Kaunas is an ancient city, where there is both usual and interesting buildings. Among the less expressive architecture is the significant one: the remains of Kaunas Castle, the Church of Vitatstva (founded in 1400), the Cathedral of Peter and Paul (15th century completed in the 17th century), the Masalski Palace (17th Renaissance) and many other ancient Interesting buildings.

3.Palanga
Palanga - a resort town on the Baltic Sea, Lithuania. Climatic, balneological and mud resort. Health centers, health centers, hospitals offer: hardening, intensity of solar radiation (high season there are more sunny days than anywhere - else in Lithuania), the cool sea bathing, low, medium and high salinity of the water, mud. Architectural monuments: Villa Anapilis (1898), Villa "Sea Eye" (1900), Brig park Palanga manor, Amber Museum.

4.Trakai
Trakai - the city submission to the district in Vilnius county in Lithuania. The city is located between 72 Trakai lakes surrounded them on all sides and has the Trakai National Historical Park. Sightseeing: Trakai Castle 14th century Karaite kenassa, Church of the Nativity of the Virgin (1863), Church of Bernardine, ruins of an old castle, Trakai History Museum, Karaite Ethnographic Museum, Trakai Palace Theatre.

5.Snow Arena
Snow Arena - indoor ski resort in Druskininkai, Lithuania. The complex is one of the largest in Europe, the total length of the tracks is more than 1100 m, width is 50 m, and the difference in height is 65.65 m. The main track is divided into two separate parts - a snowboard park and ski slopes. The room temperature is constantly maintained from -2 to -4 C (28.4 to 24.8 F).

6.Vichy Water Park
By Pofka CC3
Vichy Water Park is a water park located in Vilnius, Lithuania. "Vichy" water park can accept 1,5 thousand visitors at a time. The park is equipped with 9 of the most modern attractions in the Baltic States, including - "Maori's Vow" - acting on the principle of a pendulum and allowing you to experience a sense of weightlessness. This attraction is the largest among the closed water parks in the world. Kids are waiting for a specially designed for them playground "Island of Games", where about 50 different attractions. For the needs of the visitors of the park there is a car parking, 1300 locker-lockers, 120 shower cabins. Guests of the water park Vichy are serviced by more than 80 specially trained workers: rescuers, observers, sellers, waiters of bars and restaurants, show participants.

7.Hill of Crosses
The Hill of Crosses - Catholic church in Lithuania, a place of pilgrimage. Located 12 km from Siauliai city on the road Vilnius - Riga. It is a hill on which a plurality of crosses, their total number is about fifty thousand. This mountain is a memorial to the victims of forced deportations from Lithuania who wake undertaken by the Soviet totalitarian regime in the 1940 - 1941 years, and after the Second World War. It is unique in its shrillness memorial to the victims of anti-humane policies of the totalitarian Stalinist regime.

8.Aukštaitija National Park
By I, Hugo.arg CC3
Aukštaitija National Park is a national park in the northeast of Lithuania, 100 km north of Vilnius. The area of the park is 405.7 km2. The park's flora includes 64 plant species, 8 species of fungi, 48 species of birds, which are listed in the Red Book of Lithuania. More than 70% of the park is covered with forests, the bulk of which are pine plantations. The age of some trees exceeds 200 years. There are 126 lakes on the territory of the park, most of which are combined with streams and small rivers. There are 116 villages in the park, in which about 2300 people live.

9.Curonian Spit
By Kyle Taylor CC2
Curonian Spit - a sandy spit on the coast of the Baltic Sea,  Lithuania. The length is 98 kilometers, the width varies from 400 meters to 4 kilometers. In 2000, the Curonian Spit was included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Favorable climatic conditions allow you to rest on a spit in the period from May to November. Sightseeing: One of the highest sand dunes in Europe (up to 60 m), the most attractive of which is the mobile dune "Efa", the unique lake "Swan", "Royal Bor", Thomas Mann House in Nida. On the spit, there are about 900 plant species and live moose, deer, fox and wild boar and more than 100 species of birds. You can also enjoy windsurfing and sailing. There are also numerous museums that represent jewelry.

10.Church of St. Anne
Church of St. Anne - a famous example of a church in the style of fiery Gothic on the former lands of Belarus. After the war of 1939-1945. The city was transferred to the Republic of Lithuania. One-nave, hall type with a gothic vault. The length is twenty-two (22) meters and a width of ten (10) meters. The height of the temple is more than twelve (12.7) meters. It is built from the local bricks of thirty-three (33) different shapes, which made it possible to create flexible and rich decorative forms. Particularly talented, the main façade with three towers of the same height and varieties of Gothic arches, logically go into a complex pattern, which has no analogues.
