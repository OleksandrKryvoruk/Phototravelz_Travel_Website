1.Helsinki
Helsinki - the capital of the Republic of Finland. Helsinki is located on the shores of the Gulf of Finland. Architectural masterpieces Cathedral, Orthodox Uspensky Cathedral, the National Opera of Finland, Sveaborgs fortress Kiasma - Museum of Contemporary Art. The most famous cultural event in Helsinki is the Helsinki Festival, which takes place in late summer. Tuska Open Air is a great event of heavy music. Many of Helsinki attractions connected with the sea. The islands are about 250 historic buildings and fortifications.

2.Santa Claus Village
By Tatu Timo Newton-Syms CC2
Santa Claus Village is an amusement park located about 8 km from Rovaniemi in Finland. In its vicinity there is also a second theme park in Santa Park. Sightseeing: The Arctic Circle - it passes almost through the center of the village and is marked by a white line, Santa Claus Mail - you can not just give a letter, but also buy all kinds of things related to Christmas, Santa Claus Office is located in the main building of the village, Where tourists can chat and take a picture with Santa Claus. In the village there are several restaurants and shops where you can buy all kinds of things related to Santa Claus and Lapland.

3.Linnanmäki
By Tatu Kosonen CC4
Linnanmäki is an amusement park in Helsinki, Finland located in the Alppila district. Linnanmäki has about 40 different large and small devices. In addition, there are halls with slot machines, kiosks, restaurants and scenes outside. The oldest device is a carousel that was built in 1896. Linnanmäki is one of the most visited places for Finland.

4.Lapland
Lapland - the northern region of Finland. A popular tourist destination every year it is visited by more than: 130 000 Britons, Germans (123,000), the French (79000), the Netherlands (55 000), the Russian (43,000), Norwegians (42 000), the Swiss (32 000) and Japanese (28 000). In summer and autumn Lapland offers walks, fishing, kayaking. In winter - ski vacation. Also popular Christmas tourism.

5.Oulu
Oulu - the sixth most populous city in Finland. Oulu has a vibrant cultural life. There is a local symphony orchestra for classical music lovers. In the summer there are many outdoor cultural events. The cultural institutions of the city: City Theatre, Art Museum, Northern Ostrobothnia Museum (local history museum in the north - eastern part of the Gulf of Bothnia), Concert Hall, Cultural Centre Valve Tietoma and scientific center. The rest of the attractions: Ainol Puisto (Botanical Garden), Automobile Museum, Oulu Art Museum, Qstock rock - an open-air festival.

6.Botanic Garden of Turku
Botanic Garden of Turku - gardens in the city of Turku, Finland where more than 5,000 species and varieties of plants. In the greenhouse a lot of plants and a large collection of tropical species. Target species: rhododendron, peonies, ornamental plants, plants with practical application, meadow flowers, exotic trees and shrubs. Where you can hold meetings, training sessions and private events, in public gardens, greenhouses, rooms for seminars and cafe.

8.Suomenlinna
Suomenlinna - bastion system of fortifications on the islands near the capital of Finland Helsinki. Administrative - the district of Helsinki. From the XVIII to XX century fortifications defended Helsingfors from the sea. The total area of ​​the islands is 80 hectares. They are home to about 900 people. Fortifications of the fortress are built on 7 rocky islands that make up the "Wolves' Skerries". At the beginning of the XXI century, the fortress of Sveaborg is one of the main attractions of Helsinki, it has been listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1991. On the islands of the former fortress are several museums, the naval academy of the Finnish Navy and the prison of the facilitated regime, whose prisoners work to maintain the fortress in order. As part of the fortifications - the numerous artillery of the times of the fortress development.
