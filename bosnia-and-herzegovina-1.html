Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Bosnia and Herzegovina.

EUROPE > South Europe
1.Sarajevo
Sarajevo is the largest city in Bosnia and Herzegovina. The city is located along the Miljacka river. In the capital of Bosnia and Herzegovina, there are many architectural monuments of different cultures and religions. Among the most famous religious buildings in the city include: Cathedral of the Sacred Heart of Jesus, Church of the Nativity of the Virgin, Gazi Husrev Mosque, National Museum of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Church of Saint Vincent de Paul. Also worth a visit: Museum of Jews of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Historical Museum of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Museum of Sarajevo, Sarajevo Philharmonic, Monuments of the Ottoman Period, Monuments of the period of Austria-Hungary.

3.Konjic
Konjic - is a city in Bosnia and Herzegovina. The city was founded on an ancient trade route connecting the Mediterranean Sea to Sarajevo. The first mention comes from 1382. Worth visiting: the bridge in the old town, the Serbian Orthodox Church, local mosques.

4.Sutjeska National Park
By Radosław Botev CC2
Sutjeska National Park is the oldest national park in Republika Srpska, part of Bosnia and Herzegovina. It was established in 1962, it covers an area of ​​17,250 hectares. The nearest city to the park is Focha. On the territory of this national park is one of the four relict forests of Europe - Peruchitsa. Also in it is located the highest mountain in the RS - Maglich (2386 m above sea level). 66% of the territory of the national park is covered with dense forests. The fauna of Sutesk is rich and diverse. In this national park live bears, wolves, chamois, wild boars, martens, wild goats and the like. Also there live about 300 species of birds, of which 61 species nest in it.

Jahorina is a mountain and ski resort in the Palais community, Bosnia and Herzegovina. The height of the mountain is 1916 meters above sea level. 6.Vrelo Bosne
By aidaweb101 CC2
Vrelo Bosne is a public park by the Bosna River in the foothills of Mount Igman on the outskirts of Sarajevo, the capital of Bosnia and Herzegovina. The roads inside the park are ideal for walking and cycling, which gives visitors an opportunity to take a closer look at the swirling streams and waterfalls on the river. Local outdoor cafes and restaurants offer visitors drinks and food. The opening hours of the park vary depending on the time of the year. In the park ducks and swans live. Entrance for adults in the park is paid, for children - for free. The money raised from the sale of tickets is spent to maintain cleanliness and order in the park.

10.Sarajevo Tunnel
By Fanny Schertzer CC3
Sarajevo Tunnel - is a tunnel that was made during the siege of Sarajevo in 1993. It was built by the Bosnian army in order to link the city of Sarajevo, which was completely cut off by Serb forces. The tunnel is about 720 meters high and about 1.5 meters high. After the war, in the old private house, whose cellar served as an entrance to the Sarajevo tunnel, the Sarajevo Tunnel Museum was established.
