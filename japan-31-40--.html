31.Sensō-ji
Sensō-ji is a Buddhist monastery in Japan. Belongs to the sect of Sjokannon. The main monastery of the sect. Located at: Tokyo Prefecture, Taito District, Asakusa Quarter 2-3-1. The monastery title is the mountain of the Golden Dragon. Founded in 628 year. The main shrine of the monastery is the statue of the Bodhisattva Kannon. The valuable monuments include the Sutra (National Treasure of Japan), the Niteng Gate (an important cultural landmark of Japan). The oldest Buddhist monastery in the territory of Tokyo. Near the temple is the Shinto shrine, the Temple of Asakusa.

32.Kiyomizu-dera
Kiyomizu-dera is a Buddhist monastery in Japan. Belongs to the North Hosso sect. The main shrine of the monastery is the statue of a thousand hands Kannon. Named after a waterfall flowing in the mountains. 1994 is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Japan. Among the valuable monuments are the golden temple (the national treasure of Japan), the gate of bodyguards (an important cultural landmark of Japan), the three-story pagoda (an important cultural landmark of Japan), the bell tower (an important cultural landmark of Japan). Monument of Culture Kokufu. 2007 Kiyomizudera became one of the fourteen finalists of the contest "Seven new wonders of the world", organized by the Swiss government fund.

33.Shuri Castle
Shuri Castle is a castle in Japan, located in the Shuri area of ​​Naha city of Okinawa Prefecture. The castle of Schuri was a royal residence in the time of the Ryukyu state. Its length from north to south is 270 m, from west to east - 400 m. In the projection, the castle resembles an oval surrounded by strong stone walls. The total area is 46.167 m2. The outer wall of the fortifications and the outer courtyard have four arched gates, the inner wall and courtyard are eight gates. On the territory of the courtyard was located the main palace and government buildings of the Ryukyusha state. Known in Japan, the Ceremonial Gate is the second gate of the outer wall of the fortifications. In 2000, the Schury Castle was recognized as the World Heritage by UNESCO.

34.Mount Koya
Mount Koya is a common name for the mountain massif and the mountain plain, surrounded by eight mountains of this massif, in Japan, in the northeast of Wakayama prefecture, the city of Koya. It is considered a sacred place of Japanese Buddhism, namely the sect of esoteric Buddhism Shingon, the location of its main monastery Konbuudzi. Since 2004, the component of the site "Holy Places and Pilgrim Routes of the Kiia Mountain Range" is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. From Koya-san, the ancient path of the Kumano-Kodo pilgrimage departs to the Shinto shrines of Kumano-Hongu-tayxi Kumano-Hayatama-taisia ​​and Kumano-Naty-tai.

35.Matsue Castle
Matsue Castle is an ancient medieval castle located on the shore of Shinziko Lake in Matsue, Japan. Currently, inside the castle is a museum of weapons and armor samurai. Also known as the "Black Castle" (because of the walls painted in black), and also as "Zuyk Castle". This castle is considered the second largest, the third in height (30 meters) and the sixth in the list of the oldest castles. The design of the lock is a complex structure. Externally, the castle looks five-story, but inside it consists of six levels.

36.Kinkaku-ji
Kinkaku-ji or the Temple of the Golden Pavilion in Kyoto, Japan. Kinkakuji was in 1994, along with other monuments around Kyoto, included in the UNESCO World Heritage List under the name of the monuments of ancient Kyoto. The main shrine of the monastery is the statue of the Bodhisattva Kannon. The valuable monuments include a portrait of the Shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu (an important cultural landmark of Japan), frescoes of the office of the day (an important cultural landmark of Japan), a monastery garden. Cultural monument of China. The former residence of Ashikaga Yoshimitsu, turned into a Zeniv monastery after his death.

37.Arashiyama
Arashiyama - is a tourist area from near the city of Kyoto in Japan. It is located north of the Nishiki district, south of Ukyo and west of Kyoto, at the foot of Mount Arashi. Arashiyama is a site included in the cultural heritage of Japan. It develops around the bridge of Todeksu, a place highly regarded by the Japanese to admire the spring blooming cherry blossom, and in the autumn - maple. To the north of the bridge is the impressive bamboo forest of Sagano. Numerous shops and restaurants are located in the area near the bridge. The Buddhist temple Tenryuy is built on the side of the mountain. It has a garden designed by Mus Soseki.

38.Mt. Yarigadake
Mt. Yarigadake or Mount Yari -. Mountain in Japan from a height of 3180 m Mount Yari is located in the Chubu-Sangaku National Park. During the season, climbing Mount Yari is popular with climbers. Main routes: Higashikama ridge (trail Omoteginza), Nishikama Ridge (Uraginza track), Trail Yarisawa, Yaridaira trail, Trail travel - Yari-Hotaka Juso.

39.Shinjuku Gyoen
Shinjuku Gyoen - a large park with several gardens, located in the urban areas of Shinjuku and Shibuya in Tokyo, Japan. A favorite place of rest for the townspeople: here they go for walks and have picnics. Especially a lot of people here in the early spring, when the entire park blooms sakura. In this park, a record number (1,500) of Sakura trees of 75 species was planted. Thus, this park is one of the most famous places of the khans in Tokyo. In the territory of Shinjuku-gyoen, three gardens have been created: classical Japanese, a regular park in the French style and a landscaped English garden. The park area is 58.3 hectares, the circumference reaches 3.5 km.

40.Hamarikyu Gardens
By Steven Johnson CC2
Hamarikyu Gardens is a park located in Tokyo's special district of Chuo in Japan. It is located in the mouth of the Sumida River and is surrounded by a moat filled with the waters of the Tokyo Gulf. In the center of the park there is a tea house, in which Japanese tea ceremony is held regularly. Gardens are included in the "list of picturesque, historical places and natural monuments" of Japan. The park can be accessed through two bridges, or with the help of river trams. The park is open from 9 to 17 hours (the entrance closes at 16:30) daily.
