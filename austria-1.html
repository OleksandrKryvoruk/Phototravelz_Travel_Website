1.Vienna
Vienna is the capital of Austria. Vienna is the third city - the seat of the United Nations after New York and Geneva. The historical center of Vienna in December 2001 was listed in UNESCO. The Vienna Opera is one of the most famous in the world. Gundertwassergaus is a masterpiece of the architect Hundertwasser. Hofburg is the residence of the emperors of the Holy Roman Empire. Belvedere Palace is a luxurious baroque palace complex. The parliament building is one of the most vivid examples of the Neo-Greek in Europe. Museums in Vienna: The Museum Quarter, the Museum of Art History, Albertina - one of the largest museums of fine art in the world, the Military History Museum - one of the oldest military-historical museums in the world, the Belvedere Palace, the Vienna House of Arts.

2.Salzburg
Salzburg - a city in the west of Austria, the capital of the federal state of Salzburg. Salzburg - the fourth largest city of Austria after Vienna, Graz and Linz. The central historical part of Salzburg, on the list of UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city is located on the banks of the river Salzach, in the northern foothills of the Alps. Mountain landscape in the south contrasts with the plains to the north. The closest alpine slide Untersberg (elevation 1972 meters) is located at a distance of several kilometers from the center city. Old baroque town, numerous churches and Hohensalzburg located on the left bank of the Salzach. One of the symbols of Salzburg - fortress Gogenzaltsburg . Its history begins in the XI century, when on the hill there was the first wooden fortification. Soon there were stone walls, and then for six centuries, the fortress continuously expanded and rebuilt. Salzburg is best known as the birthplace of the brilliant composer Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart. In University Square, opposite the University Church is a house 9, where in 1756 and the birthplace of Mozart. The main facade of the building in the grain lane has undergone great changes, but part of the house facing the square has retained its original interior view. 3.Innsbruck
Innsbruck is a tourist city and the capital of Tyrol - a large region of western Austria. Innsbruck is located at an altitude of 547 m above sea level on the Inn River, in the middle of a large beautiful valley, which is surrounded by mountains: Serles, Patscherkofel, Nordkette whose height is over 2,500 m. The city is also the world famous winter sports center. In 1964 and 1976, the city hosted the Winter Olympic Games.

4.Wachau
Wachau - a region in Lower Austria, includes a valley along the Danube, which lies between the cities of Krems and Melk, 80 km west of Vienna. The Wachau Valley is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. In Wachau is developed tourism and winemaking. Also in the valley are the cities of Dürnstein, Spiez and the village of Weissenkirchen. A moderate climate contributed to the development of winemaking and fruit growing in the valley. Frequent summer sunny days with cool nights attach great importance to the wine produced in the valley. Among the fruits, the most important are the apricots grown in Wachau.

10.Grossglockner Alpine Road
Grossglockner Alpine Road - a panoramic road in Austria. It connects the state of Salzburg and Carinthia. Road Großglockner-Hochalpenstraße passes at an altitude of 1953 m above sea level. It begins in Fusch an der Großglocknerstraße (805 m above sea level), passes through Hohtor pass and overcoming 47.8 km, finishing in the municipality of Heiligenblut (1301 m). Ends at Kaiser Franz Joseph Center, with panoramic views of the glacier pasterze glacier and mountain Grossglockner. The route runs along the ridge of the Hohe Tauern. The maximum slope of the road is 10.2%. On January 12, 2016 Grossglockner High Alpine Road is a candidate for inclusion in the UNESCO World Heritage List.
