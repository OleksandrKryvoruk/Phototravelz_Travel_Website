11.Ushuaia
Ushuaia is a port city on the island of Tierra del Fuego in Argentina. The southernmost city on Earth. Place of cruise ships and yachts stop on the way to Antarctica. Nearby is the Tierra del Fuego National Park. Near the town there live seals, penguins, killer whales and many sea birds. It is worth to visit the Maritime Museum, the Museum of the End of the World, Akatushun Museum - Harberton Station, Almafuerte Cultural Center, Henrietta Gastelumendi Cultural House, Yatan Forest, Maritime Museum Picture Gallery, Avenue of craftsmen.

12.Tierra del Fuego National Park
By Douglas Scortegagna CC2
Tierra del Fuego National Park is a national park in the southern part of the Argentine sector of Isla Grande Island, located 11 km west of Ushuaia. The park is the most southern national park of the planet and the first in Argentina, created on the sea coast. The territory of the park includes Lake Roca and part of Lake Fagnano. The southern part of the national park is the northern coast of the Beagle Channel. In the park you can find: Antarctic nightfagus, Barbaris boxwood, Embothrium coccineum, Drimis Wintera, Vodianika, Empetrum rubrum, Physalis vulgaris, European rabbit, Canadian beaver, Gray fox and more than 90 species of birds.

13.Talampaya National Park
 By Kevin Jones CC2
Talampaya National Park is a national park in Argentina, located in the western part of the province of La Rioja. The area of ​​the park is 2150 km2, the average height above the sea level is 1500 m. The purpose of the park's foundation was to protect the sites of important archaeological and paleontological excavations. In 2000, UNESCO included the Talampaya Park as a World Heritage Site. Talampaya Park is in a valley between two mountain ranges, and its landscape was formed as a result of the action of water and wind erosion. Sightseeing of the park: Dry riverbed of the Talampaya river, where hundreds of millions of years ago lived dinosaurs. Talampaya Canyon - a deep canyon, the height of the walls reaches 143 m. Botanical Garden. Remains of settlements of local people.

14.Nahuel Huapi National Park
By Emilio Küffer CC2
Nahuel Huapi National Park is Argentina's oldest national park, located in the Nahuel Huapi Lake area. The height above the sea level of the national park varies between 700-3500 m. In the park located on the border of the Valdivian forests and the Patagonian steppes, high mountains, many lakes and vast hard-to-reach forests prevail. In the north, the Nanel-Uapi Park is joined by the Lanin National Park. The territory of another national park, Los Arrananes, adjoins Lake Nauel-Huapi. On the shore of the lake is San Carlos de Bariloche, the center of Argentine tourism and a winter resort, in the city there is a popular museum among the tourists named after F. Moreno with many household items of residents of these places.

15.Cerro Catedral
Cerro Catedral is a mountain with a height of 2,405 meters in the Nahuel Huapi National Park in Patagonia, Argentina. The nearest town, Bariloche, is 19 kilometers away. It is here that one of the largest ski resorts in South America with an area of 2 square kilometers, about 100 km of trails and a ski lift. The refuge of Emilio Frei, managed by the Andino Bariloche Club and located on the Tonchek lagoon, offers accommodation for tourists and mountaineers who travel the mountains.

16.Cueva de las Manos
 By Pablo Gimenez CC2  
Cueva de las Manos - a cave in the south of Argentina in the province of Santa Cruz, in the valley of the river Pinturas. Known due to the archaeological and paleontological finds made in it, primarily these are wall paintings and negative images of human hands, the oldest of which date back to the ninth millennium BC. e. In addition to the numerous hands on the walls of the cave, scenes of hunting for guanacos, ostriches-nandu, felines and other animals are also depicted. The figures show the use of bolas, the traditional throwing weapon of the Indians of South America. In 1999, the cave is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List.

17.Quebrada de Cafayate
 By Miguel Vieira CC2
Quebrada de Cafayate - a nature reserve, also known as Quebrada de Cafayat, is located in the Calhakis Valley, in the province of Salta, in the north of the Argentine Republic, in the immediate vicinity of the city of Kafayat. The valley is well known for its beautiful scenery. Characterized by the slopes of red conglomerates and sand, silt and stone clay stone. The rocks are continental deposits of the "average" Cretaceous. Recent archaeological studies have shown that a number of underground passages pass through the archaeological site.

18.Valdes Peninsula
Valdes Peninsula - a peninsula on the Atlantic coast of Argentina, in the north-east of Chubut province. The area is 3625 km2. Most of the peninsula is an uninhabited territory. There are several salt lakes, the largest of which lies 40 meters below sea level, this is the lowest point on the land of South America. In 1999, the Valdez peninsula was included in the UNESCO World Heritage List - primarily for its unique and rich fauna. On the coast of the peninsula are colonies of marine mammals, such as the southern elephant seal, eared seals, ostriches nandu, guanaco, mara. Orcas are found off the coast.

19.Fitzroy
Fitzroy is a mountain in Patagonia on the border of Argentina and Chile near the city of El Chalten. The altitude is 3405 m. It is located in the territory of the National Park Los Glaciares. Near the mountain there are also other peaks: Cerro Poincenot, Aguja Rafael, Aguja Saint Exupery and Cerro Torre. To visit the mountain you need to get a permit, you can get it in El Chulten. The Fitzroy area is popular with tourists. There are many-day trekking routes, as well as circular routes from El Chalten. The most popular purposes of such walks are lakes Laguna de los Tres, Laguna Torre and a technically uncomplicated climb to the nearby Cerro Electrico peak.

20.Las Leñas
 By mike CC2
Las Leñas is an Argentine ski resort located in the western part of Mendoza province, near the border with Chile. It is located at an altitude of 2240 m above sea level. This resort was founded in 1983 and is one of the largest in all the Andes. In the past, the World Cup in Alpine Skiing was organized here several times. It has 14 lifting facilities that allow access to 29 ski and snowboard tracks, classified as a beginner, intermediate, advanced and experienced. The ski area is about 65 km. There is also a "Landscape Park" with an area of ​​1500 m with jumps, super-levellers and rails and night skis. In Valle de las Lenias there is a program that offers safe classes that are taught by highly qualified teachers.
