1.Bucharest
Bucharest is the capital of Romania, at the same time it is the most populous city and the most important industrial and commercial center of the country. In Bucharest are located Parliament (House of Parliament or the House of the People). In the old part of many monasteries and churches of 16-17 th century: Radu Voda Monastery, Monastery Mihai Voda church Drapers Alba Other architectural monuments include the Arc de Triomphe, the Romanian Athenaeum, Royal Palace, the Victoria Palace on Victory Square. Local museums and galleries: Museum of Natural History Grigore Antipa National Dimitri Gusti Village Museum, the National Art Museum of Romania. Also, the city has 17 parks and 19 large theaters.

2.Cluj-Napoca
Cluj-Napoca - one of the historical capitals of Transylvania, Romania. The city has a number of cultural centers: the French Cultural Center, American Cultural Center "the JF Kennedy", the British Cultural Centre, German Cultural Centre in Cluj (Deutsches Kulturzentrum Klausenburg), Italian Cultural Center, Center for Japanese Art and Culture, Cultural Center Sindan, Korean Cultural Center, Chinese cultural Institute "Confucius". Also accepts festivals: International Festival of Transylvania Film, Comedy Cluj, Transylvania Jazz Festival, Festival Power Locks. Architecture attractions include the Church "Saint Michael", Orthodox Cathedral, Palace Széki, Rhédey palace Berde Palace, Teleki Palace, Palace Wass, Babos Palace, Museum of Art, Bonţida Castle, the National Museum of history. Also, the city's many nightclubs and pubs.

5.Bușteni
Buşteni - a city in central Romania. It is located in the Prahova Valley, at the foot of Bucegi Mountains (2505 m). This is one of the most popular ski resorts, offering breathtaking views. 6.Peles castle
Peles Castle - Palace in Sinaia, Romania built in 1914. The former summer residence of the Romanian kings, is now a museum. The museum includes: Hall of Honor, The Royal Library, the Music - hall, theater hall, concert hall, Imperial Suite, Royal bedroom. In the castle runs an extensive tour in 5 languages.

7.Bran Castle
Bran Castle - a historical and architectural monument located in Bran-Rucar, 30 km from Brasov, Romania. The castle was built in 1382 and its height is 66 m. The Castle owns Archduke Dominic Habsburg. The castle is located 30 km from Brasov and includes 4 museums, the royal park with two lakes, a tea house, the house manager and the house of Princess Ileana.

8.Danube
Danube Delta - located mainly in Romania and partly in Ukraine. The Danube Delta is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. The river has a varied fauna: gray oak, English oak, ash, aspen, elm, yellow lily. The fauna includes 360 species of birds and 45 freshwater fish species. Among the mammals can be found: raccoon, muskrat, golden jackal, hare, hedgehog, ground squirrel, weasel, ferret.

9.Pietrele Doamnei
Pietrele Doamnei - Nature Reserve in the north of Romania. It has an area of ​​253 hectares and is located at an altitude of 1400 m. The reserve has unique rocks, scree, karst valleys, pine forests, mixed meadows, grasslands, alpine meadows, which are several thousand years old. The reserve is home to many rare species of plants and animals.

10.Transfăgărășan
Transfăgărăşan - road in the mountains 151 km long has a lot of twists and stunning landscape. National Road 7C (DN7C) - the highest mountain group in Romania and connects the historical region of Wallachia with Transylvania. The road passes near the fortress Poenari, alpine meadows Nature Reserve Fagaras Mountains, Balea waterfall. In winter, part of the road is closed.
