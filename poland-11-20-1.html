14.Beskids
Beskid - a group of mountain ranges in the Carpathians, Poland. The length of 600 km, the width of 50-70 km. The mountains were formed 20 million years ago and include sandstone, shale, and limestone marl less. The oldest rocks are more than 100 million years. Among the vegetation dominated by beech, oak, spruce, pine, fir, maple white, currants, mountain ash. 15.Niedzica Castle
Nedzica Castle is a medieval fortress in Poland. The castle was built at the beginning of the fourteenth century on the site of former defensive structures, stands at an altitude of 566 meters on a hill, 300 meters upstream from the Dunajec River. It is considered one of the most picturesque castles in the country, adorns the covers of many books and notepads. Now operates as a museum hotel. In the museum you can see the so-called "Salamon's rooms", furnished with furniture of the sixteenth - nineteenth centuries. A wooden staircase leads to the observation deck. Through another door you can get into the castle dungeon, which served as a prison.

17.Jerzmanowice
Jerzmanowice - a village in Poland located in the Malopolska province, in the district of Krakow. The former royal village founded in 1325. The main tourist sites: Cave Nietoperzowa, Bald Rock (Fiala), Ostry Kamień, Church of Sts. Bartholomew in Jerzmanowice (1338), the Chapel of St. John the Baptist in Jerzmanowice (1696). The village is in close proximity to the national park of Ojców. In the area there are as many as 33 natural monuments. The village is located in the upper part of the valley Będkowska, one of the longest and most beautiful valleys of the landscape park Krakow valleys. The village has hiking and biking trails that allow you to visit other parts of the park.
