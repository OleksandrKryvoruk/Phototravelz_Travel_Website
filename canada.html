1.Niagara Falls
Niagara Falls - a structure of waterfalls on the Niagara River, separating the American state of New York from the province of Ontario, Canada. Height of waterfalls is 53 m, width is 320-790 m. One of the most popular tourist places in Canada. A few hundred meters downstream from the waterfall through Niagara, the "Rainbow Bridge" is opened, which is open for the movement of cars and pedestrians between the two countries. The best view opens from the Canadian territory. Under the waterfall built hydroelectric power.

2.Banff National Park
Banff National Park - the oldest national park in Canada. It has many coniferous forests, glaciers and ice fields with beautiful alpine views. In the park, you can stop at two grand hotels Banff Springs Hotel and Chateau Lake Louise. It is one of the most visited parks in the world. The animal world is represented by the park: grizzly bears, wolverines, wolves, lynxes, cougars, elk, deer, beavers, squirrels, chipmunks, eagles, falcons and many others. The park includes: hot springs, three ski resorts, horseback riding, golf, hiking and camping.

6.Quebec
Quebec is the historic city of Canada. The city of Quebec is famous for its winter carnival and Château Frontenac. Quebec is the only fortified city in North America whose city walls, gates and towers are preserved. Annually there are local holidays. Worth visiting: The Basilica of Notre-Dame de Victoire, the Quebec fortress, the Museum of Civilization, the National Museum of Fine Arts of Quebec, the Quebec Experience Theater Museum, the Maritime Museum of Quebec, the Mont Saint-Anne Ski Resort, the Montmorency, the Cabaret Capitol de Quebec, The Grand Theater de Quebec, the Dramatic Theater of the Quebec Conservatory, the Capitol Theater, the Ice Hotel and the National Day of Quebec on June 24th.

10.Whistler
Whistler is a resort town along the Pacific coast of Canada. Annually the city is visited by more than 2 million tourists, mostly fans of skiing. The ski resort consists of two large areas: the mountain slopes of Whistler and the adjacent edge of the upper part of Blackcomb. Here there are competitions in luge, bobsleigh, skeleton, ski freestyle and snowboarding. The resort has more than 200 shops, 90 restaurants and bars, as well as hiking trails, bungee jumping, kayaking, airplane rides, car tours, rope parks, climbing, wakeboarding, rafting and golf.
