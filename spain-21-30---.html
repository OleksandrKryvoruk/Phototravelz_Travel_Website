21.Plaza Mayor
By Sebastian Dubiel CC3
Plaza Mayor is one of the central squares of the Spanish capital. It is located in the part that is commonly called "Austrian Madrid". Rivals the Puerta del Sol for the right to be called the main square of the Spanish capital, but unlike the democratic Puerta del Sol, Plaza Mayor is a status area, the "navel of Spain", as Lope de Vega said. The square in the style of the Madrid baroque, one of the few monuments of the Habsburg era, was built by the architect Juan Gomez de Mora. Plaza Mayor was the first square in Madrid where there was a permanent place for bullfighting - before that, battles with bulls occurred on improvised sites.

22.Sagrada Familia
Sagrada Familia is a Catholic church in Barcelona (Spain). Its construction began in 1882, the temple is located to the north of Barcelona's Old Town district in the urban area of Eixample. The Temple of the Holy Family is not a cathedral, but a simple church - the central cathedral of Barcelona Cathedral of St. Aulalia. The design of the church was originally designed by the architect Francisco del Villar, who was replaced by Antoni Gaudi one year after the construction was started. Gaudi completely redesigned the project, having executed it in the Art Nouveau style.

23.San Lorenzo de El Escorial
By giborn_134 CC2
San Lorenzo de El Escorial is a municipality in the northwest of the Spanish region of Comunidad de Madrid. In San Lorenzo there are two monuments closely related to the Spanish history: The monastery and the palace of the Real Sitio de San Lorenzo de El Escorial, built between 1563 and 1584 on the initiative of King Philip II of Spain according to the plans of Juan Bautista de Toledo, is considered the largest building of the era Renaissance in the world. Abadía de la Santa Cruz del Valle de los Caidos may be the most important architectural symbol of the dictatorship of Franco.

24.Siam Park
Siam Park - water park on the island of Tenerife (in its southern part), Canary Islands, Spain. Located on the TF-1 highway in Costa Adeje. All 18.5 hectares of the territory of Siam Park, including all attractions, park buildings and restaurants, are decorated in Siamese themes. The park was opened by the princess of Thailand. It belongs to the same owner as Loro Park, which makes it possible to purchase double tickets that allow you to visit both parks at a discount. "The calling card" of the water park is the 28-meter hill "Tower of power". On the territory of the park there are 5 restaurants of Thai and local cuisine. The best water park in Europe in 2014-2017.

25.Palacio Real
Palacio Real is the official residence of the kings of Spain. It is located in the western part of Madrid. On the north side, near the street Bailen, stretch the upper gardens of Sabatini, planted in 1933 on the site of the former stables. The main entrance is located on the southern facade overlooking the Armory Square. The interior of the palace: the frescoes of the Italians Corrado Jakinto, Giovanni Battista Tiepolo, Luca Giordano and Caravaggio, the German master Anton Mengs and the Spaniards Diego Velasquez, Francisco Goya, Francisco Bayeux, Mariano Salvador Maelly and Vicente Lopez, crystal chandeliers, Flemish tapestries, furniture in neoclassical style, rococo and empire, porcelain, watches, unique collection of violins of Stradivarius, ancient weapons, portraits and paintings - turn the palace into one of the best museums in Madrid.

26.La Boqueria
La Boqueria is a city market in Barcelona (Spain), in the sub-district of El Raval, with an entrance from the Rambla street. The building occupies an area of 2583 m2 and is constructed of glass and steel, and is decorated with mosaic. Today the bazaar La Boqueria is one of Barcelona's largest shopping centers and one of Barcelona's most popular tourist attractions. There are fish and seafood, meat and cold meat, vegetables and fruits, including traditional Spanish products, spices, etc.

27.Museo Nacional Del Prado
Museo Nacional Del Prado - one of the largest and most important museums of European fine arts, located in Madrid, Spain. The building of the museum is a monument of late classicism. It is one of the top twenty most visited art museums in the world. The collection of the museum contains one of the most complete collections of Bosch, Velasquez, Goya, Murillo, Zurbaran and El Greco. Works in the halls of Italian painting date from the early Renaissance (Fra Angelico, Mantegna, Botticelli) to the XVIII century (Tiepolo). On the 0th floor the main center of the exhibition are the paintings of Raphael. Pictures of Caravaggio and Gentileski are located on the first floor. The creations of the artists of the Venetian school - Titian, Tintoretto, Veronese and Bassano - form one of the most rich picturesque ensembles of the Prado.

28.Guggenheim Museum
Guggenheim Museum - a museum of contemporary art in the Spanish city of Bilbao. The permanent collection of the museum includes works of art by major artists of the second half of the 20th century, among others. Andy Warhol, Rosenquist, Rauschenberg, de Kooning, Anthony Tàpiese and others. Permanent exhibitions of the museum are devoted to the art of the XX century - installations and electronic works exceed the number of traditional paintings and sculptures. The central work of the collection is a series of sculptures The essence of time, made of weatherproof steel sculptor Richard Serra and placed in the gallery of Artelor.

29.Tossa de Mar
Tossa de Mar - a city on the Costa – Brava (Spain). About 45 km from the city is the natural park of Montseny, which is part of a network of protected natural areas. Tossa de Mar has three main beaches: Tossa Beach, La Mar Menuda, El-Codolar. Also worth a visit: the ancient hospital of San - Miguel Roman villa Ametllers, Fort Tossa de Mar, the Municipal Museum, built in the late Gothic style.

30.Santiago de Compostela Cathedral
Santiago de Compostela Cathedral - the Catholic shrine of Santiago Matamoros in the Galician city of Santiago de Compostela (Spain), where its relics rest. The largest pilgrimage center of medieval Catholicism, the final destination on the path of St. James. The historical part of the city, formed around the cathedral, is included in the list of World Heritage sites. The cathedral is hung from the ceiling, the largest in the world - the size of human growth. It weighs 80 kg and is set in motion by swinging eight servants (tiraboleiros) in scarlet robes. The filling of this censer (known as botafumeiro) takes 40 kg of coal and incense.
