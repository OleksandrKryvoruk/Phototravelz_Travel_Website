Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Madagascar.

AFRICA > South Africa
1.Amber Mountain National Park
By Marco Schmidt CC3
Amber Mountain National Park - is one of the first and one of the most intriguing parks of Madagascar. The amber mountain is famous for its biodiversity and unique climate. This park, spread over an area of ​​23 thousand hectares on the slopes of an extinct volcano, is called a cool oasis of tranquility. The park is just 14 km south of the city of Diego Suarez, in the north of Madagascar. The vegetation of the park is a moist rainforest. On its territory there are 6 lakes and several picturesque waterfalls and rivers. Visitor excursions lasting from 4 hours to 3 days are available for visitors. The difference in heights on the hiking trails of this park ranges from 850 to 1450 m above sea level. The Montagne d'Ambre National Park is home to many endemic species of animals. To date, of endemics there are 77 species of birds, 7 species of lemurs and 24 species of amphibians. It is here that visitors can see the smallest lemur - brown mouse lemur. A unique inhabitant of the park is the crested Madagascar ibis. A recent discovery was that the smallest chameleon also lives in this park - micro-brucose, whose adult length is barely 3 cm. The park's flora is represented by 1020 species of plants.

2.Nosy Boraha
Nosy Boraha - an island off the east coast of Madagascar. One of the most popular places where you can watch the whales. In the 17th century on the island we lived the most real pirates, there now you can visit their graveyard. The island is safe from a shark lagoon, which is well suited for diving. The island is found several species of lemurs and many orchid growing.

3.Isalo National Park
By Bernard Gagnon CC3
Isalo National Park is a national park on the island of Madagascar. Located in the province of Toliara, the island of Madagascar. The area of the park is about 800 km2. The park is known for a wide variety of landscapes. In the park there are 82 species of birds, 33 species of reptiles, 15 species of frogs and 14 species of mammals.

8.Rova of Antananarivo
By Hery Zo Rakotondramanana CC2
Rova of Antananarivo - the former royal palace in Antananarivo, Madagascar. The palace complex is located at an altitude of 1480 m above sea level on Mount Analamanga. This place was occupied by local leaders as early as the beginning of the 17th century. Fortress structures on the mountain were constantly in disrepair. To increase the area of the complex by 1800, the height of Analamanga was even reduced by 9 m.
