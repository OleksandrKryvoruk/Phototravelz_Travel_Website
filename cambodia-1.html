3.Angkor Thom
Angkor Thom - urban complex built during the reign of Jayavarman VII in Cambodia. (Rules 1181-1220), the most important ruler of the Angkor empire. The outer walls of the city built on a square plan. The length of these walls is about 13 km, height of 8 m. Around them there is the defensive moat 100 m wide. The gates have the same decor and a height of about 20 m. Among the preserved buildings stands out the most was the central Bayonne with rich decoration of the relief and 49 towers decorated with huge parties. In 1992 Angkor - Thom, along with other monuments included in the UNESCO list.

5.Royal Palace
The Royal Palace, Phnom Penh is a complex of buildings in Phnom Penh, the capital of Cambodia, the residence of the kings of Cambodia, who have lived here since the palace was built in 1860, with the exception of the Khmer Rouge regime. The palace was built on the site of the fortress of Banteay Kev on the west bank of the Tonle Sap River at the confluence of the Mekong River. In 1865, King Norodom I moved the capital from Udong to Phnom Penh.

8.Ream National Park

Ream National Park is a national park in Cambodia, located 18 km from Sihanoukville in the southeast of Cambodia. It was founded in 1993. The national park protects the sea coasts, mangroves and tropical jungles. There are more than 150 species of birds, as well as a few populations of monkeys. In the south-east of the park is an uninhabited island.

9.Phnom Kulen
By Narith5 СС2
Phnom Kulen is a plateau in the province of Siem Reap in northern Cambodia. On the territory of the plateau is the national park Phnom Kulen. The Kulen Plateau is an isolated chain of small mountainous plateaus of medium height to the south of the Dangrak Mountains. This chain extends 40 km in the direction from the northwest to the south-east and is located 48 km north of Siem Reap. The highest point is 487 m, and the average height of the plateau is about 400 m.
