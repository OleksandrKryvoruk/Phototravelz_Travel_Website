21.Waitomo Caves
Waitomo Caves - is a cave system and the main attraction in the southern Waikato region in the North Island in New Zealand. They are known for their stalactites and stalagmites. Today, several companies, large and small, send tourists through the caves - hundreds of tourists per hour. The main caves are the cave of Waitomo, the cave of Ruakuri, the cave of Aranui and the cave of Gardner. Tourists can practice extreme sports.

22.Ruapehu
Ruapehu is the active volcano and the highest point of the North Island (2,797 m) in New Zealand. It has 3 peaks of Tahuranga (2797 m), Te Heuheu (2755 m), Paretetaitonga (2751 m). On the slopes of the volcano, forests grow, and a height of over 1,500 m is covered with ice and snow. Ruapehu has two commercial ski fields, Whakapapa on the north side and Turoa on the southern slope. These are the two largest ski areas in New Zealand. Both ski fields are accessible by car and chairlifts and mostly belong to private clubs.

23.Whakaari
By Krzysztof Belczyński CC2
Whakaari is the only active volcanic island of New Zealand. The island of White Island is the summit of an active volcano, located 48 km from the east coast of the New Zealand island of North, in the Bay of Plenty. The nearest settlements are Fakatane and Tauranga. The island has a rounded shape; diameter is about 2 km. The highest point reaches 321 m. The age of the geological volcanic formation is about 2 million years. With the exception of a small colony of gannets nesting here, the island is completely uninhabited. The Isle of Wight is one of the most visited active volcanoes in the world and is an object of constant study by scientists. The island is also open for tourist trips.

24.Mount Cook
Mount Cook - the highest point of New Zealand (3754 m). Part of the Southern Alps. The mountain is covered with snow and glaciers. It is located on the territory of the national park of the same name. Neighbors Cook is another 140 mountains, which are about 2000 m high. A popular destination among tourists, is also a favorite destination for climbers adventure. The average annual rainfall in the surrounding lowland areas is about 7.6 m. This very high amount of rainfall leads to the growth of tropical forests in the coastal lowlands and a reliable source of snow in the mountains to preserve the flow of ice.

25.Kelly Tarlton's Sea Life Aquarium
Kelly Tarlton's Sea Life Aquarium is located in Auckland, New Zealand. It is a mixed museum, an aquarium and a mini-theme park. The main entrance is in Tamaki Drive 23 in Waitemata harbor. The most important exhibits are the Antarctic encounter and the underwater world. Visitors can watch penguins, collections of sea horses, eels, crabs and fish, such as poisonous Stonefish and Pufferfish. The project is also one of the first who uses conveyors to slowly move people through viewing areas.

26.Karori Wildlife Sanctuary
By russellstreet CC2
Karori Wildlife Sanctuary is a conservation area in Wellington, New Zealand. Here an attempt is made to restore biodiversity on an ecological island with an area of 225 hectares of forest. The most important feature is a 8.6-km-long fence that is designed to hold fourteen species of non-native mammals. The protected area is now an important tourist destination in the city of Wellington and is responsible for greatly increasing interest in bird observations.

27.Paihia
Paihia is a city in New Zealand. Located in the north-west of the North Island, in the Northland region. In Paihia, the first cricket game was created in New Zealand, and the first church of New Zealand was built. Once a small fishing village, Paihia is also a popular tourist destination because of its proximity to Waitangi and Russell. Worth visiting: Theater House "T-Uara Runanga", Maori Warskanu, Treaty Grounds Waitangi, Haruru waterfall. From the bay you can also go kayaking along the river to the waterfall and back.

28.Whakapapa skifield
By P. Doyle CC4
Whakapapa skifield - is located on the north-eastern slopes of Mount Ruapehu (North Island). This is the largest resort of New Zealand, which has everything you need to take international skiers from all over the world.43 Routes of all difficulty levels - for beginners and off-piste experts. There are schools of mountain skiing and snowboarding. North exposure, many steep slopes, picturesque, treeless. In late July, the team competition Dynastar Te Heu Heu Trophy. In August - amateur competitions Haensli Cup. The traditional Spring Festival is held in late September.

29.Mt Ngauruhoe
Mount Ngauruhoe is an active volcano in the central part of the North Island, New Zealand. It is located between the active volcanoes of Tongariro mountain in the north and Ruapehu mountain in the south, west of the Rangipo desert and about 25 km south of the southern shore of Lake Taupo. Another place where the masterpieces of Lord of the Rings and Hobbit were filmed. The height of the mountain is 900 m, the width of the crater is 150 m. The mountain is 2,5 thousand years old. Its slopes are devoid of vegetation and covered with snow in the winter.

30.Otago
Otago is a region in the south of the South Island, New Zealand. In the territory there are many attractions: the Southern Alps, Lake Wanaka, the river Kluta, the reservoir Dunstan. Also here is the fault of the Indo-Australian and Pacific tectonic plates. In the area of ​​Central Otago high-quality wines are produced by varieties such as Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Merlot and Riesling. Bungee jumping, rafting and paragliding in all variations are just some of the many sporting events that annually attract about 1.7 million tourists.
