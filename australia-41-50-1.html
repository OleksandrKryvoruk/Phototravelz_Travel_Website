41.Lake Moogerah
By Paul Welding CC2
Lake Moogerah is a national park in the south of Queensland (Australia), located 70 km south-west of Brisbane. The park occupies 676 hectares and consists of four protected areas, which are surrounded by volcanic peaks and rocky cliffs. On the territory of the park grow eucalyptus forests, as well as a large number of rare plants. Gorges of Monte François - one of the most visited places in the state for mountaineers. All four parts of the national park are accessible from the Cunningham Highway.

42.Lord Howe Island
Lord Howe Island - a small volcanic island, shaped like a boomerang, stretched from north to south for 10 km and has a width of about 2 km. Belongs to Australia. The age of the island of Lord Gavu is about 20 million years, which makes it one of the oldest volcanic islands in the Pacific Ocean. The Travel Association of the Island of Lord Howe organizes island tourist companies. Visitors are offered about 20, mostly small, private houses, guest houses and lodges of different price categories, many restaurants and cafes, as well as a small conference center.
Where to stay Weather

43.Kings Canyon
Kings Canyon is part of the main attraction and Watarrka National Park in Australia. Tourist season is opened from October to March, because the summer heat is unbearable. Canyon is composed of sandstone, which is more than 400 million years. The park is home to more than 50 endangered plant species. The fauna is mainly represented by kangaroos, because only they can withstand heat.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Kings Canyon: Transfers & Ground Transport Walking & Biking Tours
 

44.Lady Musgrave Island
Lady Musgrave Island is a large uninhabited coral island that forms part of the Great Barrier Reef in Australia. The island is inhabited by numerous species of birds: gulls, frigans and cormorants. In the lagoons, there are large turtles. The underwater world is full of rare fish and coral reefs. The island's floor is in the national park. The number of tourists on the island is strictly limited. It is a small small island surrounded by a coral wall forming a large turquoise lagoon.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Lady Musgrave Island: Day Trips & Excursions Multi-day & Extended Tours

45.Ulladulla
Ulladulla is a coastal city in New South Wales, Australia. It is located 179 km south of Sydney. In recent years, tourism is developing here very actively: many recreational zones are being built, beaches are being equipped. Every year on Easter, a fleet festival is held here, which ends with a grandiose fireworks display. This is a popular holiday destination for residents of Sydney and Canberra. At the edge of the beautiful Ulladulla harbor is a number of beautiful beaches.
Where to stay Weather
 

46.Buccaneer archipelago
Buccaneer archipelago is an archipelago off the coast of Western Australia near the town of Derby in Kimberley County. The nearest settlement is the city of Bardi, located about 54 km (34 miles) from the western edge of the Island group. Includes more than 800 islands, from King Sound Bay and Collier Bay. The area is characterized by huge tidal waves, reaching more than 12 m. Located in the Bukanir archipelago are horizontal waterfalls - an amazing natural landmark of Australia. Some islands have rare fragments of rain forest, and some parts of the coast are covered with mangroves in areas of silt accumulation.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Buccaneer archipelago: Air, Helicopter & Balloon Tours Day Trips & Excursions
47.Bunbury
Banbury is a city on the shores of the Indian Ocean and the third largest city in Western Australia. The city has a lot of entertainment centers with its theatrical, comedy and music bands. The most significant attractions of Banbury include: the Rose Hotel, the old pier, the St. Mark Church and the Cottage King Museum. The city is also famous for its small Koombana beach, where you can swim with wild dolphins and dive. In Banbury there are many tourist and recreational opportunities. Some of the most popular attractions include: Dolphin Detection Center, Bunbury Back Beach, Coomban Bay, Banbury Wildlife Park. It is also very close to the Ferguson Valley.
Where to stay Weather

48.Twelve Apostles
Twelve Apostles is a group of limestone rocks in the ocean near the coast in Port Campbell National Park, located on the so-called Great Ocean Road in the Australian state of Victoria, Australia. Their appearance and a short distance from each other made this place a popular tourist attraction. The height of some rocks is about 45 meters. Twelve Apostles are located between the cities of Port Campbell and Princeton on the Great Ocean Road. In the early 2000s, a tourist center was built on the road to provide convenient parking and a better overview of the group. Also popular is the flying around the group by helicopter.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Twelve Apostles: Day Trips & Excursions
 

49.Pinnacles Desert
The Pinnacles Desert - limestone structures are part of the Nambung National Park near Cervantes in Western Australia. Once upon a time there was a rich flora and fauna, but now only creative pieces of sand remained from them. Every year the park is visited by 150 thousand. tourists. Most animals in the desert are nocturnal, but emu and kangaroos can be seen at any time of the day. Near the Indian Ocean there are several parks. As elsewhere in Australia, each car park has an active and fully efficient gas barbecue.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Pinnacles Desert: Day Trips & Excursions Multi-day & Extended Tours
 

50.Perry Sandhills
Perry Sandhills desert occupying 160 acres, was formed almost 40 thousand years ago, is located 6 km west of Wentworth, New South Wales, Australia. Because of the winds, the desert constantly migrates. During the Second World War it was used as a test site. Every year in Perry, the Wentworth Mildura arts festival is held. Today, this area is often used as a background for films and TV shows.
