21.San Miguel de Allende
San Miguel de Allende - the city and administrative center of the same-named municipality in the Mexican state of Guanajuato, is on the World Heritage List. UNESCO. The city is a monument of Spanish colonial culture. The main architectural sights include the El Chorro district, the Parish Church of San Miguel, the Cultural Center of Ignacio Ramirez, the Casa de Allende Museum, the Oratorio de San Felipe Neri, the Juarez Park. Now the city is a popular tourist center and a place where rich pensioners from Mexico, USA and Canada settle.

22.Isla Mujeres
By Falco Ermert CC2
Isla Mujeres is an island in the Caribbean Sea in the Riviera Maya region, off the east coast of the Yucatán peninsula in Quintana Roo state in Mexico. The size of the island is about seven kilometers in length and up to 3x km. in width, it lies in thirteen kilometers to the east of the coast of the city of Cancun. The eastern coast of the island is rocky and washed by the strong surf of the Caribbean Sea. Surf and strong waves are absent on the leeward west coast, where tourist beaches are located and a view of Cancun and the clear waters of the strait. From the end of May to September is the site of the accumulation of one of the largest quantities of Whale Sharks in the world.

23.Isla Contoy
 By Son of Groucho CC2
Isla Contoy, a small Mexican island owned by the municipality of Isla Mujeres, is in the state of Quintana Roo, about 30 km to the north of the island. The island is 8.75 km long, the average width is 500 m, and has an area of ​​230 hectares. To the south of the island is the reef Ixlaché, which is part of the second largest barrier reef in the world. Service providers have the right to bring up to 200 visitors a day. Each visitor must apply for permission in the national offices of the park in Cancun or Isla Mujeres. Activities include visits to the lighthouse, mangroves and the museum, where the biologist explains the functioning of ecosystems.

24.Sayulita
By Paul Hamilton CC2
Sayulita is a small town located in the Bay of Banderas in the southern state of Nayarit on the Pacific coast, in Mexico. The population is about 2300 inhabitants. Tourism in Sayulita is very good during the summer season, mostly the town is visited by surfers, as this is a very good place for surfing. Other tourists are attracted by the mysterious folk cultures of Sayulita. Nightlife in Sayulita is very active. Every weekend there are parties in nightclubs. Every year thousands of tourists visit the village on their way to the Bay of Banderas.

25.Cancún Underwater Museum
 By Ran Allen CC2
Cancún Underwater Museum or MUSA is a museum located under the waters of the Mexican Caribbean Sea in the waters surrounding Cancun, Isla Mujeres and Punta Nizuk. The museum was officially opened in November 2010 and is currently one of the largest and most ambitious underwater attractions in the world. Jason Decairez Taylor, artist and founder of the museum, is a British sculptor and photographer who was born in 1974, is known for his unique underwater artwork. Every year, the museum is visited by an average of 200,000 visitors. Sculptures occupy an area of ​​420 m2 sterile substrate and reach a weight of more than 200 tons.

26.Museo Soumaya
Museo Soumaya - Museum of Art in Mexico City, Mexico. Founded in 1994, since 2011 it is located in a new six-story building. It was built with the funds of the billionaire Carlos Slim in his hometown of Mexico City to exhibit his personal collection of works of art, numbering over 66,000 exhibits. The museum exhibits works of Mexican and European artists in a permanent exhibition. The collection includes paintings by French Impressionists Camille Pissarro, Claude Monet, Edgar Degas and Pierre-Auguste Renoir. In addition, the museum is known for one of the world's most extensive collections of sculptures of Rodin (380 exhibits).

27.Puebla
Puebla de Zaragoza is the capital of the eponymous state in Mexico. Also the city is considered the capital of Mexican cuisine. The historical center of the city contains a large number of buildings of the colonial period and is on the World Heritage List. The main attractions include: the local cathedral, the Santo Domingo church (masterpiece of the Baroque), the Amparo Museum, the Santa Rosa Monastery, the Chapel of the Virgin of the Rosary, the Palace of the Government of Puebla, the San Cristobal Temple in the historic center of the city.

28.Xel-Ha Park
 By Kirt Edblom CC2
Xel-Ha Park is an ecological park located in the state of Quintana Roo, Mexico. Hotel Xel-Ha is located in the heart of the Mayan Riviera, 122 km south of Cancun and 13 km north of Tulum archaeological area. It is characterized by a bay in which the underground waters of the Yucatán Peninsula flow into the Caribbean Sea, forming a space for the coexistence of various marine and freshwater species. It is for this reason that Xel-Ha is known as a natural aquarium, in which 70 species of aquatic fauna, such as the blonde, parrot fish, surgeon, horse mackerel, major, chakka, perch, macabi, rays, chop and some others, live. Diving and snob are popular.

29.Merida
Merida is the capital and largest city of the Mexican state of Yucatán, as well as the administrative center of the municipality of Mérida. In Merida, a rectangular network of streets (at the base - the streets of the ancient Mayan city). Most of the buildings of the 16th-18th centuries have a strict serf-like appearance. The most famous architectural structures are the cathedral of San Idelfonso (1563-1599, the architects P.Avstia and F.Alarcon), the monastery of San Francisco (1561, the architect A.Tarancon), "the cashier where Montego" (1549-1551), the church of Las Mongas (1610-1633), La Megorada (1640), La Tercero (late 17th century), San Cristobal (1755-1799), San Juan de Dios (1770).

30.Cascada Basaseachic
Basaseachic Falls - a waterfall on the river of the same name. The second largest waterfall in Mexico. The height is 246 m. It is located in the Basaseachi National Park. The Basaseachi Waterfall is located in the Sierra Madre Mountains, in the municipality of Ocampo. The waterfall is formed from two streams of Arroyo del Dusnazi and Arroyo de Basaseachi. The surroundings of the waterfall are famous for their natural beauties, such as rock formations and pine forests. About 4 km is the even higher waterfall of Piedra Volada.
