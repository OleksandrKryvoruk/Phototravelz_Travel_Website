Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Seychelles.

AFRICA > South Africa
1.Victoria
Victoria - the capital of the Seychelles. One of the smallest capitals in the world. Among the main attractions include the Natural History Museum, Clocktower, a botanical garden, Lovenut nightclub.

2.Mahé
Mahe - the island of the Seychelles archipelago. The island is the capital of Victoria state. The highest point is 905 meters Morne is located in the eponymous national park. In the southern part of the island there are two marine parks and the port. Island forests are rare plants that nowhere in the world is no longer there, as there are many unique species of orchids.

3.Curieuse Island
By So Seychelles CC2
Curieuse Island - a small granite island, is part of the Northeast Group of the Inland Seychelles. The length of the island is 3.6 km, width - 1.6 km. The island is known as one of two places where the Seychelles symbol grows - the Seychelles palm tree. Also spread to the taman. In the southern part of the island there is a mangrove swamp. About 500 gigantic tortoises live in Curiuji, which were brought from the Aldabra Atoll. In coastal waters, another kind of turtle, a bisce, inhabits the shore, only to lay eggs. Of the birds inhabits a rare Seychelles variety of black parrots, which can be found only here and on the island of Praslin. In coastal waters there is a parrot fish.

4.Amirante Islands
Amirante Islands is an archipelago of 150 small islets in the western Indian Ocean near Africa, Seychelles. The total area is about 83 km2. Only six of them are visited occasionally by people who are attracted by valuable coconut palms.

5.Cousin Island
Cousin Island - a small granite island in the Indian Ocean, belongs to the Northeast group of the Inner Seychelles. Located 4 km south-west of Praslin Island and 2 km from the nearest island of South Kazin. The length of the island is 700 m, width - 500 m. The island is covered with tropical forests and is almost completely surrounded by sandy beaches, except for the rocky southern coast. The highest point of the island is a hill 69 m high in the southern part of the island. Also mangroves grow and there are several freshwater lakes. Coral reefs stretch for 400 m from the coast.

6.Sainte Anne Island
By Hansueli Krapf CC3
Sainte Anne Island is an island in the Indian Ocean from the Seychelles archipelago. Located 4 km east of the island of Mahe and 2 km north of the island of Cerf. Other neighboring islands are Rond, Muayen, Sesh. The length of the island is 2.1 km, width - 1.7 km. The highest point on St. Anne is 246 m high. St. Anne is the largest of the six islands included in the National Maritime Park of Saint Ann. The island is covered with rich tropical vegetation, has 4 beaches - Grand Anse, Royal, Turtle Beach and Anse Manon. Since 2002, on the south-west coast of the island operates a luxury resort Saint Anne Resort, for 87 villas.

7.La Digue
La Digue Island - the island of the Seychelles archipelago. One of the best islands of the archipelago. Close tourist resort known for its beaches. Also, the island is a nature reserve Veuve, home to rare species of animals. The highest point of the island Mount Eagle's Nest 300 m. The island has a lot of guest houses, hotels, restaurants, diving centers and tours with guides. On the island you can find: huge turtles, geckos, bats, rare birds flycatchers, butterflies, coconut crabs and many exotic species.

8.Anse Lazio
By Bjørn Christian Tørrissen CC4
Anse Lazio is a beach located in the northern part of the island of Praslin, Seychelles. According to "Lonely Planet" this is one of the best beaches in the archipelago. On the beach there are large granite boulders, as well as coral reefs. Near the beach there are rocks.

9.Sainte Anne Marine National Park
Sainte Anne Marine National Park is a marine national park, located about 5 km from the capital of the Seychelles, Victoria, and includes 6 small islands. Known and appreciated by tourists, the park prohibits fishing and water sports, popular diving and scuba diving.

10.Vallée de Mai
CC3
Vallée de Mai is a 19.5 hectare natural park located on the island of Praslin (Seychelles) in the center of Praslin National Park. Since 1983 it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The most part is occupied by well-preserved palm forests, mainly from the endemic Seychellois palm, the seeds of which are the largest among all plants, as well as five other endemic species of palm trees. The reserve fauna includes endemic birds, mammals, crustaceans, snails and reptiles.
