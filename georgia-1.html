Top 10 places for travel & vacation in Georgia.

ASIA > West Asia
1.Tbilisi
Tbilisi is the capital of Georgia. Worth visiting: Abanatubani, Tsminda Sameba, Narykala, Museum of Soviet occupation. There are many sulfur baths on the territory of Tbilisi. The temperature of the thermal water is 37-38 ° C. Baths are located near the embankment, in the ancient quarter of Abanotubani. Washrooms are equipped with underground, as water is pumped into the basins from the depths of the mountains. Here Alexander Pushkin once visited, who wrote in his Journey to Arzrum: "I have never seen anything more luxurious than the Tiflis bath in Russia or in Turkey."

2.Batumi
Batumi - a city in the south - west of Georgia. Located on the shores of the Black Sea. University, theater, circus, hospitals, State Museum of Ajara, Stalin Museum, Architecture Museum, Oceanarium aquarium and dolphinarium, a huge botanical garden and park, fort, a Turkish mosque - the main attractions of the city.

3.Borjomi
By Kober CC3
Borjomi is a city, the center of Borjomi municipality of Georgia, a balneological and climatic health resort. The climate is warm, moderately humid. Borjomi is famous for its medicinal mineral water, which is used as a drinking water for diseases of the gastrointestinal tract, biliary tract, metabolism, kidneys and in the form of baths - with cardiovascular diseases, diseases of the nervous system and respiratory organs. Archaeological excavations here found stone baths, which indicates that the healing properties of mineral water were known and used in ancient times. In Borjomi gorge there are more than 200 historical monuments: fortresses, churches, monasteries. A special place among the historical monuments is the palace of the Russian Romanov tsars, built in the late 18th century.

4.Ushguli
Ushguli - is a group of four villages in the valley along the upper reaches of Inguro and Georgia at the foot of the highest mountain in the area Schar Upper Svaneti in Georgia. Some buildings in these villages are part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site. Ushguli village located at an altitude of 2086-2200 meters above sea level - is the highest mountain village in Europe. In the villages there are several medieval castles, churches, restaurants, hotels, hostels.

5.Vardzia
By I, Wojciech Bijok CC3
Vardzia is a cave monastery complex of the 12th-13th centuries in the south of Georgia, in Javakheti. An outstanding monument of medieval Georgian architecture. It is located in the Aspindz region of Samtskhe-Javakheti province, in the valley of the river Kura (Mtkvari), about 70 km south of Borjomi, near the village of the same name. Within 900 meters along the left bank of the Kura, up to 600 rooms are carved in the steep wall of the tuff mountains of Yerusheti (Bear). Churches, chapels, living cells, storerooms, baths, refectory, financial institutions, libraries. The premises of the complex go 50 meters deep into the rock and rise to a height of eight floors. Secret passages that connected the rooms, the remnants of the water pipe and the irrigation system were preserved.

Prometheus Cave is a cave in Georgia, in the vicinity of the city of Tskhaltubo, a natural monument (category III according to the classification of the International Union for Conservation of Nature). The length of the cave is 1600 m.

7.Kolkheti National Park
Kolkheti National Park - located in Western Georgia, on the coastal plain of Kolkheti, between the mouths of the Tikuri and Supsa rivers. The park covers coastal areas in the east of the Black Sea and the basin of Lake Paleostomi. The coastal zone of the park is one of the main migration routes of African and Eurasian waterfowl and wading birds. In this region, you can find more than 194 different bird species, including 21 species of migratory birds. In the park there are a visit center and hotel, tourist walks along the paths and reservoirs, sport fishing and ecotourism services. The park is located on a large wetland, it is an ideal place for tourists who want to watch birds. The picturesque wetlands of the region are rarely found elsewhere in the Black Sea coastal zone. They are ideal for the development in the National Park of such types of tourism as boat trips, diving, hiking and horse riding.

8.Mount Kazbek
Mount Kazbek is a volcano and one of the main peaks in the Caucasus, Georgia. The height is 5047 m. Many ancient legends and sights are associated with Kazbek. Approximately at an altitude of 3800 m on an 80-meter rock wall in the cave is the ancient Georgian monastery of Betlemy (Bethlehem). According to the chronicle "Kartlis Tskhovreba", this monastery was a repository of shrines and church treasure, in which in ancient times the monks ascended the hung iron chain. According to information, St. Joseph, who ascended to the Betlem monastery by the will of God, presented the king of Iraq with a piece from the tent of Abraham.

9.Tetnuldi
By Gogi Bedenashvili CC2
Tetnuldi is the peak in the spur of the Bezengi Wall, the Main Caucasian Range in the Upper Svaneti region, Georgia, 2 km south of the summit of Gestola and the Russian Federation border (Kabardino-Balkaria). The height is 4852 m. The summit is two-headed, composed of ancient crystalline rocks. Tetnuld glaciers are drained by Oish, Nageb, (Inguri sources), Adish, and others. The total area of glaciers is 46 km2. 22 km to the west of the summit is the regional center of Mestia. For today, the ascent to Tetnuld is a sporting climb of an average difficulty category.

10.Kintrishi River
Kintrishi River - a river in the Autonomous Republic of Adjara in the south - west of Georgia. It flows at an altitude of 2200 m. To the north of the river is a nature reserve Kintrishi, south Mtirala National Park. A popular place for rafting.
