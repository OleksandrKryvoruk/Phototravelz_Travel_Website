31.Mont-Tremblant
Mont-Tremblant is a city in Quebec, Canada. Located 130 km (81 miles) northwest of Montreal and 140 kilometers (87 miles) northeast of Ottawa. It is located at the foot of the Mont-Tremblant mountain. The city is the most famous ski resort in the country. Also here is the self-titled autodrome. There are many golf courses, there is a developed ski infrastructure. A ski resort town located at the foot of the mountain and several ski lifts were built from scratch in the nineties on the European model.

32.Hudson Bay
Hudson Bay - part of the Arctic Ocean, Canada. Hudson Bay is about 960 km in width and 1,390 km from north to south. Depth of 260 m. Most of the year is covered with ice. In the bay there is an industrial herring, cod and flounder. The Gulf was formed 6,000 years ago by melting a giant glacier. Hudson Bay has many islands, mainly near the east coast. Near the city of Churchill, polar bears spend the summer season in a dormant regime.

33.Kluane National Park
Kluane National Park and Reserve - Park in southwestern Yukon (Canada) and is part of the World Heritage. 10,000 years of Native American tribes lived here. In the early 20th century there was a small gold rush, and the park itself was formed in 1972. The park has the highest mountain in Canada Logan (5956 m), and most of the area is covered by glaciers. In the park grow: aspen, white spruce, balsam poplar, dwarf birch. Fauna is represented by a grizzly, caribou, coyotes, wolverines, wolves, sheep, mink, and more than 150 species of birds.
Where to stay Weather
 

34.Naramata
Naramata is a small settlement in British Columbia, Canada. Naramata is located in the Okanagan Valley on the south-eastern shore of Lake Okanagan. The village has agricultural specialization. There are many gardens of plums, pears, apricots, cherries, apples, peaches. Wine making is also very developed. Naramata is also the place of filming for the film "My American Cousin". Every week there is a farmers' fair. In general, the village is suitable for those who seek peace and tranquility, as well as beautiful nature.

35.Mount Robson
Mount Robson is the highest point of the Canadian Rockies (3959 m), Canada. Located on the territory of the same regional park. Although the mountain below 4,000 m of easily accessible routes to the top does not exist, and bad weather conditions overwhelm most attempts at recovery. Only 1 in 10 people conquers the summit due to bad weather conditions. There are about 10 routes to conquer the summit. The southern side of Mount Robson is clearly visible from Yellowhead Highway (Highway 16). The height of the southern slope reaches 3000 meters from the foot to the top.
Where to stay Weather
 

36.Okanagan Lake
Lake Okanagan - a lake in British Columbia, Canada. The length of the lake is almost 135 km and a width of 6.5 km. In the south-western shore of Lake Okanagan is Park Mountain. According to legend of local people in the lake lives a huge monster. Around the lake there are many beaches and parks, and its length makes it ideal for sailing and racing boats. The lake is home to several species of fish and a lot of trout.

37.Parlee Beach Provincial Park
Parlee Beach Provincial Park is a regional park in New Brunswick, Canada. One of the most popular in the region. The beach is well equipped, there is a 190-seat camping, Picnic places, a restaurant, a dining room, cabins, playgrounds and parking for more than 1000 cars. The park is open from 8am to 10pm in the summer months. Parlee Beach is patrolled by rescuers. Over the summer, about 500,000 people visit the park. The nearby town of Shediac has a summer service center for tourists.

38.Tofino
Tofino is an area on the west coast of Vancouver Island, Canada. It is a popular tourist destination in the summer, the population is increasing several times over in winter. The place will be interesting for surfers, ornithologists, nature lovers and fishermen. Nearby there is the park Maquinna Marine Provincial Park with hot springs, also a popular destination for day trips for tourists - Pacific Rim National Park Reserve. Every year in March, thousands of gray whales can be observed here.
Where to stay Weather Webcams
 

39.Belcarra
Belcarra is a village in the North Vancouver area on the eastern shore of the fjord of the Indian Armagh, Canada. The village is known for its 1100 hectare regional park. Woodland, mountains and rocky beaches make the park an excellent place for outdoor activities every day. On the territory of the park there is a warm lake Sasamat. A large number of animals and plants live on the rocky shores. Also the village offers horseback riding, hiking and biking trails and fishing (with a special license).

40.Bosk Lake
Bosk Lake - one of the best places for fishing in British Columbia, Canada. Fishermen can find here various kinds of trout: rainbow, bull, lake, thug, as well as perch, bullhead, and many others. In order to fish on the lake you need to purchase a license in British Columbia Fish & Wildlife. Also, the lake is suitable for camping.
