41.San Pedro Cholula
San Pedro Cholula is a region in the state of Puebla, Mexico. Chochula is an Indian city, known as Tlaciualtepetl. It contains the oldest building of the pyramid, dating from about 3,500 BC. The city also has the market square of Plaza de la Concordia, the Museum of Cholula, the Great Pyramid of Cholula, the Parish Church of San Pedro. To the center of the municipality is Zapoteac, a volcanic hill known among the athletes of the region and which contains the only wooded area of ​​the municipality. "Zapo" allows for several recreational activities, such as picnics, jogging, hiking, mountain biking and motocross.

42.Cuernavaca
Cuernavaca is the capital of the state of Morelos, Mexico. In the vicinity of the city there are many beautiful and culturally rich regions of the country. Because of the climate, the city received the nickname "City of Eternal Spring". The city has a huge number of attractions: the Palace of Cortez, the gardens of Morelos and Juarez, the Cuernavaca Cathedral, the Borda garden, the Teopanzolco archaeological site, the Robert Brady Museum, the San Jose church and the Nuestra Señora de Milas church, the phytotherapy museum, Chapultepec Ecological Park, the castle El Castillito.

43.Taxco
Taxco is a city in Mexico, part of the state of Guerrero, located about 200 km south-west of the city of Mexico. Taxco is called a silver city, because it was an important center of silver mining. It is famous for its dense development, narrow cobbled streets, picturesque houses of distinctive colonial architecture. The main market is dominated by the Santa Prisca church of pink stone, built in the Baroque style. Also worth a visit: Borda House, Humboldt House, Church of San Bernardino (Saint Bernard).

44.Agua Azul Waterfalls
Agua Azul is a multi-cascade system of waterfalls (about 500 separate small waterfalls) in the state of Chiapas, Mexico. Located on the road to San Cristobal de los Casas. Cascades located one after another, the largest one has a height of 6 meters. Water flowing by waterfalls has a bright blue color due to the high mineral content. They also settle on rocks and trunks of trees. The same river in its lower reaches also forms the Agua Clara waterfalls and the Misol-Ha waterfall.

45.Chapultepec
Chapultepec - the name of the hill in the center of Mexico City (Mexico), around which the same park with an area of ​​about 800 hectares is broken. This is the largest of the city parks in America. In the days of the Aztecs - the location of the country residence of the emperor, which was connected with Tenochtitlan in bulk and an aqueduct. Among the attractions of Chapultepec - the eponymous zoo, the rock with the relief image of Montezuma I, the palace of Emperor Maximilian and the National Museum of Anthropology - the world's largest collection of the heritage of Mesoamerican civilization. In the park next to the museum you can watch the dance-ritual volador.

46.Iztaccihuatl
Iztaccihuatl is a volcano with four peaks and the third highest peak in Mexico (after the volcanoes of Orizaba, 5 636 m, and Popocatepetl, 5 426 m). In everyday life, the mountain is called simply Ista. From the west and east, the silhouettes of these peaks resemble the head, chest, knees and feet of a sleeping female figure. Iztaccihuatl is 70 km southeast of Mexico City, and its snow-capped summit can be seen from the city. All the peaks are covered with snow and glaciers. Near the volcano used to live the Aztecs.

47.Popocatépetl
By Russ Bowling CC2
Popocatépetl is an active volcano and the second highest peak of Mexico (5452 m) after Pico de Orizaba (5636 m). Popocatepetl is associated with the volcano Istaksiatl in the north by a high pass, known as Paso de Cortez, and lies in the eastern part of the Trans-Mexican volcanic belt. Popocatepetl is one of the most active volcanoes in the world. It is located just 64 kilometers southeast of the Mexican capital of Mexico City. A large volcanic eruption occurred on March 28, 2016, as a result, a column of ash and smoke 2000 meters high rose to the sky.

48.San Juan de Ulúa
San Juan de Ulua is a small island with fortresses, a prison and a 16th century palace in Mexico. Fort San Juan de Ulua was founded in 1535. Since then, the fortress has been modified several times. Until 1825 it was owned by the Spaniards. Then the fort was several times in the hands of foreigners: in the first half of the XIX century it was conquered by the French, and a little later by the Americans. Now the complex acts as a museum. To the fortress, standing on the ocean, you can get both by car and by boat. The way on the water surface is considered optimal, as it saves time.

49.Misol-Ha Waterfalls
Misol-Ha is a waterfall consisting of a single cascade of 35 m high, in Mexico, located 15 km south of Palenque. The waterfall falls into a pool surrounded by tropical vegetation. The water that falls is pure blue because of the mineral content. Behind the cascade is a cave 20 meters deep. It is one of the tourist attractions of the southern Chiapas. The waterfall was the scene where the scenes were filmed for the movie "Predator of 1987" directed by John McTiernan.
Where to stay Weather Things to do in Misol-Ha: Day Trips & Excursions
 

50.Lake Chapala
Chapala is the largest freshwater lake in Mexico. It is located on a high plateau about 50 km south of the city of Guadalajara on the border between the states of Jalisco (about 86%) and Michoacan (about 14%). The average depth is 5 m, and the maximum is 10.5 m. Pink pelicans and more than 1000 species of plants and animals live on the lake. Despite environmental problems, the fish still lives on Lake Chapala. Tourism is of particular importance to the inhabitants of the city of Guadalajara.
