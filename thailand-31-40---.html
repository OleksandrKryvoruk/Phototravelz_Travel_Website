31.Ko Lanta Yai
By Jens-Petter Salvesen CC2
Ko Lanta Yai is an island in the Andaman Sea, located on the west coast of Thailand in the southern part of Krabi province. The area of the island is 81 km2, the length is 25 km and the width is 6 km. In the north, Lanta Yai is adjacent to the narrow canal of Lanta Noi. The island is relatively developed, most of the available premises are mainly bamboo huts. Ko Ko Lanta can make a worthy competition to the southern beaches of Thailand. There are a lot of beaches with shell sand here, most of them still remain wild. Divers have long been chosen this island of Lanta.

32.Ko Pha Ngan
By James Evans CC2
Ko Pha Ngan is a Thai coastal island in the southeast of the Gulf of Thailand. The Sadet-Ko Pha-Ngan National Park is located on the north-east of the island, there are also many beaches and palm trees. First of all, the island is widely known for its monthly "Full Moon Party" party on Haad Rin beach. It is a popular destination for backpackers in Thailand. There are several diving and kite schools on the island. The main attractions of the island are of course the temples: Chinese Temple, Wat Paa Sang Tham, Wat Samai Kongka, Wat Maduea Wan, Wat Pho & Herbal Sauna, Wat Phu Khao Noi, Wat Ampawa, Wat Chaloklum.

33.Angthong National Marine Park
Angthong National Marine Park is a marine national park, Thailand. Includes 42 small limestone islands. Very popular is swimming among the islands surrounding coral reefs. The islands are amazing for their variety of birds and plants. Worth visiting: Bois-Boke cave, Inner lake, Natural bridge, Koh Taiplao and northern islets. You can explore the islands with a kayak or a boat. The administration of the National Park rents several simple bungalows on the island of Ko-Uatalab and maintains a restaurant on the same island. The menu is mostly Thai food.

34.KhaoYai National Park
KhaoYai National Park - the oldest national park in Thailand. It is located about 200 kilometers from Bangkok. For tourists it is the most accessible park, situated at an altitude of 800 meters above sea level, there are hiking trails that lead you to a number of interested local magnificent waterfalls. Included in the list of UNESCO. In the park there are various types of trees with a predominance of sandalwood. In the park they live in different kinds of animals: tigers, Asian elephants, venomous snakes, pheasants, Nepal and bats. The park is open to visitors year round. You can also rent cars and bikes.
Where to stay Weather
 

35.Doi Inthanon National Park
Doi Inthanon National Park is a national park in the north of Thailand and includes the highest mountain in Thailand - Doi Inthanonas (2,576 m). Are dominated by: evergreen, mountain, mixed and deciduous forests. It ranks second among the national parks of Thailand in the number of species of birds. The park is worth a visit: the waterfall Mae Ya, which is considered one of the most beautiful in Thailand, the Syrifum waterfall, Vachiratan Falls and Boritchinda Cave - an interesting limestone cave among a mixed forest with a huge camera at the entrance.

36.Erawan National Park
Erawan National Park is a national park located in western Thailand, in the province of Kanchanaburi. This is one of the most visited national parks of this country. To the west of the park is the Sai Yok National Park. In the north there is Lake Sinakharin. The park is open for tourists throughout the year. The main attraction is the waterfall Erawan Falls, which was named after Eravan, a three-headed elephant from Hindu mythology. There are also four caves that are worth seeing: Tam Mi, Tam Ryu, Tam Van Badan and Tam Franat.

37.Sukhothai
Sukhothai is a medieval Thai state that was located in the territory of modern Thailand. The former capital of the kingdom is in ruins and has been designated as a UNESCO heritage, located in the Muang Kao tambon, 12 km from the modern city of Sukhothai. The city is also one of the places where Loy Krathong, one of the most important Thai festivals, which is held every year, is celebrated in the most magnificent way.

38.Wat Mahathat
Wat Maha That - a famous Buddhist temple in Ayuthya, Thailand. The temple is known sculpture of Buddha head, which is entangled roots of the tree. Construction of the temple was started by the architect Mahathera Thammakanlayan during the time of King Boromarachi I v1374 year. Buddha head in the tree roots are now in fact a symbol of Ayutthaya.

39.Wat Phra Singh
Wat Phra Singh is a very large temple built by King Fao Yuo in 1345 as a place for the remains of his father, King Kam Fu. The temple is located in the inner city of Chiang Mai, the northern province of Thailand. In the temple there is a unique bible. The temple's storehouses are a place for storing, protecting the Bible written on palm leaves, or fragile silk sheets used by monks for recording and copying folklore. Here you can also order short courses on Buddhism. In particular, daily visitors will take part in exercises, meditation and a hostel as monks.

40.Safari World
Safari World - a private park for animals and recreation in the area of Khlong Sam Wa in the northeastern outskirts of Bangkok. The park claims to own the largest open zoo in Thailand in which various animal species and hundreds of samples can move freely in a natural landscape adapted to the natural conditions of their regions of origin. The park has more than 75 species of mammals, 300 species of birds from Africa and Asia It is open every day from 9 to 15 hours.
